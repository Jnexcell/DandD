Welcome, hero-to-be 🌿

New to D&D? You don't need to know a single rule. Just make some choices you like.

Travelers keep vanishing on the old Briar Road through the Mournwood. The village of Greywater has scraped together a bounty and hired you to find what's taking them — and put a stop to it. The woods feel wrong. Something is out there. You and your companions are the ones brave (or foolish) enough to go in.

How this works

  1. Click your way through five quick choices: Race, Class, Background, Ability Scores, and a little Personality.
  2. Everything is explained in plain language as you go. Stuck? Pick whatever sounds fun — there are no wrong answers.
  3. At the end, hit Finish and send the result to your DM. They'll do all the math and hand you a ready-to-play character sheet.

You'll start the adventure as a level 3 hero — but here you just pick the bones of who you are. Your DM levels you up the rest of the way. Your progress saves automatically on this device.

A few words you'll see

AC — Armor Class: how hard you are to hit.
HP — Hit Points: your health.
d20 — a 20-sided die; most rolls use it.
Modifier — the +/− you add to a roll.
Race — what you are.
Class — what you do.

Step 1 · Choose a Race

What you are. It gives small ability boosts, a speed, and a few always-on traits.

Step 2 · Choose a Class

What you actually do. "Hit Die" hints at toughness (d12 = very tough … d6 = fragile).

Step 3 · Choose a Background

Your past life. It grants two skills, some gear, and a handy non-combat perk.

Step 4 · Ability Scores

Your raw talent. We add your race's bonuses for you, live.
