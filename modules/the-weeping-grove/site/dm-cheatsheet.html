Ability & skill checks — spot them & run them

When to call one: only when the outcome is uncertain and failure is interesting — otherwise just say yes. Then pick the ability, set a DC, the player rolls d20 + ability mod (+ proficiency if trained); meet or beat = success.  DCs: Easy 10 Medium 15 Hard 20 (very easy 5 · very hard 25).  ·  Adv/Disadv: 2d20 take higher/lower — don't stack, one of each cancels.  ·  Passive (no roll, for noticing/secret things): 10 + mods.  ·  Group check: half the party succeeds → all succeed.  ·  Contest (grapple, stealth-vs-perception): opposed rolls, higher wins.

Golden rule for this mystery — fail forward: never lock a clue behind a bad roll. On a miss, hand it over on the next look, from an NPC, or later.

The core loop

You describe → a player says what they do → if the outcome's in doubt they roll d20 + a number off their sheet vs a target you hold → you narrate the result. That's the whole game.

Lean toward yes. Clever ideas usually just work; when unclear, pick an ability + DC and move on. A toolbox, not a menu.

Who rolls what

  • Attacks (either side): d20 + bonus vs. AC. Meet-or-beat = hit → roll damage dice.
  • You never roll the PCs' saves. When a monster forces one, just announce ability + DC ("Dex save, DC 11") — the player rolls.
  • PC casts a save-spell? The target rolls vs. the caster's Spell DC — the player tells you the DC.
  • Nat 20 = crit (roll damage dice twice); Nat 1 = miss. (On checks: best/worst version of the try.)

A turn: Move + 1 Action (+ maybe 1 Bonus, + 1 Reaction)

Player says…Your instant ruling
"I attack"d20+bonus vs AC → damage on hit
"Grapple / Shove"Their Athletics vs target's Athletics or Acrobatics. Win = speed 0, or shove 5ft / prone
"I Help"An ally gets advantage on next attack/check
"I Dodge"Attacks vs them have disadv; adv on Dex saves
"I Disengage"No opportunity attacks this turn · "I Dash" = double move
"I Hide"Stealth vs passive Perception; hidden = first attack adv, then revealed
"I Ready…"Name a trigger now; fires later as a Reaction

Reactions (1 / round, each side)

  • Opportunity attack: a creature leaves your reach without Disengaging → spend your reaction for one melee attack.
  • Concentration: a caster holding a conc. spell who takes damage → Con save, DC 10 or ½ the damage, whichever is higher. Fail = spell drops. Call it every hit — it's how the party breaks the Overseer's hold person.

At 0 HP — Death Saves

  • On your turn, roll d20: 10+ = success, under = fail.
  • 3 successes = stable · 3 fails = dead. Nat 20 = up at 1 HP. Nat 1 = two fails.
  • Damage while down = an auto-fail (a crit = two). Any healing wakes them at that HP.
  • Massive hit (damage ≥ max HP over 0) = instant death.

Cover & the conditions you'll use

Cover: half +2 AC/Dex · three-quarters +5 · total = can't be targeted.

Prone: melee adv / ranged disadv vs it. Grappled/Restrained: speed 0; restrained = attacks vs it adv, its attacks disadv, Dex-save disadv. Paralyzed (hold person): can't act, auto-fails Str/Dex saves, melee within 5ft auto-crits. Frightened: disadv while source in sight, can't move closer. Invisible (Sere): attacks vs it disadv, its attacks adv.

Rests & Inspiration

  • Tonight the party gets one short + one long rest. Short = breather, spend Hit Dice to heal, some abilities/Warlock slots recharge. Long = full HP + all slots. Tell them when it's safe.
  • Inspiration: reward brave / funny / truly in-character play (esp. when it costs them) and curiosity. Hold one; spend to reroll one die, take the better. (Play the Trait/Ideal/Bond/Flaw on p.2.)

The Weeping Grove — Run It

The Key = the whole twist

The "monster" is the grove's wronged Guardian Sorrowroot; the villains are the Withering death-cult bleeding it for heart-sap to make Mother Sere immortal — and they seeded the beast rumor.

  • Get the Key from Clue 3 (heart-sap crate + brand, Scene 3) or Clue 4 (Fenn, Scene 2).
  • Key found → flips to Ending B / Showdown Version B (fight the cult, Guardian is your ally).
  • 3-clue rule / fail forward: never withhold a clue on a bad roll — hand it over next look. Clue 5 (Guardian's plea) is the fail-safe: even a kill-first party gets one last flip.
  • No clock. The Harvest is already underway; the showdown IS the interruption. Let them linger.

Showdown A — fight the Guardian no Key · Deadly · TPK risk

Say it aloud before a caster commits: lightning HEALS it · resists cold & fire · AC 15 / 136 HP (a grind by design).
  • Grief, not malice: it grapples + Engulfs to stop "the axes" — never chases fleers or finishes the downed. Engulfed PC at 0 still rolls death saves. Escape DC 14; free by dropping it low.
  • Flip is open every round: lower weapons or Persuasion DC 20 → Version B, it becomes an ally.
  • Valves: too fast? +20–25 HP / grasping-roots grapple a 2nd PC. On the ropes? Cut thorn-spawn, or Engulf-and-hold (no damage).

Showdown B — turn it on the cult has Key · Deadly · heroic

The objective isn't a clock — it's the vial. Mother Sere's Withered Vitality lets her auto-succeed one failed save / shrug one condition each round while the green fire burns. Smash the vial/fire (object, AC 12) → she's just a Green Hag.
  • Sere opens hidden (invisible / posing as a frightened captive): Insight DC 14 or the Guardian's blindsight outs her. Invisibility ends when she attacks/casts.
  • Guardian is your ally (you run it, focuses Sere) — pull it back so it doesn't steal the kill. Grave-husks exist to soak its actions.
  • Morale: cultists break when Sere falls; husks fight to nothing but collapse when she dies. If the Overseer died in S3, delete his token.

Remember at the table

Keep S1 to ~2 rounds — the wood "lets them go," don't grind all eight wolves. · Reward curiosity with Inspiration; "wait — why?" is the road to Ending B. · Fail forward, never gate a clue on a die. · Safety: X-card / tap the table — this one gets dark (grief, a death-cult). · Wren (Tomas's sister) = only bones in Ending A; comes home alive in Ending B. · Close on the matching hook.

Wolf — Bramble-Wolf CR 1/4 · AC 13 · HP 11 · Scene 1

STR 12 (+1) · DEX 15 (+2) · CON 12 (+1) · INT 3 (−4) · WIS 12 (+1) · CHA 6 (−2)

Attacks: Bite +4 (2d4+2 pierce); the target makes Str 11 or is knocked prone. Pack Tactics · Keen Hearing & Smell (adv Perception).

  • Pack Tactics is the plan: bite whatever an ally is already next to → advantage; hunt in twos and threes, never solo.
  • Prone-lock: knock a squishy down (Str 11), then every adjacent wolf gets melee adv on them.
  • Grief, not hunger — the pack drives the party off, it doesn't hunt to kill. Keep S1 to ~2 rounds; An.Handling / Nature DC 15 scatters them without a death.

Twig Blight — Thorn-Spawn CR 1/8 · AC 13 · HP 4 · Showdown A

STR 6 (−2) · DEX 13 (+1) · CON 12 (+1) · INT 4 (−3) · WIS 8 (−1) · CHA 3 (−4)

Attacks: Claws +3 (1d4+1 pierce). Blindsight 60 ft (can't be blinded/deafened) · False Appearance (motionless = a dead shrub).

Vulnerable to fire — Alary's Produce Flame or any fire double-damages them (4 HP = a one-shot). Let the party feel clever.
  • Fragile swarm: 4 HP each — they exist to chip damage and to soak PC attacks & opportunity attacks so Sorrowroot keeps grappling.
  • Ambush out of the "scenery" via False Appearance, then close and rake.

Cultist — Grey-robe CR 1/8 · AC 12 · HP 9 · S3 & Show B

STR 11 (+0) · DEX 12 (+1) · CON 10 (+0) · INT 10 (+0) · WIS 11 (+0) · CHA 10 (+0)

Attacks: Scimitar +3 (1d6+1 slash). Dark Devotion — advantage on saves vs charmed & frightened.

  • Fodder & screen: put bodies between the party and the real threat (the Overseer / Mother Sere).
  • Fear & charm tricks fizzle (Dark Devotion) — they win on numbers, not nerve.
  • Morale: they break and flee when the boss drops.

Thug — Poacher-Muscle CR 1/2 · AC 11 · HP 32 · Scene 3

STR 15 (+2) · DEX 11 (+0) · CON 14 (+2) · INT 10 (+0) · WIS 10 (+0) · CHA 11 (+0)

Grave-Husk — Risen Taken CR 1/8 · AC 13 · HP 4 · Showdown B

  • 4 HP fodder — they soak the Guardian-ally's actions in Show B and chip the party while Sere works.
  • Collapse the instant Mother Sere dies — her rite is what holds them up.

Cult Fanatic — Harvest Overseer CR 2 · AC 13 · HP 33 · S3 / Show B

STR 11 (+0) · DEX 14 (+2) · CON 12 (+1) · INT 10 (+0) · WIS 13 (+1) · CHA 14 (+2)

Attacks: Multiattack — 2× Dagger +4 (1d4+2). Dark Devotion (adv vs charm/fear).

Spells (Wis, save DC 11, +3 to hit): cantrips — light, sacred flame (Dex 11, 1d8 radiant), thaumaturgy · 1st (4 slots) — command, inflict wounds (+3, 3d10 necro), shield of faith · 2nd (3) — hold person, spiritual weapon.

hold person (Wis 11) paralyzes a PC → melee within 5 ft auto-crits; inflict wounds (3d10) is a burst-down. Call concentration saves to break its hold / spiritual weapon.
  • Opening play: shield of faith (+2 AC) or spiritual weapon (bonus-action 1d8+1 that floats & attacks), then hold person the tank, then inflict wounds whoever's in melee.
  • Stay back and cast; the dagger is a last resort.

Green Hag — Mother Sere CR 3 · AC 17 · HP 82 · Showdown B boss

STR 18 (+4) · DEX 12 (+1) · CON 16 (+3) · INT 13 (+1) · WIS 14 (+2) · CHA 14 (+2)

Attacks: Claws +6 (2d8+4 = 13 avg). Invisible Passage (turns invisible until she attacks or casts) · Illusory Appearance.

Innate spells (Cha, save DC 12): at will — dancing lights, minor illusion, vicious mockery (Wis 12, 3d6 psychic + disadv on its next attack). Mimicry of voices (Insight DC 14 to catch).

Withered Vitality: while the green fire burns she auto-succeeds one failed save OR ends one condition every round. Smash the vial / green fire (object, AC 12) and she's just a hag — that's the win, not her 82 HP.
  • Opens hidden — invisible, or posing as a frightened captive (Insight 14, or the Guardian's blindsight outs her). Invisibility drops when she attacks/casts.
  • Hit-and-hide: Claw the healer (Alary) or squishy (Douglas), then Invisible Passage away; use illusions / mimicry to split their focus.

Shambling Mound — Sorrowroot CR 5 · AC 15 · HP 136 · Show A boss / B ally

STR 18 (+4) · DEX 8 (−1) · CON 16 (+3) · INT 5 (−3) · WIS 10 (+0) · CHA 5 (−3)

Attacks: Multiattack — 2× Slam +7 (2d8+4); if both hit a Medium-or-smaller target it's grappled (DC 14) + Engulfed (blinded, restrained, can't breathe; Con 14 or 2d8+4 at the start of its turns; escape DC 14).

Lightning HEALS it — say so before a caster commits. Resists cold & fire. AC 15 / 136 HP = a grind by design.
  • Grief, not malice: it Engulfs to stop "the axes," never chases fleers or finishes the downed (an Engulfed PC at 0 still rolls death saves).
  • Show A boss — flip it any round by lowering weapons or Persuasion DC 20; in Show B it's your ally (you run it vs Sere — pull it back so it doesn't steal the kill).

Dire Wolf — Elder Bramble-Wolf CR 1 · AC 14 · HP 37

  • Pack Tactics is the whole plan: only ever bite a target an ally is already next to → advantage. Never let it duel alone.
  • Prone-lock: Bite forces Str 13 or prone; a prone PC = melee adv for every wolf on them. Knock down the low-AC back line (Douglas 12, Jarlaxle 15), then swarm.
  • A pack animal, not a martyr — if isolated or bloodied it falls back to reform. Slips in cleanly beside the S1 bramble-wolves.

Needle Blight — Thornspitter CR 1/4 · AC 12 · HP 11

  • Artillery, not a duelist: hang back 30–60 ft and fire Needles (+3 2d6+1) at the squishiest PC each turn (Douglas / Alary). Only Claw if cornered.
  • Blindsight 60 ft — it still "sees" invisible or hidden foes within 60 ft; darkness won't hide the party.
  • Not fire-vulnerable (only Twig Blights are) — don't over-reward Produce Flame here. 11 HP drops to one hit, so use them in pairs.

Vine Blight — Snare-Root CR 1/2 · AC 12 · HP 26

  • Ambush controller: False Appearance = it lurks as a vine tangle until the party's close, then Constrict (reach 10) grapples + restrains (escape Str 12).
  • Pin a key PC: lock down the archer/caster (Jarlaxle, Douglas) so your other reserves pile on. Entangling Plants (recharge 5–6) restrains a cluster (Str 12) + difficult terrain.
  • Speed 10 — it can't chase. Play it as an anchor, not a skirmisher.

Shadow — Withering Shade CR 1/2 · AC 12 · HP 16

Nonmagical weapons do HALF (Tuf's greataxe, mundane arrows). Radiant does DOUBLE — I'Drenk's Divine Smite & radiant spells wreck it. Immune necrotic & poison.
  • Withering flavor incarnate: Strength Drain (2d6+2 necrotic) also drops the target's STR by 1d4 until a rest — sap the bruisers (Tuf, I'Drenk) to gut their damage and Athletics.
  • Lurker: Shadow Stealth = Hide as a bonus action in dim light; Amorphous squeezes through any gap. Run it at night/in shadow — in sunlight it has disadvantage on everything.

Ghoul — Risen Taken CR 1 · AC 12 · HP 22

Paralysis chains into auto-crits. Claws force Con 10 or paralyzed 1 min (save each turn's end); a paralyzed PC auto-fails Str/Dex saves and any melee hit within 5 ft auto-crits.
  • The combo: one ghoul paralyzes a low-Con PC, the next (or the Overseer) steps up and auto-crits. Terrifying in pairs — the intended Showdown-B escalation.
  • Jarlaxle (Drow = elf) is immune to the paralysis; half-elf Alary is your call (RAW: not immune). DC 10 is low, so don't overuse it or it's a save-or-suck death spiral.

Berserker — Poacher-Brute CR 2 · AC 13 · HP 67

The durable threat — 67 HP and a Greataxe (+5 1d12+3, avg 9), no saves to track. Pure damage + action sponge; the easiest reserve to run when the table's tired.
  • Reckless is a knob: at the start of its turn it can take advantage on all its swings, but attacks against it have advantage too — turn it on when the party's cruising, off when they're hurting.
  • Charge the juicy target: straight at Douglas (AC 12) to force a peel-off, or at Tuf for a slugfest he can survive. Best paired with the Overseer as muscle.
