# The Weeping Grove

> **The pitch (one line):** Travelers keep vanishing on the old forest road; the village hires you to kill the beast responsible — but the woods are *mourning*, not hunting, and the real predator walks on two legs.

## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Quest (told to players up front)** | Find what's taking travelers on the Briar Road through the Mournwood, and stop it — make the road safe again. |
| **Party** | 4 PCs · level 3 |
| **Target length** | ~3–4 hours (4 scenes + showdown) |
| **Tone** | Mystery + heroic adventure (a tragic twist underneath) |
| **Setting** | The Mournwood — wilderness forest road (the "Briar Road") |
| **Content notes / safety** | A grieving, wronged guardian; a death-cult harvesting a sacred grove; an undeath/immortality rite. No on-screen cruelty to children/animals. Run an **X-card / lines & veils** check at session zero (`../../quick-reference/new-dm-one-shot-tips.md`). |
| **Party XP budget** | Easy 300 / Medium 600 / Hard 900 / **Deadly 1,600** · Adventuring-day ≈ 4,800 |

## How to run this

- **Pacing / clock:** target ~3–4 hrs. The cult finishes the **Great Harvest at dusk on the third day** — an invisible clock. The more the party dawdles, the closer the ritual. If they're fast, the Showdown is a fight to *stop* it; if slow, it's a fight *during* its final moments.
- **Tags:** **★ CORE** scenes always run (Cold Open + Showdown); **⬡ MODULAR** scenes are skippable — each has a **Bypass**.
- **Cut-list (drop in this order if short on time):** Scene 4 (Warded Grove → bypass with one Nature check) → Scene 1 (Bramble-Pack) → Scene 3 (Harvesters' Camp). **Never cut the Showdown.** *Note: cutting **Scene 2 (Fenn)** removes the clearest route to the Key — only do it if the party is racing straight up the road (that's Ending A's path).*
- **The Two Paths, plainly:** the road (fast, fewer truths, a tragic boss) vs. the drag-marks off-trail (slower, the Key, a heroic finish). **Choosing the detour over the direct route is the meaningful choice.**
- **Seed the truth (three-clue rule):** clues 1–2 are on the direct path, clue 3–4 on the side path, and the **guardian itself pleads at the Showdown** (clue 5) so even a kill-first party gets one last chance to flip. See the **Clue tracker** in Appendix B.
- **Stat blocks:** all seven are inlined once in **Appendix A** (referenced by each scene). Reflavor notes are with each. Pull anything else from `../../monster-manual/stat-blocks/`.
- **Pacing & safety tools:** `../../quick-reference/new-dm-one-shot-tips.md` (rulings-not-rules, lines & veils, X-card).

## Cold open  [★ CORE] · framing · ~10 min (or ~20 with the optional village opening)

**What the players already know (Step 0):** They've taken the village of **Greywater's** coin to find what's taking travelers on the **Briar Road** and put a stop to it.

### ☑ Before you start — Rules of the Table  (~3 min, before any read-aloud)
*(Grounded in DMG p.235–237; the safety tools are community practice — `../../quick-reference/new-dm-one-shot-tips.md`.)* **Say it to the table in your own words — something like this:**

> *"Quick table stuff before we start:*
> 1. ***One night, one story.*** *This is a self-contained adventure with a single goal — find what's taking people on the road, and stop it. We'll reach a real ending tonight.*
> 2. ***I'll keep things moving.*** *If you describe something clever, it'll usually just **work** — I won't make you quote rules at me. When something's unclear I'll make a quick call so we can play on; ask me about it afterward if you want.*
> 3. ***One voice at a time, and everyone gets a moment.*** *Watch out for each other's spotlight. I'll be rolling [**in the open** / **behind the screen** — pick one] tonight.*
> 4. ***Safety.*** *If anything gets uncomfortable, just say **'X'** or tap the table and we'll move past it — no explanation needed. Fair warning, this one gets dark: there's grief, a death-cult, and an old guardian of the wood pushed past breaking. Anything you'd rather we keep off the table?*
> 5. ***Tone:*** *mystery and heroics with a sad streak — haunted forest, not a dungeon crawl.*
> *Sound good? Then tell me who your character is in a sentence, and we'll begin."*

### 🏘️ Opening in Greywater — quest-givers & questions  [⬡ MODULAR · recommended for new players] · ~10 min
*Lets players meet the quest-givers, ask questions, and pick up the first threads. Short on time or want the straight-into-the-action cold open? **Skip this** — the quest was given above the table — and open on the road below.*

> **Read aloud (the village):** Greywater is a muddy huddle of houses where the Briar Road meets the river — the kind of place that lives or dies by the road. Today the market's half-empty and too quiet. The reeve waits outside the meeting-hall, arms crossed; beside her a barrel-chested man twists his cap in his hands, eyes red-rimmed. "You're the ones come about the road," the reeve says. "Good. Ask what you need — then make it safe."

**Quest-givers (let the party question them):**

- **Reeve Maelis Carrow** *("Reeve" is her title; swap "Carrow" if you like).* **Backstory:** a former caravan-master who quit the roads after a bad season cost her a wagon and two friends, and settled in Greywater for a quieter life — then took the reeve's chain because no one else would. Now the very road that drew her here is strangling the town she chose. **Practical, weary, blunt.** *Wants* the Briar Road safe and trade moving; scrapes the **bounty (150 gp + trail supplies)** from a near-empty town coffer. Knows the *what*, never the *why*. *Tell:* counts coin on her fingers as she talks.
- **Tomas the Cooper** — Greywater's barrel-maker. **Backstory:** raised his younger sister **Wren** himself after fever took their parents; she gathered the ash and oak his trade depends on. Six days ago she went up the Briar Road for staves and never came home, and he blames himself for sending her. **Grieving, desperate, painfully hopeful.** *Unknowingly gives Clue 1:* "She only went in to cut staves — she'd no quarrel with a soul…" *Tell:* can't finish a sentence about her. **🔗 Links / payoff:** Wren went up the road **to cut staves** — she *took* from the wood, so she's almost certainly one the **Guardian** took, not the cult. The grove holds the recently-taken in root and soil (not yet gone): **Ending B** can send **Wren home to Tomas**; **Ending A** leaves her among the lost. *(See Endings.)*
- **Brother Aldous** *(DM-only: he's a cult plant).* A soft-spoken almsman "passing through" who tends the sick and presses coppers into hands. **Backstory:** he's a scout for **the Withering**, here to keep Greywater believing in "the beast" and to nudge any hired blades into killing the Guardian — so the harvest can finish unopposed. **Mild, pious, helpful — and steering everyone wrong.** *Urges:* "Put the poor beast down quickly, and don't go disturbing the deep wood — some graves are best left shut." A sharp party may clock him later (his blessings smell faintly of sap and rot). **🔗 Links:** Aldous is **the Withering's** eyes in Greywater (the cult you meet at **Scene 3**); the *"great beast"* rumor (table #2) is **his** planted cover, steering hired blades to kill the **Guardian** (the Showdown) so the Harvest finishes unopposed. Clocking him is an *early* thread to the truth — not proof on its own.

**Word around Greywater — rumors the party can dig up.**
*How to use this: when the players ask around, gossip, or buy a round, hand them rumors — **just pick whichever fit who they're talking to, or roll a d6 for a random one.** Each line is what a villager actually **says**; the italics in the last column are for **your** eyes only (what it really means).*

| Roll / pick | A villager says… | *(DM only — what it points to)* |
|:--:|---|---|
| 1 | "Only folk who went in to **cut or hunt** have gone missing — never a pilgrim, never a child." | *Clue 1 — true: the wood takes only those who harm it.* |
| 2 | "It's a **great beast** — a thorn-wolf big as a horse, they say." | *The cover story — half-true: the bramble-wolves (Scene 1) are real, but not the cause.* |
| 3 | "Old **Fenn** still keeps her hut past the fork. Daft as a coot, but she knows that wood." | *→ Scene 2: Fenn is the clearest route to the Key.* |
| 4 | "**Grey-robed strangers** bought the old logging rights — and cart *something* out at night." | *True: the Withering's harvest crew (Scene 3).* |
| 5 | "Folk used to leave **bread at the standing stones** each spring. Kept the wood kind, my gran said." | *Lore — the grove and its Guardian.* |
| 6 | "The **cutting's been worse this year** than ever. The wood feels… *angry*." | *Thematic — true.* |

**Then you set out** → the road, below.

### 🌲 On the road — the vanishing site
*(Open here directly if you skipped the village.)*

> **Read aloud:** The Briar Road runs into the Mournwood like a tongue into a dark mouth. Late-afternoon light comes down green and thin through the canopy, and the birds have stopped. An overturned handcart lies half off the road, one wheel still lazily turning. There's no blood — only a deep smell of cut sap and wet earth, and a single iron hatchet sunk in the dirt, its owner nowhere. Ahead, the road forks: the worn ruts bend on through the trees, while a fainter trail peels left, the ferns flattened as if something heavy was *dragged* into the deep wood.

**Then (launch the Two Paths):** let them investigate the cart, then choose — **follow the road** (Path of Least Resistance → Scene 1) or **follow the drag-marks** off-trail (Path Least Traveled On → Scene 2/3).

**Investigate the cart** *(seed clues 1 & 2 — with the lines you can read on a hit):*

- **Clue 1 — the victim pattern.** *Wis (Insight) or Int (Investigation) **DC 12** — or just hand it over from the cargo.*
  - **On a hit, read:** *"It's a woodcutter's load — fresh ash staves, bundled snares, a billhook. You think back on the reeve's list of the lost: a trapper, two cutters, a charcoal-burner. Every one of them came into this wood to **take** something from it."*
  - **On a miss:** *"It's a hauler's cart, nothing stranger — though the more you look, the more the load nags at you."* (Try another angle, or it resurfaces at the next site.)
  - **🔗 Clue 1 recurs:** the same victim-pattern returns at **Scene 1** (the maddened wood drives back those who press in to *cut*) and is spelled out on the **Scene 4 carving** (axe-bearers *pulled into the earth*) — three touches so the players feel it.
- **Clue 2 — no beast.** *Wis (Survival) or Int (Nature) **DC 13**.*
  - **On a hit, read:** *"You crouch over the sign. No blood. No tracks of any beast you can name, no drag of a kill. Instead the earth is **churned** — root-scars gouged deep, thorn-scratches raked across the cart, a slick of sweet sap gone tacky. Whatever took the driver didn't hunt them. It pulled them **down** — roots and all."*
  - **On a miss:** *"The ground's a torn-up mess you can't quite read — but it sits wrong for any animal you know."*
- **Scout the fork.** *Wis (Survival) **DC 15**:* the drag-trail and a faint thread of woodsmoke run the same way off-trail — **someone is camped in the deep wood** (the Harvesters' Camp, Scene 3).
- **Avenues from the fork:** the road (fast, the obvious hunt) · the drag-marks (slower, the truth) · or scout first (above).

## Scene 1 — The Bramble-Pack  [⬡ MODULAR] · combat · ~15 min

*(Path of Least Resistance — on the road, deeper in.)*

> **Read aloud:** The road narrows where the old trees lean close. You hear them before you see them — a low, wrong panting, more sob than snarl. Six wolves slink from the brush, but no wolves were ever like *these*: thorned vines have grown *through* their hides, their eyes weep sap, and they move like something is driving them from behind. They don't circle to feed. They circle to **push you back the way you came.**

**Setup / DM notes:** These are ordinary wolves the wounded wood has seized and maddened — the forest lashing out at intruders on the harm-road. **They fight to herd, not to kill:** a clever party notices the wolves break off if the party *retreats* or shows they mean the grove no harm. *(Reinforces Clue 2 — the woods themselves are hostile to those who press deeper to cut.)*

**🔎 Clues here:** reinforces **Clue 2** (it's the *wood*, not a beast) and nods **Clue 1** (it drives back those who came to cut). *(Appendix B.)*

> **▸ First fight — keep it short.** This is likely the party's first taste of combat: let them feel a round or two of teeth (a knockdown, a near-miss), then end it. After about **2 rounds** — or the moment they retreat, land a couple of solid hits, or calm the pack (below) — the wood *lets them go.* **Don't grind all six wolves down to zero;** the point is the *wrongness* of these beasts, not a war of attrition.

**Encounter (Medium):**
- **Budget check:** Party 300/600/900/1,600. This fight: **6 Wolves ×50 = 300 base ×2 (3–6 band) = 600 adjusted → Medium.**
- **Roster & tactics:** 6 **Wolves** (reflavor: *bramble-wolves*). **Pack Tactics** — they gang up for advantage; **Keen Hearing & Smell** makes surprise hard. They focus the frontline, try to knock prone (Bite rider) and drive PCs *back toward the road's edge*. **Morale (end it in ~2 rounds):** as forest-puppets they don't flee for self-preservation, **but** the wood "lets them go" early — after about **2 rounds**, or the moment the party turns to leave, lands a couple of solid hits, calms them (see the calming check below), or drops the pack to **2**, the vines go slack and the survivors melt into the wood.
- **Stat block (at-the-table copy — also in Appendix A):**
  > **Wolf** *(reflavor: Bramble-Wolf)* — CR 1/4 (50 XP) · MM p.341
  > **AC** 13 (natural armor) · **HP** 11 (2d8 + 2) · **Speed** 40 ft.
  > **STR** 12 (+1) · **DEX** 15 (+2) · **CON** 12 (+1) · **INT** 3 (−4) · **WIS** 12 (+1) · **CHA** 6 (−2)
  > **Skills** Perception +3, Stealth +4 · passive Perception 13
  > ***Keen Hearing and Smell.*** Advantage on Wis (Perception) checks relying on hearing or smell.
  > ***Pack Tactics.*** Advantage on an attack roll vs. a target if a wolf ally is within 5 ft. of it and not incapacitated.
  > ***Bite.*** +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (2d4 + 2) piercing; if the target is a creature, **DC 11 Str save or knocked prone.**

**Reading the pack — the calming check** *(Animal Handling or Nature **DC 15**; the beat that turns the fight into mercy):*
- **On a hit, read:** *"You catch it between heartbeats — the way they flinch from their own thorns, the sap running down their muzzles like tears, the cringe in them even as they lunge. These aren't hunting you. Something behind them is driving them, and it's hurting them to do it. They don't want your throats — they want you to turn back."* *(A PC who then lowers weapons or gives ground ends the fight without a kill — the vines go slack and the pack withdraws.)*
- **On a miss:** *"They're frothing and wrong, and past the snarling you can't get a read on them — best keep your guard up."* *(No penalty; another PC can try next round, or the party just wins the round-or-two fight.)*

**Avenues:** fight · **Survival DC 13** to spot the ambush and gain surprise/avoid it · **Animal Handling/Nature DC 15** to recognize they're driven, not rabid, and calm/scatter them (read-aloud above) · simply **retreating** ends it (they herd, not hunt).
**Bypass (⬡):** if cut for time, the pack is a single tense moment — a wolf or two lunge from the brush, a Survival DC 13 scatters them, and the party reads Clue 2 off the thorns and sap.
**Exit / lead:** the road climbs toward a ridge; through the trees they glimpse **smoke** (the camp, Scene 3) and, beyond, a **green-glowing hollow** (the grove, Scene 4).

## Scene 2 — Fenn's Hollow  [⬡ MODULAR] · roleplay · ~30 min

*(Path Least Traveled On — follow the drag-marks / smoke off-trail. **This is the clearest route to the Key.**)*

> **Read aloud:** The drag-trail ends at a sod-roofed hut grown half into a living oak, its door hung with bundles of dried herbs and little bone charms. An old woman sits on the step with a carving knife and a piece of heartwood, not looking up. "You smell of the road," she says. "Of axes. Tell me true — did you come to cut my wood, or to save it? Lie, and the wood will know."

**Setup / DM notes:** **Fenn** is the last living **grove-warden** — keeper of the grove and old friend to its guardian, **Sorrowroot**. She knows everything: Sorrowroot is no beast, it takes only those who harm the grove, and the **Withering** has been felling the heartwood for months to brew **heart-sap** for **Mother Sere's** rite. She's terrified and grief-stricken (she's watched the guardian go mad with loss) and won't trust killers.

**🔎 Clues here:** **Clue 4 = the Key** — Fenn's testimony, or her journal (full flow below). *(Appendix B.)*

**NPC — Fenn (the grove-warden).** **Backstory:** Greywater-born, she left for the wood sixty years ago to apprentice to the old grove-warden and never came back down the road — a lifetime of leaving bread at the standing stones to keep the wood kind. **Sorrowroot is the only family she has left,** and she's watched grief turn her oldest friend into the thing the village now calls a monster; she blames herself for being too old, and too few, to stop the cutting. *Wants* the grove and her old friend saved · *voice:* slow, plain, weighs every word · *tell:* keeps carving the same little oak figure, and won't meet your eyes at the word "harvest." *(Live dialog: `../../quick-reference/dm-toolkit/social-and-dialog.md`.)*

**Checks & DCs — how the conversation runs, what each sets, what it triggers:**

- **Step 1 — Her test (free, no roll).** She asks: *cut, or save?* The answer rolls no dice — it sets the **modifier** on the trust check below. Honest concern, or naming what they've already seen, leans her open; bluster, bounty-talk, or bared steel closes her.

- **Step 2 — The trust check → the Key.** *Triggered the first time the party makes a real case to her.* **Persuasion / Insight / Deception · DC 15** (player picks the approach: appeal to her, read-and-meet her grief, or a clean lie).
  - **Advantage** if they've found **Clue 1 or 2** and *say so.* · **Disadvantage** if they brag about the bounty, threaten, or wave weapons.
  - **On a hit → this _sets Clue 4_ (the full Key).** Read:
    > *(Fenn, low and steady)* "Then hear it true. The thing on the road is no beast — it's **Sorrowroot**, the old heart of this wood, and it takes only those who come to **wound** it. The grey-robes — the **Withering** — have been felling the heartwood for months, boiling it down to **heart-sap** so their **Mother Sere** will never die. They started the beast-tale themselves: they want hired swords to put my friend down, so none are left to guard the grove when they take the last of it. **Dusk, the third day, they finish.** If you mean to save it — go to the grove *unarmed*, and *grieve* with it. It will know."
  - **On a miss → fail forward** (never a dead end):
    - *Persuasion / Insight miss:* she goes quiet and closes the door — *"You smell too much of axes."* The **journal** (Step 4) is still there and the drag-trail still leads to **the camp (Scene 3)**. **Retry only with new leverage** — bring her **proof** (a heart-sap crate = Clue 3) or cite a Clue 1/2 they hadn't yet mentioned — then **one** more DC 15 attempt, at advantage.
    - *Deception miss:* **the wood knows.** She catches the lie, turns hostile, and flees deeper into the grove — **journal only, no retry.** (This is the risk she warned about up front — flag it to a player *before* they commit to lying.)

- **Step 3 — The threat route (alternative).** If they strong-arm her instead of persuading: **Intimidation · DC 20.** Even on a **hit** → only **fragments** (no "go unarmed" method), her attitude flips **hostile**, she warns them the cult is *using* them, then **flees** (leaving the journal → Clue 4 still gettable). On a **miss** → she clams up and flees; journal only.

- **Step 4 — Fenn's journal — _Bypass (⬡)._** Searching the hut: **Investigation · DC 15** finds the journal = **Clue 4** — the gist (the felling, the heart-sap, *"they mean for heroes to slay my friend so none can stop the Harvest"*) but **not** the live ally or the unarmed-approach method. Auto-handed over if Fenn flees. Use this if the scene is cut.

**🔑 What gets set / triggered (record in the tracker):**
- **Clue 4 = the Key** is obtained from *any* of: winning her trust (full Key + the grove-approach method), the journal, or even her hostile fragments (partial).
- **Key = found** → **flips the trending Ending A → B** and **unlocks Showdown Version B** (fight the cult; Sorrowroot fights as your ally). *(Appendix B: the Key unlocks once the party holds the truth from **Clue 3 or 4**.)*
- **The "go unarmed and grieving" line** is the trust-route's exclusive prize: at the Showdown, a PC who approaches Sorrowroot *without attacking* **reaches it automatically** — no roll to flip the guardian. (A journal-only party still gets Version B, but has to *earn* that moment live — see the Showdown's *"the guardian pleads"* beat.)
- **Fenn's fate flag:** trusted → she lives and can return in **Ending B's hook**; threatened or ignored → she flees deeper into the wood (her survival is yours to decide).

**Exit / lead:** Fenn points them to **the grove** (Scene 4 → Showdown) and begs them not to harm the guardian — or, if they didn't earn trust, they leave with doubt and the journal, and the drag-trail still leads on to **the camp (Scene 3)**.

## Scene 3 — The Harvesters' Camp  [⬡ MODULAR] · combat · ~40 min

*(Path Least Traveled On — the cult's work-camp; the **physical proof** for Ending B.)*

> **Read aloud:** The trees simply *stop*. A raw clearing opens where a stand of ancient oaks used to be — stumps weeping pale sap into catch-buckets, a reek of resin and rot over it all. Robed figures work a crude still that drips a glowing green ichor into iron crates stamped with a **withered crown — a ring of dead, leafless branches.** A harder-eyed man in vestments turns at your approach, hand drifting to a dagger. "Pilgrims? No. The Mother said hunters would come. She didn't say they'd be fool enough to come *here*."

**Setup / DM notes:** The **Withering's** harvest-crew, distilling **heart-sap** (concentrated grove life-force) for Mother Sere's immortality rite. The **overseer** (a Cult Fanatic) will bluff, then fight; the **cultists** defend the still and the crates. **A crate of heart-sap + the withered-crown brand is undeniable proof** (Clue 3) that the "beast" story is a cover.

**🔎 Clues here:** **Clue 3 = the proof** — a heart-sap crate + the withered-crown brand; a captured cultist confirms it aloud. *(Appendix B.)*

> **🔗 Links:** the brand's **withered crown** is the grove's **own leaf-crown** (carved on the Scene 4 standing stones) — stolen and killed; **Mother Sere** wears its dead-branch form at the Showdown. The **heart-sap** in these crates is the same glowing sap she drinks to finish the rite (Showdown). A party that clocks the matching crown *knows* the cult is the rot behind the "beast."

**Encounter (Hard):**
- **Budget check:** This fight: **1 Cult Fanatic (450) + 4 Cultists (4×25=100) = 550 base ×2 (5 monsters, 3–6 band) = 1,100 adjusted → Hard.** *(Tougher party? Add a Thug or two as hired poacher-muscle — see Appendix A.)*
- **Roster & tactics:** the **Cult Fanatic** overseer hangs back, opens with **hold person** on the party's heaviest hitter and **spiritual weapon**, heals himself with nothing (he won't — he *inflicts*), and uses **command ("flee"/"grovel")**; the **Cultists** swarm and protect the still. Terrain: catch-buckets (difficult footing), the still (a hazard — a hit on it sprays scalding sap, DC 13 Dex or 1d6 fire). **Morale (`../../quick-reference/dm-toolkit/encounters-and-monsters.md`):** when the overseer falls or the cultists are cut to **2**, survivors break and beg/flee — **a captured cultist is a goldmine** (the rite, the timing, Mother Sere at the grove).
- **Stat blocks — at-the-table copies below** *(also in Appendix A; mechanics identical).*

> **Cult Fanatic** *(reflavor: Harvest Overseer)* — CR 2 (450 XP) · MM p.345
> **AC** 13 (leather) · **HP** 33 (6d8 + 6) · **Speed** 30 ft.
> **STR** 11 (+0) · **DEX** 14 (+2) · **CON** 12 (+1) · **INT** 10 (+0) · **WIS** 13 (+1) · **CHA** 14 (+2)
> **Skills** Deception +4, Persuasion +4, Religion +2 · passive Perception 11
> ***Dark Devotion.*** Advantage on saves vs. charmed/frightened.
> ***Spellcasting.*** 4th-level (Wis, **save DC 11**, +3 to hit). Cantrips — *light, sacred flame, thaumaturgy*; 1st (4 slots) — *command, inflict wounds, shield of faith*; 2nd (3 slots) — *hold person, spiritual weapon*.
> ***Multiattack.*** Two melee attacks. ***Dagger.*** +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft. — *Hit:* 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing.

> **Cultist** ×4 — CR 1/8 (25 XP each) · MM p.345
> **AC** 12 (leather) · **HP** 9 (2d8) · **Speed** 30 ft.
> **STR** 11 (+0) · **DEX** 12 (+1) · **CON** 10 (+0) · **INT** 10 (+0) · **WIS** 11 (+0) · **CHA** 10 (+0)
> **Skills** Deception +2, Religion +2 · passive Perception 10

## Scene 4 — The Warded Grove  [⬡ MODULAR] · exploration / puzzle · ~30 min

*(Both paths funnel here — the approach to the Showdown.)*

> **Read aloud:** The air thickens, sweet and grievous. Ahead, a ring of standing stones leans around the oldest trees you've ever seen — and the way *shifts*: paths double back, a fallen log is suddenly a yawning ravine, birdsong loops like a stuck bell. Old fey wards still guard this place, tangled now with the wood's grief. On the nearest stone, worn carvings catch the green light: a great tree **crowned in leaves**, a procession of small robed figures — and, over and over, others laid into the earth.

**Setup / DM notes:** A **fey-warded** grove approach — illusion and shifting paths protect the sacred heart. Crossing it is a **skill challenge**; the standing-stone carvings hold the grove's *ancient* story.

**🔎 Clues here:** *no new numbered clue* — the carving **reinforces Clue 1/2** and reveals the guardian's nature (a nudge toward mercy, **not** the Key). *(Appendix B.)*

> **▸ What the carvings are (and aren't).** They reveal the **guardian's nature** — an old, grieving protector that takes only those who *harm* the wood — a strong nudge toward mercy and the Showdown's plea. But they're cut in **old stone, so they can't show the modern Withering cult.** They **reinforce** the truth; they are **not the full Key.** A party that learned nothing on the side path (no **Clue 3 or 4**) still arrives **without the Key → Version A / Ending A**; a party that already holds Clue 3 or 4 has the carvings *confirm* it.

**▸ How to run a skill challenge** *(read this if it's new — grounded in `../../quick-reference/dm-toolkit/traps-puzzles-hazards.md`):*
1. **Name the goal & stakes:** *reach the grove's heart before the wards — or the rite — turn on you.*
2. **Go round the table:** each PC **describes how they push forward**, then rolls the skill that fits *what they described* (list below) at **DC 15**. Reward creativity; **don't let everyone roll the same skill** — the same one twice running gets harder or is disallowed.
3. **Narrate every roll:** a hit = ground gained / a stone read; a miss = a looping path, a false floor, a near-fall.
4. **Tally to 4 successes (they're through) or 3 failures (through it the _hard_ way).** Never a dead end.

**Skill avenues (DC 15 each):**
- **Survival / Nature** — read true ground from illusion; find the safe path.
- **Arcana** — recognize and step through the fey wards.
- **Perception / Investigation** — catch the looped birdsong; read the standing-stone carvings.
- **Athletics / Acrobatics** — clear a real ravine or a tangle of grasping roots.

**Outcome — 4 successes (they cross).** They reach the grove **surefooted and forewarned**, and the stones tell it plainly. **Read:**
> "The worn carvings come clear as you pass. A great tree with a grieving face, **crowned in living leaves**, sheltering generation upon generation of small robed keepers who leave bread at its roots. Then others: figures who come bearing **axes** — and are **pulled down into the earth.** Last of all the tree stands alone, weeping, its branches bare and its **leaf-crown broken and fallen.** This is no beast's den. It is a **grave-keeper**, old as the wood, and it strikes only those who *wound* it."

> **🔗 DM-only — the crown links the scenes (let a sharp party connect it).** The tree's **living leaf-crown** is the grove's own ancient emblem. A party that has seen the **Scene 3 crates** (the *withered*-crown brand) — or who later sees **Mother Sere's crown of dead branches** at the Showdown — can realize the Withering **stole the grove's crown and killed it:** the same symbol in three stages — *living → branded → worn dead.* It's a chilling confirmation of the truth, **but not a standalone Key** (a party that never reached the camp just sees ancient heraldry). And the carved **robed keepers** are the line of **grove-wardens before Fenn** (Scene 2) — she is the last of them.

**The edge (pick one):** the party may **act first at the Showdown** (a free surprise round *before* initiative), **or** take **advantage on their first attempt to reach / plead with the guardian.**

**Outcome — 3 failures (the hard way).** The wards turn on them — **1d6 psychic** each (grief made manifest), the carvings stay a blur, and they spill into the Showdown **off-balance** (no edge; the rite/guardian is already mid-action).

**Bypass (⬡):** a single **Nature or Arcana DC 15** walks the party through and gives the gist of the carvings — the **guardian's nature only**, *not* the cult (same reinforcement-only rule).
**Exit / lead:** beyond the stones, the **grove itself** — and the Showdown.

## The Showdown — The Weeping Grove  [★ CORE] · climax · ~45–60 min

> **Read aloud:** The grove opens like a wound. At its heart, the greatest tree of all has half torn *itself* from the earth — bark like a grieving face, roots dragging through soil churned with cut stumps. Around it, robed figures chant over a fire of green flame, and a withered woman in a crown of dead branches lifts a vial of glowing sap to her lips. The great tree turns its slow, sorrowing face toward you. *"Another axe,"* it says, in a voice like splitting heartwood. *"Have you come to cut me too?"*

**Setup:** The **Great Harvest** at its climax: **Mother Sere** is about to complete her immortality rite, and the grove's **Guardian** stands maddened with grief. **The Key decides who you fight.** *(Keep the mechanics the same; change the flavor — MMOS Step 5.)*

**🔎 Clues here:** **Clue 5 = the Guardian's plea + vision** — the fail-safe: even a kill-first party gets one last chance to flip to the truth. *(Appendix B.)*

> **🔗 Links:** Mother Sere's **crown of dead branches** is the grove's own leaf-crown (Scene 4 carving) withered into the cult's brand (Scene 3 crates) — the same symbol in three stages: *living → branded → worn dead.* The **vial of glowing sap** she lifts is the **heart-sap** distilled at the camp (Scene 3). The **Guardian** here is **Sorrowroot** — the oak figure Fenn keeps whittling (Scene 2).

**Before initiative — the guardian pleads (Clue 5, the fail-safe).** Whatever path they took, the Guardian — **Sorrowroot** — *speaks first* and shows a **vision** (free action): the grove green and whole, then the axes, then its children laid in the earth. **Any PC may try to reach it** — Persuasion/Insight, or simply lowering weapons.

> **Read — the vision, then the plea (Clue 5):** The tree's sorrow washes over you as a *seeing:* this grove green and singing, small keepers leaving bread at its roots — then the axes come, the green goes grey, and one by one its children are laid in the earth. The vision breaks. *"I was the keeping-place of this wood. I took only those who came to **wound** it. Now they bleed my heart into their vials and send me killers wearing the faces of heroes. If you too have come for my heartwood — strike; I am so tired. But if you have eyes to see who truly holds the axe… lay down your blade, and help me."*

**Then it branches on what they do:**
- **If the party has the Key** (Fenn's truth *or* the heart-sap proof — Scene 4's carvings *reinforce* it but don't supply it alone): they can name the real enemy. The Guardian's grief turns to **wrath at the cult** → **Version B**. **Read:** *"Then I am not alone. Stand with me — and we will end the ones who did this."* *(With Fenn's full Key, a PC who simply lays down arms and grieves with it reaches the Guardian **automatically — no roll**; that's the Scene 2 promise paying off.)*
- **If they don't:** the Guardian, seeing only more armed strangers in its dying home, attacks (or the cult goads it onto them) → **Version A**. **Read:** *"…Another axe. So be it."* Its roots tear free of the soil. *(A lone PC can still attempt DC 20 Persuasion to flip it mid-fight — reward the attempt.)*

### Version A — "The Beast" (no Key) · Deadly
The party fights the **Guardian** itself. Tragic: they "win the quest" by killing the grove's wounded protector; the cult slips away to finish elsewhere.
- **Budget check:** **Guardian (Shambling Mound, 1,800) + 3 thorn-spawn (Twig Blights, 25 ea — *ignored for the multiplier*, CR ≪ boss) ≈ 1,800 adjusted → Deadly.**
- **Tactics:** the Guardian **grapples + Engulfs** the heaviest-armored PC (rooting them in living wood), slams the rest; thorn-spawn harry the backline. **It is immune to lightning (absorbs it) and resists cold & fire** — note this before a caster wastes a *fireball*. It fights with grief, not malice: if reduced low it *keens* and gives the party one more chance to stop (a final Persuasion DC 20 can still flip to Version B even now).

> **▸ Running Version A safely — your biggest TPK risk.** This is a **solo CR 5 Engulfer** (≈1,800 vs the 1,600 Deadly line) that *can* take a PC out of the fight. Keeping it survivable is **by design**, not softness:
> - **Grief, not bloodlust.** Like the wolves, Sorrowroot fights to *drive off* — it doesn't chase fleeing PCs or finish the downed; it grapples and Engulfs to **stop** them, keening the whole time.
> - **Engulf has an exit.** Only **one** creature at a time; **escape DC 14**, or free them by dropping it low. A PC at 0 HP inside it still makes **death saves** — never kill quietly.
> - **The flip is always open.** Any round, lowering weapons or a **DC 20 Persuasion** ends it into **Version B** (Sorrowroot stops, becomes an ally) — the cleanest win and a pressure valve.
> - **Dial it live.** Cut a thorn-spawn or two; or have it Engulf and *hold* a downed PC "in the roots" (no damage) instead of crushing, if the table's on the ropes.

- **Stat blocks:** **Sorrowroot** (Shambling Mound) + **thorn-spawn** (Twig Blights) — **inlined below** (also Appendix A).

### Version B — "The True Harvest" (has the Key) · Deadly
The party turns the Guardian on the cult. **Mother Sere + her cultists** are the enemy; the **Guardian fights as the party's ally** (DM runs it; it focuses Mother Sere).
- **Budget check (enemies only):** **Mother Sere (Green Hag, 700) + 4 Cultists (100) = 800 base ×2 (5 monsters) = 1,600 adjusted → Deadly** — and the **Guardian ally** tips it to a winnable, heroic Deadly. *(Add the Cult Fanatic overseer here if he survived/escaped Scene 3.)*
- **Tactics:** **Mother Sere** opens with **Invisible Passage** and **Illusory Appearance** (she may look like a frightened captive — let a PC's Insight or the Guardian's senses cut through), strikes with **Claws (2d8+4)**, and uses **vicious mockery / minor illusion** to split the party; cultists screen her and tend the green fire (the rite — a **clock:** 3 rounds to completion unless disrupted; smashing the fire/vial sets it back). The **Guardian** grapples and Engulfs Mother Sere if it can. **Morale:** cultists break when she falls.
- **Stat blocks:** **Mother Sere** (Green Hag) + **Cultists** + the allied **Sorrowroot** (Shambling Mound) — **inlined below** (also Appendix A).

## Endings

- **Ending A — Adequate** *(no Key):* The Guardian falls. The Briar Road goes quiet, Greywater pays the bounty — but the grove keeps dying, the heart-sap carts still roll out of the wood, and somewhere Mother Sere drinks to her long life. A hollow victory the players will *feel*. *(Quest complete, the obvious way.)*
  - **🔗 Wren (Tomas's sister):** with the Guardian dead, what it took stays taken — the roots give up only bones. **Tomas gets a body to bury, not a sister.** Let that land when they collect the bounty.
- **Ending B — Ballsy** *(requires the Key):* Mother Sere falls, the rite breaks, and the Guardian — grief spent — sinks its roots back into the earth and **begins to heal the grove**. It marks the party as friends of the wood. The cult's surviving harvesters scatter. The road is safe *because the truth was.*
  - **🔗 Wren (Tomas's sister):** as the grief lifts, the grove **loosens its grip on the recently-taken** — the soil opens and the still-living are given back, **Wren among them.** Her homecoming to Tomas is the warm beat that pays off the Cold Open. *(DM's call how many of the lost return; keep the oldest taken as graves.)*

## Hooks
- **If Ending A →** weeks later the vanishings begin again on a *different* road — the cult has other groves — and a robed envoy of the Withering arrives in Greywater to "thank" the famous beast-slayers… and offer them work.
- **If Ending B →** the grateful Guardian (or Fenn) brings word that the Withering tends **three more groves** deeper in the wilds, each with its own bound guardian and its own withered prophet. The wood asks the party to finish what they started.
