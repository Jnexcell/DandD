# Creating a Character

The step-by-step procedure for building a 1st-level D&D character — choosing race and class, generating ability scores, describing the character, picking equipment — plus the key derived numbers and how characters advance beyond 1st level.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 1, "Step-by-Step Characters" (printed pages 11–15), including "Beyond 1st Level" (p.15).

---

## The six steps

Building a character follows six steps in order (PHB p.11). Each choice may reshape your idea of the character; the goal is to arrive at a character you're excited to play.

1. **Choose a race** (PHB p.11)
2. **Choose a class** (PHB p.11)
3. **Determine ability scores** (PHB p.12–13)
4. **Describe your character** (PHB p.13)
5. **Choose equipment** (PHB p.14)
6. **Come together as a group** (PHB p.15)

> The PHB walks through these steps with a running example, "Building Bruenor," a mountain dwarf fighter.

### 1. Choose a race (PHB p.11)

Every character belongs to a **race** (dwarf, elf, halfling, human, and the less common dragonborn, gnome, half-elf, half-orc, tiefling). Some races have **subraces** (e.g., mountain dwarf, wood elf). Race grants:

- **Racial traits** — special senses, weapon/tool/skill proficiencies, minor spells, etc.
- One or more **ability score increases** (applied in step 3).
- A **base speed** and **starting languages**.

Record all of these on your character sheet (PHB p.11).

### 2. Choose a class (PHB p.11)

Every character is a member of a **class** (described in Chapter 3). Your class is the single biggest source of your capabilities. It provides:

- **Class features** (including spellcasting for some classes).
- **Hit Dice** — the die type for your hit points (e.g., a fighter has a d10).
- **Proficiencies** — armor, weapons, skills, saving throws, and sometimes tools.
- **Starting equipment** (combined with what your background gives).

Most characters begin at **1st level** with **0 XP** (PHB p.12). A higher-level start (DM's option) uses the minimum XP for that level.

### 3. Determine ability scores (PHB p.12)

Your character has six abilities — **Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma**. Generate six numbers, then assign one to each ability, **then apply your racial increases** (PHB p.13). There are three common methods:

**a) Roll for scores.** Roll **4d6, drop the lowest die**, and total the rest. Do this six times to get six numbers (PHB p.13).

**b) Standard array.** Use the fixed set of scores instead of rolling (PHB p.13):

| 15 | 14 | 13 | 12 | 10 | 8 |
|----|----|----|----|----|---|

**c) Point buy (variant: Customizing Ability Scores).** You have **27 points** to spend. Every score starts at 8; the highest you can buy is **15** (before racial increases), and the lowest is 8 (PHB p.13).

| Score | Cost | Score | Cost |
|-------|------|-------|------|
| 8 | 0 | 12 | 4 |
| 9 | 1 | 13 | 5 |
| 10 | 2 | 14 | 7 |
| 11 | 3 | 15 | 9 |

After assigning scores and applying racial bonuses, determine each **ability modifier**: subtract 10 from the score and divide by 2, rounding down (PHB p.13).

| Score | Mod | Score | Mod | Score | Mod |
|-------|-----|-------|-----|-------|-----|
| 1 | −5 | 10–11 | +0 | 20–21 | +5 |
| 2–3 | −4 | 12–13 | +1 | 22–23 | +6 |
| 4–5 | −3 | 14–15 | +2 | 24–25 | +7 |
| 6–7 | −2 | 16–17 | +3 | 26–27 | +8 |
| 8–9 | −1 | 18–19 | +4 | 28–29 | +9 |
| | | | | 30 | +10 |

### 4. Describe your character (PHB p.13)

Flesh out your character as a person using Chapter 4: pick a **name, sex, height/weight, and alignment**, and develop **personality traits, ideals, bonds, and flaws**. Choose a **background**, which gives a **background feature, proficiency in two skills**, and possibly additional **languages or tool proficiencies** (PHB p.13). Let your ability scores inform your description — a strong, low-Intelligence character behaves very differently from a frail genius.

### 5. Choose equipment (PHB p.14)

Your class and background grant **starting equipment** (weapons, armor, gear). Alternatively, you may **buy equipment** with a starting pool of gold pieces based on your class (see Chapter 5). You may also take **one trinket** for free. Don't buy gear weighing more (in pounds) than your **Strength score × 15** — your carrying capacity (PHB p.14).

### 6. Come together as a group (PHB p.15)

D&D is a team game. Talk with the other players and the DM about how the party members know each other, how they met, and what kinds of quests they'll pursue (PHB p.15).

## Key derived statistics

Once the six steps are done, calculate these (PHB p.12–14):

- **Proficiency bonus.** **+2 at 1st level**, rising as you advance (see the Character Advancement table). It applies to attack rolls with weapons you're proficient with, attack rolls with spells, ability checks using skills or tools you're proficient with, saving throws you're proficient in, and your spell save DCs. It is **never added more than once** to a single roll (PHB p.12).
- **Hit points & Hit Dice.** At 1st level you have **1 Hit Die** (type set by your class). Your starting hit points equal the **maximum** of that die **+ your Constitution modifier**; this is also your hit point maximum. After resting you can spend Hit Dice to recover hit points (PHB p.12).
- **Armor Class (AC).** With no armor or shield, **AC = 10 + Dexterity modifier**. Armor and shields change this calculation (Chapter 5). You must be proficient with armor/shields to use them effectively (PHB p.14).
- **Ability modifiers.** Derived from each score as shown above; used throughout the game (PHB p.13).
- **Attack bonus & damage.** A weapon attack = **d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus** (if proficient). Melee weapons use **Strength** (a **finesse** weapon may use Dexterity); ranged weapons use **Dexterity** (a **thrown** weapon may use Strength). Add the same ability modifier to damage (PHB p.14).

## Beyond 1st Level (PHB p.15)

Characters advance by earning **experience points (XP)**. Reaching an XP threshold means **gaining a level**.

On gaining a level you typically gain new class features, sometimes an **Ability Score Improvement** (raise one score by 2 or two scores by 1, never above **20**), and possibly a higher **proficiency bonus**. You also gain **1 additional Hit Die**: roll it (or take the fixed average from your class), add your Constitution modifier, and add the total to your hit point maximum (PHB p.15).

> If your Constitution **modifier** later increases by 1, your hit point maximum rises by 1 **for every level you have** (PHB p.15).

### Tiers of play (PHB p.15)

The tiers have **no rules of their own**; they describe how play changes as characters grow:

- **Tier 1 (levels 1–4):** apprentice adventurers learning their class's defining features; threats are local.
- **Tier 2 (levels 5–10):** characters come into their own — many spellcasters reach 3rd-level spells (*fireball*, *lightning bolt*), and many martials gain Extra Attack; they face threats to cities and kingdoms.
- **Tier 3 (levels 11–16):** characters rise far above ordinary folk; many casters reach 6th-level spells; threats span regions and continents.
- **Tier 4 (levels 17–20):** characters reach the pinnacle of their class and become legendary; the fate of the world or multiverse may be at stake.

## Common questions

**Q: What's my proficiency bonus at 1st level?**
A: **+2** for every class (PHB p.12). It rises with level per the Character Advancement table — +3 at 5th, +4 at 9th, +5 at 13th, +6 at 17th.

**Q: How do I roll ability scores, and can I avoid rolling?**
A: Roll **4d6 and drop the lowest die**, six times. To skip rolling, use the **standard array** (15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8) or the **27-point buy** variant (PHB p.13).

**Q: What's the most a point-buy score can be, and how much does it cost?**
A: The cap is **15 before racial increases**, costing **9 of your 27 points**; 14 costs 7, 13 costs 5 (PHB p.13).

**Q: How are 1st-level hit points calculated?**
A: Take the **maximum value** of your class's Hit Die and add your Constitution modifier (e.g., a fighter with +2 Con has 10 + 2 = 12 HP) (PHB p.12).

**Q: Can I increase an ability score above 20?**
A: Not through normal Ability Score Improvements — the cap is **20** (PHB p.15). Certain magic items or epic boons can exceed it.

**Q: How much XP do I need to reach 5th level?**
A: **6,500 XP**, which also bumps your proficiency bonus to +3 (PHB p.15).
