# Personality and Background

How to define a character beyond race and class — name, sex, physical details, alignment, languages, inspiration, the personality framework (traits, ideals, bonds, flaws), and the rules for backgrounds, including a reference table of all 13 PHB backgrounds.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Personality and Background" (printed pages 121–141).

---

## Character details (PHB p.121–122)

Flesh out your character with basic details:

- **Name.** Each race's description in Chapter 2 includes sample names.
- **Sex.** Play any sex/gender without mechanical benefit or penalty; how the character relates to cultural expectations is yours to define.
- **Height and weight.** Pick freely, or roll on the **Random Height and Weight** table in your race entry. The Height Modifier roll adds inches to a base height; that same roll, times the Weight Modifier, adds pounds to a base weight (PHB p.121).
- **Other physical traits.** Choose age, hair/eye/skin color, and distinctive features (a scar, a limp, a tattoo).

## Alignment (PHB p.122)

Alignment combines a **morality** axis (good / neutral / evil) with an **order** axis (lawful / neutral / chaotic), giving nine combinations. It describes typical behavior; individuals vary.

| | Good | Neutral | Evil |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lawful** | **LG** — reliably does the right thing as society expects (gold dragons, paladins, most dwarves) | **LN** — acts by law, tradition, or a personal code (many monks, some wizards) | **LE** — methodically takes what it wants within a code (devils, blue dragons, hobgoblins) |
| **Neutral** | **NG** — does the best it can to help others (many celestials, most gnomes) | **N** — steers clear of moral questions; does what seems best (many druids, lizardfolk) | **NE** — does whatever it can get away with, without compassion (many drow, yugoloths) |
| **Chaotic** | **CG** — follows its conscience with little regard for others' expectations (copper dragons, many elves, unicorns) | **CN** — follows its whims, prizing personal freedom (many barbarians and rogues) | **CE** — acts with arbitrary violence, driven by greed or bloodlust (demons, red dragons, orcs) |

Creatures incapable of moral choice (most beasts) are **unaligned** (PHB p.123). Note: in most games **evil alignments are reserved for villains and monsters**, not player characters (PHB p.123).

## Languages (PHB p.123)

Your race grants default languages; a background may grant additional ones of your choice. Pick from the **Standard Languages** table, or — with DM permission — an **Exotic Language** or a secret language (such as **thieves' cant** or the **Druidic** tongue). Some languages (e.g., **Primordial**, with its Auran/Aquan/Ignan/Terran dialects) are families whose speakers can understand each other.

## Inspiration (PHB p.125)

**Inspiration** is an optional reward the DM grants for playing your character true to their personality trait, ideal, bond, or flaw — or otherwise being compelling.

- **Gaining it:** the DM awards it; you either have inspiration or you don't — it **can't be stockpiled**.
- **Spending it:** expend it on an **attack roll, saving throw, or ability check** to gain **advantage** on that roll.
- You can also **give your inspiration to another player** to reward their great roleplaying or clever play.

## Personality framework (PHB p.123–125)

Four categories of characteristics bring a character to life. Each background offers suggested options (with dice tables) you can roll, pick, or use as inspiration.

- **Personality traits (choose two):** specific, fun self-descriptions that set the character apart ("I've read every book in Candlekeep," not "I'm smart").
- **Ideal (choose one):** the fundamental moral/ethical principle that drives the character. Each background lists six, five tied to alignment aspects (law, chaos, good, evil, neutrality) and one tied to the background itself.
- **Bond (choose one):** a connection to a person, place, or event — a source of motivation that can also be exploited against you.
- **Flaw (choose one):** a vice, fear, compulsion, or weakness someone could use to bring you to ruin or push you against your interests.

## Backgrounds (PHB p.125–141)

A background reveals where your character came from and how they became an adventurer. Mechanically, every background grants (PHB p.125):

- **Proficiency in two skills.**
- Usually **one or more tool proficiencies** and/or **additional languages**.
- A **package of starting equipment** (skipped if you instead spend coin on gear, per Chapter 5).
- A **background feature** — a non-combat benefit (social access, contacts, shelter, etc.).
- **Suggested characteristics** (trait/ideal/bond/flaw tables).

If a background would grant a proficiency you already have, choose a **different** one of the same kind (PHB p.125). You may also **customize** a background: swap its feature for another, choose any two skills, and choose two tool proficiencies/languages from the sample backgrounds (PHB p.126).

## Common questions

**Q: What does a background give me mechanically?**
A: Two skill proficiencies, usually some tool proficiencies and/or languages, a starting-equipment package, and a background feature (a social/utility benefit) (PHB p.125).

**Q: My class and background grant the same skill proficiency — do I lose one?**
A: No. When two sources grant the same proficiency, pick a **different** proficiency of the same type (skill or tool) instead (PHB p.125).

**Q: What does spending Inspiration do?**
A: It gives you **advantage** on one attack roll, saving throw, or ability check. You can't hold more than one Inspiration at a time, and you may instead hand it to another player (PHB p.125).

**Q: How many alignments are there, and can I play an evil character?**
A: Nine (three order × three morality), plus "unaligned" for non-thinking creatures. Evil alignments are generally reserved for villains; most parties run good or neutral PCs (PHB p.122–123).

**Q: Can I change a background's skills or feature to fit my concept?**
A: Yes — the Customizing a Background option lets you swap the feature, choose any two skills, and pick two tool proficiencies/languages from the sample backgrounds (PHB p.126).

**Q: How do I pick languages?**
A: Take what your race gives, plus any from your background. Choose from the Standard Languages table freely; Exotic or secret languages (thieves' cant, Druidic) require DM permission (PHB p.123).
