# Equipment

Coinage and exchange rates, the full armor and weapons tables with rules for how they work, weapon properties, adventuring gear and packs, tools, mounts and vehicles, trade goods, lifestyle expenses, and carrying capacity.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 5, "Equipment" (printed pages 143–161); carrying capacity from Chapter 7 (p.176).

## Coinage (PHB p.143)

The standard unit of wealth is the **gold piece (gp)**. Five coin denominations exist; a standard coin weighs about a third of an ounce, so **50 coins weigh 1 pound**.

| Coin | = cp | = sp | = ep | = gp | = pp |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Copper (cp) | 1 | 1/10 | 1/50 | 1/100 | 1/1,000 |
| Silver (sp) | 10 | 1 | 1/5 | 1/10 | 1/100 |
| Electrum (ep) | 50 | 5 | 1 | 1/2 | 1/20 |
| Gold (gp) | 100 | 10 | 2 | 1 | 1/10 |
| Platinum (pp) | 1,000 | 100 | 20 | 10 | 1 |

In short: **10 cp = 1 sp**, **5 sp = 1 ep**, **10 sp = 1 gp**, **10 gp = 1 pp**. Electrum and platinum come from fallen empires and can arouse suspicion in transactions (PHB p.143).

**Selling treasure:** undamaged weapons, armor, and gear sell for about **half** their cost; gems, art objects, and trade goods retain **full value**; magic items are rarely buyable or sellable except potions and scrolls. *(This is a DMG guideline — "Selling Treasure," DMG Ch.7, not a PHB rule.)*

## Armor and shields (PHB p.144–145)

The Armor table lists the common armor types in three categories — **light, medium, heavy** — plus shields.

- **Armor proficiency.** Wearing armor you're **not** proficient with gives **disadvantage** on any ability check, save, or attack involving Strength or Dexterity, and you **can't cast spells** (PHB p.144).
- **How AC works by category:**
  - **Light armor:** AC = listed value **+ full Dex modifier**.
  - **Medium armor:** AC = listed value **+ Dex modifier (max +2)**.
  - **Heavy armor:** AC = listed value (**no Dex modifier**; a negative Dex doesn't penalize you).
- **Strength requirement.** If the Strength column shows "Str 13/15," your speed drops by **10 ft.** unless your Strength meets the listed score (PHB p.144).
- **Stealth.** "Disadvantage" in the Stealth column means **disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks** (PHB p.144).
- **Shields.** A shield is held in one hand and gives **+2 AC**; you benefit from only one at a time (PHB p.145).

## Weapons (PHB p.146–149)

Weapons are **simple** or **martial** (proficiency category) and **melee** or **ranged**. With proficiency, add your proficiency bonus to attack rolls with that weapon (PHB p.146).

### Weapon properties (PHB p.147)

- **Ammunition** *(range X/Y)*: requires ammo to fire; drawing it is part of the attack; recover **half** your spent ammo after the fight. A sling must be loaded to deal damage.
- **Finesse:** use **Strength or Dexterity** (your choice) for attack and damage — same ability for both.
- **Heavy:** **Small creatures have disadvantage** on attacks with it.
- **Light:** ideal for two-weapon fighting.
- **Loading:** you can fire only **one** piece of ammunition per action/bonus action/reaction, no matter how many attacks you have.
- **Range** *(X/Y)*: first number is normal range, second is maximum. Beyond normal range you have **disadvantage**; you **can't** attack beyond long range.
- **Reach:** adds **5 ft.** to your reach for attacks.
- **Special:** the weapon has unique rules (e.g., lance, net) in its description.
- **Thrown** *(range X/Y)*: can be thrown for a ranged attack, using the same ability modifier you'd use in melee.
- **Two-handed:** requires two hands to attack with.
- **Versatile** *(damage)*: usable with one or two hands; the parenthesized die is the **two-handed** damage.

## Adventuring gear highlights (PHB p.150–153)

Common gear includes backpacks, rope, torches, rations, waterskins, tinderboxes, bedrolls, and more. Items with special rules include:

- **Acid (vial), Alchemist's fire (flask), Oil:** thrown improvised weapons (ranged attack, ~20 ft.). Acid deals **2d6** acid damage on a hit. **Alchemist's fire** deals **1d4 fire** damage on a hit, then **1d4 fire** at the start of each of the target's turns until it uses an action to make a DC 10 Dexterity check to extinguish the flames. **Oil**, if thrown and ignited, deals **5 fire** damage (PHB p.152).
- **Potion of Healing:** drinking it (an action) restores **2d4 + 2 HP**.
- **Holy water (flask):** thrown at a fiend/undead for 2d6 radiant.
- **Lantern, hooded:** bright light 30-ft. radius + 30 ft. dim, burns 6 hours per pint of oil.
- **Healer's kit, manacles, hunting trap, portable ram, magnifying glass, lock, etc.**

## Tools (PHB p.154)

A tool lets you do something you otherwise couldn't (craft, forge, pick a lock). Proficiency adds your proficiency bonus to checks using the tool; the DM decides which ability the check uses, depending on the task (PHB p.154).

- **Artisan's tools** (each a separate proficiency): alchemist's supplies, brewer's, calligrapher's, carpenter's, cartographer's, cobbler's, cook's utensils, glassblower's, jeweler's, leatherworker's, mason's, painter's, potter's, smith's, tinker's, weaver's, woodcarver's.
- **Other tools:** disguise kit, forgery kit, herbalism kit, navigator's tools, poisoner's kit, thieves' tools.
- **Gaming sets:** dice, dragonchess, playing cards, Three-Dragon Ante.
- **Musical instruments:** bagpipes, drum, dulcimer, flute, lute, lyre, horn, pan flute, shawm, viol.

## Carrying capacity (PHB p.176)

- **Carrying capacity = Strength score × 15** (pounds). Most characters never need to track this.
- **Push, drag, or lift:** up to **Strength score × 30** (twice carrying capacity). While pushing/dragging weight above your carrying capacity, your **speed drops to 5 ft.**
- **Size:** for each size above Medium, **double** carrying capacity and push/drag/lift; **halve** for Tiny creatures.

### Variant: Encumbrance (PHB p.176)

A more detailed option (ignore the Armor table's Strength column when using it):

- Carrying weight over **Strength × 5**: you're **encumbered** — speed −10 ft.
- Carrying weight over **Strength × 10** (up to max capacity): you're **heavily encumbered** — speed −20 ft. **and** disadvantage on ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws using Str, Dex, or Con.

## Common questions

**Q: How is AC calculated for each armor type?**
A: Light = base + full Dex; medium = base + Dex (max +2); heavy = the flat value with no Dex. A shield adds +2 on top (PHB p.144–145).

**Q: Does the heavy/splint/plate Strength requirement stop me from wearing it?**
A: No — you can still wear it, but if your Strength is below the listed score (13 or 15), your speed drops by 10 ft. (PHB p.144).

**Q: What does the finesse property let me do?**
A: Choose Strength or Dexterity for both the attack and damage rolls — you must use the same ability for both (PHB p.147). Common on daggers, rapiers, shortswords, scimitars, whips, and darts.

**Q: How much can my character carry, push, drag, or lift?**
A: Carry up to **Strength × 15** lb.; push, drag, or lift up to **Strength × 30** lb. (PHB p.176). Optional encumbrance rules add speed penalties at Str × 5 and Str × 10.

**Q: How do the coins convert?**
A: 10 cp = 1 sp, 5 sp = 1 ep, 10 sp = 1 gp, 10 gp = 1 pp (PHB p.143). Gold is the standard unit of value.

**Q: A weapon with loading and Extra Attack — can I fire it twice?**
A: No. The loading property limits you to one shot per action/bonus action/reaction regardless of how many attacks you'd normally get (PHB p.147).
