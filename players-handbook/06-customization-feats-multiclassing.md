# Customization Options: Multiclassing & Feats

Two optional rule sets for fine-tuning a character beyond race, class, and background: **multiclassing** (gaining levels in more than one class) and **feats** (special abilities taken in place of an Ability Score Improvement). Both require the DM's permission to be available in a campaign.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 6, "Customization Options" (printed pages 163–170).

---

## Multiclassing

Multiclassing lets you gain levels in more than one class, mixing their abilities to realize a character concept the standard classes don't cover. Whenever you advance in level, you may instead take your first level in a *new* class. Your levels in all classes are added together to set your **character level** (PHB p.163).

Compared with a single-class character of the same level, a multiclass character trades focus for versatility.

### Prerequisites

Multiclassing is an optional rule. To take a level in a new class — and to keep advancing in your current one once you've multiclassed — you must meet the ability score **prerequisite of both your current class and the new class** (PHB p.163). This represents the natural aptitude needed to pick up a new discipline without full training.

| Class | Ability Score Minimum |
|---|---|
| Barbarian | Strength 13 |
| Bard | Charisma 13 |
| Cleric | Wisdom 13 |
| Druid | Wisdom 13 |
| Fighter | Strength 13 **or** Dexterity 13 |
| Monk | Dexterity 13 **and** Wisdom 13 |
| Paladin | Strength 13 **and** Charisma 13 |
| Ranger | Dexterity 13 **and** Wisdom 13 |
| Rogue | Dexterity 13 |
| Sorcerer | Charisma 13 |
| Warlock | Charisma 13 |
| Wizard | Intelligence 13 |

### Experience points

The XP cost to gain a level is always based on your **total character level** (the Character Advancement table in Ch. 1), not your level in any single class. A cleric 6/fighter 1 must earn enough XP to reach 8th level before taking a second fighter level or a seventh cleric level (PHB p.163).

### Hit points and Hit Dice

You gain the hit points of your new class **as described for levels after 1st** (you only get a class's 1st-level hit points when you're a 1st-level character). Add the Hit Dice granted by all your classes into one pool; pool dice of the same type, and track different types separately. A paladin 5/fighter 5 has ten d10 Hit Dice; a paladin 5/cleric 5 has five d10 and five d8 (PHB p.163).

### Proficiency bonus

Your proficiency bonus is always based on your **total character level**, not your level in a single class. A fighter 3/rogue 2 uses a 5th-level character's +3 bonus (PHB p.163).

### Proficiencies gained when multiclassing

When you gain a level in a class **other than your first**, you receive only *some* of that class's starting proficiencies (PHB p.164). You do **not** get the class's starting equipment, saving-throw proficiencies, or full skill list.

| Class | Proficiencies Gained |
|---|---|
| Barbarian | Shields, simple weapons, martial weapons |
| Bard | Light armor, one skill of your choice, one musical instrument of your choice |
| Cleric | Light armor, medium armor, shields |
| Druid | Light armor, medium armor, shields (druids won't wear armor or use shields made of metal) |
| Fighter | Light armor, medium armor, shields, simple weapons, martial weapons |
| Monk | Simple weapons, shortswords |
| Paladin | Light armor, medium armor, shields, simple weapons, martial weapons |
| Ranger | Light armor, medium armor, shields, simple weapons, martial weapons, one skill from the class's skill list |
| Rogue | Light armor, one skill from the class's skill list, thieves' tools |
| Sorcerer | — (none) |
| Warlock | Light armor, simple weapons |
| Wizard | — (none) |

### Class features with special multiclassing rules

Most class features work normally, but four have extra rules (PHB p.164):

- **Channel Divinity.** Gaining this feature again from a second class lets you use any Channel Divinity effect from either class, but it does **not** grant extra *uses*. You get more uses only at the specific class levels that explicitly grant them (a cleric 6/paladin 4 has two uses per rest because of the cleric side).
- **Extra Attack.** Multiple sources of Extra Attack **do not stack**; you can't make more than two attacks with it unless a feature says so (only the fighter's higher-level version increases the count). The warlock invocation Thirsting Blade likewise gives no extra attacks if you already have Extra Attack.
- **Unarmored Defense.** If you already have this feature, you **can't** gain it again from another class.
- **Spellcasting.** See below.

### Spellcasting while multiclassing

Your spellcasting depends partly on your **combined** levels in spellcasting classes and partly on your **individual** levels. If you have the Spellcasting feature from only one class, you simply follow that class's rules. Once you have it from more than one class, use the rules below (PHB p.164).

**Spells known and prepared.** Determine the spells you know and can prepare for **each class separately**, exactly as if you were a single-classed member of that class. A ranger 4/wizard 3 knows ranger spells based on ranger level 4 and wizard spells based on wizard level 3. Each spell is tied to one class, and you use that class's spellcasting ability (and matching focus) when you cast it (PHB p.164).

**Spell slots.** Determine your slots from a single combined caster level, computed as (PHB p.164–165):

- **Full** (add full level): every level of **bard, cleric, druid, sorcerer, and wizard**.
- **Half** (add half, rounded down): your levels in **paladin and ranger**.
- **Third** (add a third, rounded down): your **fighter or rogue** levels **only if** you have the **Eldritch Knight** or **Arcane Trickster** subclass.

Use that total on the **Multiclass Spellcaster: Spell Slots per Level** table below. The table can grant slots of a *higher* level than any spell you actually know; you may use those slots to cast your lower-level spells (and to gain a spell's upcast effect, like *burning hands* at a higher level). Example: a ranger 4/wizard 3 counts as a **5th-level** caster — four 1st-, three 2nd-, and two 3rd-level slots — even though they know no 3rd-level (or 2nd-level ranger) spells (PHB p.164–165).

> **Note:** Use *this* combined table only for the **Spellcasting** feature. The **Pact Magic** slots a warlock gets are tracked separately on the warlock's own table.

## Feats

A feat represents a talent or area of expertise beyond what a class provides. At certain levels your class grants the **Ability Score Improvement** feature; under this **optional** rule you may **forgo that feature to take a feat instead** (PHB p.165).

- You can take each feat **only once**, unless its description says otherwise (Elemental Adept is repeatable).
- You must meet any **prerequisite** listed. If you later lose a prerequisite (e.g., Strength drops below 13), you can't use the feat until you regain it (PHB p.165).

In the list below, each feat notes its prerequisite (if any) and a concise mechanical summary. Feats that say "increase a score by 1, to a maximum of 20" raise that ability as part of taking the feat.

### Alert (PHB p.165)

+5 bonus to initiative; you can't be surprised while conscious; hidden creatures don't gain advantage on attack rolls against you.

### Athlete (PHB p.165)

+1 Str or Dex. Standing up from prone costs only 5 ft. of movement; climbing doesn't halve your speed; you can make running long/high jumps after moving only 5 ft.

### Actor (PHB p.165)

+1 Cha. Advantage on Deception/Performance checks to pass as another person; you can mimic the speech of a person or the sounds of a creature you've heard for at least 1 minute (a contested Insight vs. Deception check can detect the fake).

### Charger (PHB p.165)

When you take the **Dash** action, use a bonus action to make one melee attack or to shove. If you moved at least 10 ft. straight first, you either gain **+5 damage** (on a hit) or push the target up to 10 ft. (on a successful shove).

### Crossbow Expert (PHB p.165)

Ignore the **loading** quality of crossbows you're proficient with; being within 5 ft. of a hostile creature doesn't impose disadvantage on your ranged attacks; when you Attack with a one-handed weapon, you can use a bonus action to attack with a loaded hand crossbow you're holding.

### Defensive Duelist (PHB p.165)

*Prerequisite: Dexterity 13+.* While wielding a finesse weapon you're proficient with, use your **reaction** when hit by a melee attack to add your proficiency bonus to AC against that attack, possibly making it miss.

### Dual Wielder (PHB p.165)

+1 AC while wielding a separate melee weapon in each hand; you can two-weapon fight even with **non-light** one-handed weapons; you can draw/stow two one-handed weapons when you'd normally do one.

### Dungeon Delver (PHB p.166)

Advantage on Perception/Investigation checks to find secret doors and on saving throws to avoid/resist traps; resistance to trap damage; you can search for traps at a normal travel pace.

### Durable (PHB p.166)

+1 Con. When you spend a Hit Die to regain HP, the minimum you regain equals **twice your Constitution modifier** (minimum 2).

### Elemental Adept (PHB p.166)

*Prerequisite: the ability to cast at least one spell.* Choose acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder. Your spells **ignore resistance** to that type, and you treat any **1 on a damage die** for spells of that type as a **2**. Repeatable — choose a different type each time.

### Grappler (PHB p.166)

*Prerequisite: Strength 13+.* Advantage on attacks against a creature you're grappling; you can use your action to **pin** a grappled creature (a second grapple check leaves both of you **restrained** until the grapple ends); creatures one size larger than you don't automatically succeed on escape checks.

### Great Weapon Master (PHB p.166)

- On your turn, when you **score a critical hit** with a melee weapon **or reduce a creature to 0 HP** with one, make **one melee attack as a bonus action**.
- Before a melee attack with a **heavy** weapon you're proficient with, you can take a **−5 penalty to the attack roll** for **+10 damage** on a hit.

### Healer (PHB p.166)

When you use a **healer's kit** to stabilize a dying creature, it also regains 1 HP. As an action, spend one use of a healer's kit to restore **1d6 + 4 HP** plus the creature's **maximum number of Hit Dice**; that creature can't be healed by this feat again until it finishes a short or long rest.

### Heavily Armored (PHB p.166)

*Prerequisite: proficiency with medium armor.* +1 Str; gain proficiency with **heavy armor**.

### Heavy Armor Master (PHB p.166)

*Prerequisite: proficiency with heavy armor.* +1 Str; while wearing heavy armor, bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from **nonmagical weapons** is reduced by **3**.

### Inspiring Leader (PHB p.167)

*Prerequisite: Charisma 13+.* Spend 10 minutes to inspire up to six friendly creatures within 30 ft. (can include you) who can see/hear and understand you. Each gains **temporary HP equal to your level + your Charisma modifier**; a creature can't benefit again until it finishes a short or long rest.

### Keen Mind (PHB p.167)

+1 Int. You always know which way is north and how many hours remain until the next sunrise/sunset, and you can accurately recall anything seen or heard within the past **month**.

### Lightly Armored (PHB p.167)

+1 Str or Dex; gain proficiency with **light armor**.

### Linguist (PHB p.167)

+1 Int; learn three languages; you can create written ciphers others can't decipher without your teaching, an Intelligence check (DC = your Int score + your proficiency bonus), or magic.

### Lucky (PHB p.167)

You have **3 luck points** (regained on a long rest). Spend one when you make an **attack roll, ability check, or saving throw** to roll an extra d20 and choose which die to use (you may decide after rolling but before the outcome is known). You may also spend one when an attack is rolled **against you** — roll a d20 and choose whether the attack uses the attacker's roll or yours. If two creatures both spend luck points on the same roll, the points cancel and no extra dice are rolled.

### Mage Slayer (PHB p.167)

When a creature within 5 ft. casts a spell, use your reaction to make a melee attack against it; when you damage a creature concentrating on a spell, it has **disadvantage** on the saving throw to maintain concentration; advantage on saves against spells cast by creatures within 5 ft. of you.

### Magic Initiate (PHB p.167)

Choose bard, cleric, druid, sorcerer, warlock, or wizard. Learn **two cantrips** from that list and **one 1st-level spell**, which you can cast at 1st level **once per long rest** (it can also be cast with any spell slots you have). Spellcasting ability follows the chosen class (Cha for bard/sorcerer/warlock, Wis for cleric/druid, Int for wizard).

### Martial Adept (PHB p.167)

Learn **two maneuvers** from the **Battle Master** archetype (maneuver save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Str or Dex modifier, your choice). Gain **one superiority die** (a **d6**), or one additional die if you already have them; recover expended dice on a short or long rest.

### Medium Armor Master (PHB p.167)

*Prerequisite: proficiency with medium armor.* Wearing medium armor doesn't impose disadvantage on Stealth; and you can add **+3** (instead of +2) Dexterity to AC in medium armor if your Dexterity is **16 or higher**.

### Mobile (PHB p.167)

- Your speed increases by **10 ft.**
- When you **Dash**, difficult terrain doesn't cost extra movement that turn.
- When you make a melee attack against a creature, you **don't provoke opportunity attacks from that creature** for the rest of the turn, whether you hit or not.

### Moderately Armored (PHB p.167)

*Prerequisite: proficiency with light armor.* +1 Str or Dex; gain proficiency with **medium armor and shields**.

### Mounted Combatant (PHB p.167)

While mounted and not incapacitated: advantage on melee attacks against any unmounted creature smaller than your mount; you can force an attack aimed at your mount to target you instead; and an effect that lets your mount make a Dex save for half damage deals it **no** damage on a success (half on a failure).

### Observant (PHB p.168)

+1 Int or Wis. If you can see a speaking creature's mouth and understand its language, you can **read lips**; and you gain a **+5 bonus** to passive Perception and passive Investigation.

### Polearm Master (PHB p.168)

- When you take the Attack action and attack with **only** a glaive, halberd, or **quarterstaff**, use a bonus action to attack with the **opposite end**: a **d4 bludgeoning** attack (using the same ability modifier as the weapon).
- While wielding a glaive, halberd, **pike**, or quarterstaff, creatures **provoke an opportunity attack from you when they enter your reach**.

### Resilient (PHB p.168)

Choose one ability: **+1 to that score** and gain **saving throw proficiency** with it.

### Ritual Caster (PHB p.168)

*Prerequisite: Intelligence or Wisdom 13+.* Gain a **ritual book** with two 1st-level spells (with the ritual tag) from a chosen class (bard/cleric/druid/sorcerer/warlock/wizard); you can cast them as rituals only, holding the book. You can copy additional ritual spells from the chosen class's list (spell level ≤ half your level rounded up) into the book; copying costs **2 hours and 50 gp per spell level**. Spellcasting ability follows the chosen class.

### Savage Attacker (PHB p.168)

Once per turn, when you roll damage for a **melee weapon attack**, you can reroll the weapon's damage dice and use either total.

### Sentinel (PHB p.168)

- When you hit a creature with an **opportunity attack**, its speed becomes **0** for the rest of the turn.
- Creatures within 5 ft. provoke opportunity attacks from you **even if they Disengage** before leaving your reach.
- When a creature within 5 ft. attacks a target **other than you** (and that target lacks this feat), use your reaction to make a melee attack against the attacker.

### Sharpshooter (PHB p.170)

- Attacking at **long range** doesn't impose disadvantage on your ranged weapon attacks.
- Your ranged weapon attacks **ignore half cover and three-quarters cover**.
- Before a ranged attack with a weapon you're proficient with, take a **−5 to the attack roll** for **+10 damage** on a hit.

### Shield Master (PHB p.170)

While wielding a shield: if you take the Attack action, use a bonus action to **shove** a creature within 5 ft.; add the shield's AC bonus to Dexterity saves against effects targeting only you; and on an effect that allows a Dex save for half damage, use your reaction to take **no damage** on a success.

### Skilled (PHB p.170)

Gain proficiency in any combination of **three** skills or tools of your choice.

### Skulker (PHB p.170)

*Prerequisite: Dexterity 13+.* You can try to **hide** when only lightly obscured from the creature you're hiding from; **missing** a ranged weapon attack while hidden doesn't reveal your position; and dim light doesn't impose disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight.

### Spell Sniper (PHB p.170)

*Prerequisite: the ability to cast at least one spell.* Spells requiring an **attack roll** have their **range doubled**; your **ranged spell attacks** ignore half and three-quarters cover; and learn one **attack-roll cantrip** from any class's list (ability per chosen class).

### Tavern Brawler (PHB p.170)

+1 Str or Con; proficiency with **improvised weapons and unarmed strikes**; your unarmed strike deals **1d4** damage; and when you hit with an unarmed strike or improvised weapon on your turn, use a bonus action to attempt to **grapple** the target.

### Tough (PHB p.170)

Your **hit point maximum increases by twice your level** when you take the feat, and by an additional **+2 each level** thereafter (effectively **+2 HP per character level**).

### War Caster (PHB p.170)

*Prerequisite: the ability to cast at least one spell.*

- **Advantage on Constitution saving throws** to maintain concentration when you take damage.
- You can perform **somatic components** even with weapons or a shield in one or both hands.
- When a hostile creature's movement provokes an **opportunity attack** from you, you can instead **cast a spell** at it (the spell must have a casting time of 1 action and target only that creature).

### Weapon Master (PHB p.170)

+1 Str or Dex; gain proficiency with **four weapons** of your choice.

## Common questions

**Q: Do I need 13 in an ability to multiclass, or 15?**
A: 13. You need a 13+ in the listed ability (or abilities) of **both** the class you're leaving and the class you're entering (PHB p.163). Many classes have just one prerequisite, but Monk, Paladin, and Ranger require two, and Fighter accepts either Str **or** Dex.

**Q: When I take a level in a new class, do I get all its starting equipment and saving throws?**
A: No. You get only the limited **proficiencies** listed in the Multiclassing Proficiencies table — no starting equipment, no saving-throw proficiencies, and not the full skill selection (PHB p.164).

**Q: How do I count caster level for spell slots if I'm, say, a paladin 6/sorcerer 4?**
A: Full level for sorcerer (4) plus half (rounded down) for paladin (6 → 3) = **7th-level caster** on the Multiclass Spellcaster table. Fighter/rogue levels count only at a third, and only with the Eldritch Knight or Arcane Trickster subclass (PHB p.164–165).

**Q: Can I cast a 3rd-level spell if the multiclass table gives me a 3rd-level slot but I only know 1st- and 2nd-level spells?**
A: You can use the higher-level slot, but only to cast spells you actually know — typically as an upcast of a lower-level spell. The slot doesn't grant you spells you don't know (PHB p.164).

**Q: How does Great Weapon Master's bonus-attack trigger work?**
A: On your turn, scoring a **critical hit** with a melee weapon **or** dropping a creature to **0 HP** with a melee weapon lets you make one melee attack as a bonus action. The −5/+10 part is separate and only requires a heavy weapon you're proficient with (PHB p.166).

**Q: Does War Caster let me cast any spell as an opportunity attack?**
A: Only a spell with a casting time of **1 action** that targets **only that one creature**. It replaces the opportunity attack; it isn't an extra free spell (PHB p.170).

**Q: Can I take a feat at 1st level?**
A: Not normally. Feats replace an **Ability Score Improvement**, and your first ASI comes at a later class level (most classes at 4th). The Variant Human race is the usual exception (PHB p.165).

**Q: Does taking a feat count against my ability score caps?**
A: Feats that raise a score say "to a maximum of 20," so they obey the normal 20 cap unless a special feature raises it (PHB p.165).
