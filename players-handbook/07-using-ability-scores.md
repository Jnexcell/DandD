# Using Ability Scores

How the six abilities work and how to resolve almost any non-attack roll: ability modifiers, the proficiency bonus, ability checks against a DC, advantage/disadvantage, passive checks, the 18 skills, working together, contests, and saving throws.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 7, "Using Ability Scores" (printed pages 173–179).

---

## The six abilities

Every creature is described by six ability scores. Each governs a broad set of tasks (PHB p.173):

| Ability | Measures | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| **Strength** | Physical power | Lifting, pushing, breaking, melee weapon attacks, Athletics |
| **Dexterity** | Agility, reflexes, balance | Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth, AC, initiative, ranged/finesse attacks |
| **Constitution** | Endurance, health | Hit points, holding breath, concentration saves (no associated skill) |
| **Intelligence** | Reasoning and memory | Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion |
| **Wisdom** | Perception and insight | Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival |
| **Charisma** | Force of personality | Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion |

A score of **10–11** is the human average. **18** is about the highest a person normally reaches; adventurers can reach **20**, and monsters and divine beings can go as high as **30** (PHB p.173).

### Ability modifiers (PHB p.173)

Every score has a **modifier** that you add to d20 rolls. To find it without the table: **(score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down**.

| Score | Mod | Score | Mod |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | −5 | 16–17 | +3 |
| 2–3 | −4 | 18–19 | +4 |
| 4–5 | −3 | 20–21 | +5 |
| 6–7 | −2 | 22–23 | +6 |
| 8–9 | −1 | 24–25 | +7 |
| 10–11 | +0 | 26–27 | +8 |
| 12–13 | +1 | 28–29 | +9 |
| 14–15 | +2 | 30 | +10 |

### Proficiency bonus (PHB p.173)

Your **proficiency bonus** is set by your level (it starts at **+2** at 1st level; monsters carry it in their stat block). You add it to ability checks, saving throws, and attack rolls when you're **proficient** with the relevant skill, tool, save, or weapon.

Key rules:
- You can never add it to a single roll **more than once**, even if two features say you can (PHB p.173).
- Some features **multiply** it (e.g., a rogue's Expertise doubles it for certain checks), but you still apply it only once and multiply only once.
- If a feature multiplies your bonus for a check you're **not** proficient in, the bonus is effectively **0** (multiplying 0 changes nothing).

## Ability checks

When a character attempts an action (other than an attack) that has a chance of failure and an uncertain outcome, the DM calls for an **ability check**. Roll **d20 + the relevant ability modifier** (+ proficiency bonus if proficient in an applicable skill), and compare to a **Difficulty Class (DC)**. Equal or higher = success (PHB p.174).

### Typical Difficulty Classes (PHB p.174)

| Task difficulty | DC |
|---|---|
| Very easy | 5 |
| Easy | 10 |
| Medium | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Very hard | 25 |
| Nearly impossible | 30 |

The DM decides which of the six abilities applies and how hard the task is. On a failure you make no progress, or progress with a setback the DM determines.

## Advantage and disadvantage

When something grants **advantage** or imposes **disadvantage**, roll **2d20**: keep the **higher** for advantage, the **lower** for disadvantage (PHB p.173).

- **Don't stack.** Multiple sources of advantage (or disadvantage) still mean only **one** extra d20 — you never roll more than 2d20.
- **They cancel.** If a roll has both advantage and disadvantage, you have **neither** and roll a single d20 — even if several sources push one way and only one pushes the other.
- **Rerolls.** If you also have an effect that lets you reroll a d20 (like a halfling's Lucky), you reroll only **one** of the two dice (your choice).

## Passive checks

A **passive check** is an ability check with no die roll — useful for repeated tasks or for the DM to secretly check (e.g., noticing a hidden monster). The total (called a **score**) is (PHB p.175):

> **10 + all modifiers that normally apply** to the check.

If the character would have **advantage**, add **+5**; for **disadvantage**, subtract **5**. Example: a 1st-level character with Wisdom 15 (+2) and proficiency in Perception (+2) has a passive Wisdom (Perception) score of **14**.

## The skills (by ability)

A skill represents a specific facet of an ability; proficiency in it lets you add your proficiency bonus to checks that use it. There are **18** skills (no skill is tied to Constitution) (PHB p.174).

| Ability | Skills |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Athletics |
| **Dexterity** | Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth |
| **Intelligence** | Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion |
| **Wisdom** | Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival |
| **Charisma** | Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion |

The DM may ask for a check using an **unusual ability + skill** combination — e.g., a Constitution (Athletics) check to swim a long distance, or a Strength (Intimidation) check to menace someone with raw power. If you're proficient in the named skill, you add your bonus regardless of the ability used (PHB p.175, Variant: Skills with Different Abilities).

## Working together, and group checks

**Working together (PHB p.175):** When several characters team up on a task, the one leading the effort (or with the highest modifier) makes the check **with advantage**. A character can help only if the task is one they could attempt alone (you can't help pick a lock without thieves' tools proficiency), and only when extra hands would actually help — some tasks (like threading a needle) aren't easier with help. In combat, this is the **Help** action.

**Group checks (PHB p.175):** When the whole party attempts something together (e.g., sneaking through a swamp), everyone rolls. **If at least half the group succeeds, the whole group succeeds.** Otherwise the group fails. The skilled members effectively carry those who aren't.

## Contests

When two creatures directly oppose each other (both grabbing the same ring; one forcing a door open while another holds it shut), the outcome is a **contest** (PHB p.174). Both make appropriate ability checks with all their bonuses and penalties, then **compare totals** instead of comparing to a DC. The higher total wins. On a **tie**, the situation stays as it was — so a defender can win a contest by default (the door stays shut; nobody grabs the ring).

---

## Saving throws

A **saving throw** (or "save") represents resisting a spell, trap, poison, disease, or similar threat. You don't choose to make one — you're forced to because you're at risk. Roll **d20 + the relevant ability modifier**, and add your **proficiency bonus if you're proficient** in that save (PHB p.175).

- You use the matching ability — e.g., your Dexterity modifier for a Dexterity save.
- Each class grants proficiency in **at least two** saving throws (wizards, for instance, are proficient in Intelligence saves).
- The **DC** is set by whatever causes the save. For a spell, it's **8 + the caster's spellcasting ability modifier + their proficiency bonus**.
- A successful save usually means **no harm or reduced harm** from the effect.

## Common questions

**Q: Do I add my proficiency bonus to every check I make with a stat I'm good at?**
A: No. You add it only when you're **proficient** in a skill (or tool/save) that applies. Without proficiency it's just d20 + ability modifier (PHB p.174).

**Q: Two effects give me advantage and one gives me disadvantage. What do I roll?**
A: A single d20. Any combination of advantage and disadvantage on the same roll cancels to neither — it doesn't matter how many of each you have (PHB p.173).

**Q: What's the difference between a "skill check" and an "ability check"?**
A: There's no separate skill check — it's always an ability check. A skill just lets you add your proficiency bonus to certain ability checks (e.g., a Dexterity (Stealth) check is a Dexterity check you're proficient in via Stealth) (PHB p.174).

**Q: How is a spell's save DC calculated?**
A: 8 + the caster's proficiency bonus + their spellcasting ability modifier (PHB p.175).

**Q: In a group check, how many of us need to succeed?**
A: At least **half** the group. If half or more succeed, everyone succeeds (PHB p.175).

**Q: Can I ask to use a skill with a different ability than usual?**
A: You can ask, and the DM may allow it (e.g., Constitution (Athletics) for a long swim, Strength (Intimidation) for a show of force). If you're proficient in the skill, you still add your bonus (PHB p.175).
