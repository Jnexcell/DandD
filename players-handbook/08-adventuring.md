# Adventuring

The rules that cover life between fights: tracking time, movement and travel pace, **activity while traveling** (marching order, noticing threats, navigate/map/track/forage), difficult terrain, special movement (climbing/swimming/jumping/crawling), environmental hazards (falling, suffocation, vision and light), food and water, resting (short and long rests with Hit Dice), and **between adventures** (lifestyle & downtime).

**Source:** PHB Chapter 8, "Adventuring" (printed pages 181–188).

---

## Time

The DM picks a time scale to fit the situation (PHB p.181):

- **Rounds** (about 6 seconds each) in combat and other fast-paced moments.
- **Minutes** for dungeon exploration (creeping down a hall, checking a door for traps, searching a chamber).
- **Hours** for travel through a city or wilderness.
- **Days** for long overland journeys.

## Movement and travel pace

Every creature has a **speed** — the distance in feet it can walk in 1 round. From that, the rules derive how far a party covers over longer spans (PHB p.181–182).

### Travel Pace (PHB p.182)

| Pace | Per minute | Per hour | Per day | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fast** | 400 ft. | 4 miles | 30 miles | −5 penalty to passive Wisdom (Perception) |
| **Normal** | 300 ft. | 3 miles | 24 miles | — |
| **Slow** | 200 ft. | 2 miles | 18 miles | Able to use stealth |

The table assumes about **8 hours** of travel per day.

- **Forced march.** Beyond 8 hours, for each extra hour the party covers the "per hour" distance, and each character makes a **Constitution save** at the hour's end. The DC is **10 + 1 per hour past 8**. On a failure, the character gains one level of **exhaustion** (PHB p.181).
- **Mounts and vehicles.** A mount can gallop for about an hour at **twice** the fast-pace distance. Mounted/vehicle travel otherwise uses a chosen pace. Waterborne travel is limited to the vessel's speed (no fast-pace penalty or slow-pace benefit), and ships may travel up to 24 hours/day (PHB p.181–182).

### Difficult terrain (PHB p.182)

In difficult terrain (dense forest, deep swamp, rubble, steep mountains, ice), every foot of movement costs **2 feet** — you move at **half speed**, covering half the normal distance in a minute, hour, or day. This is true even if several things in a space count as difficult terrain (they don't add up further).

## Activity while traveling (PHB p.182–183)

While the party travels, they stay alert for danger and some characters can take on a travel job. This is the bundle of rules that actually governs an exploration scene.

### Marching order (PHB p.182)

Set a **marching order** — front rank, one or more middle ranks, and a back rank. It decides **who triggers traps, who can spot hidden enemies, and who's closest when a fight breaks out**. Front and back ranks need room to travel side by side; when space is tight, the order collapses (move people to a middle rank — in a narrow tunnel, single file). With only two ranks they're front + back; with one rank, it counts as the front.

### Noticing threats (PHB p.182–183)

The DM uses the characters' **passive Wisdom (Perception)** scores to see who notices a hidden threat — and may rule that only a particular **rank** has a chance (e.g., only the back rank can hear something tailing the group). Modifiers:

- **Fast pace:** **−5** to passive Perception (easier to ambush).
- **Slow pace:** the party can move **stealthily** (if not in the open) to surprise or sneak past creatures — use the hiding rules (Ch.7).

### Travel jobs — one focused task instead of watching (PHB p.183)

A character who turns to a task **stops contributing their passive Perception** to the group's chance of noticing danger. In exchange they can do **one** of these (or another the DM allows):

| Job | Check | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Navigate** | Wis (Survival) when the DM calls | Try to keep the group from getting **lost** (the lost rules are in the DMG). |
| **Draw a Map** | none | Record the route; helps the party get **back on course** if they get lost. |
| **Track** | Wis (Survival) when the DM calls | Follow another creature's tracks (DMG has the tracking DCs). |
| **Forage** | Wis (Survival) when the DM calls | Watch for food and water on the move (DMG has the foraging rules). |

> **Trade-off to remember:** every traveler doing a job is one fewer set of eyes — a party that maps, navigates, *and* forages is wide open to a surprise round. Decide who's watching.

## Special types of movement

### Climbing, swimming, and crawling (PHB p.182)

Each foot of climbing or swimming costs **1 extra foot** (2 extra in difficult terrain), unless you have a climbing or swimming speed. At the DM's option, a slippery or handhold-poor surface, or rough water, may require a successful **Strength (Athletics)** check.

### Jumping (PHB p.182)

Your **Strength** determines how far you jump.

- **Long jump:** with at least a **10-foot running start**, you cover a number of feet up to your **Strength score**. A standing long jump covers **half** that. Each foot jumped costs a foot of movement. (Optionally, a DC 10 Strength (Athletics) check is needed to clear a low obstacle no taller than a quarter of the jump; landing in difficult terrain requires a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or you land prone.)
- **High jump:** with a 10-foot running start, you leap **3 + your Strength modifier** feet up. A standing high jump is **half** that. You can reach up an additional **1½ times your height** above yourself.

### Crawling and being prone

Moving while prone means crawling, which costs **1 extra foot per foot** (see Chapter 9 for prone in combat). Standing up costs half your speed.

## The environment

### Falling (PHB p.183)

At the end of a fall, a creature takes **1d6 bludgeoning damage per 10 feet fallen**, to a **maximum of 20d6**. It lands **prone** unless it avoided the damage entirely.

### Suffocating (PHB p.183)

A creature can hold its breath for **1 + its Constitution modifier minutes** (minimum 30 seconds). When out of breath, it can survive a number of **rounds equal to its Constitution modifier** (minimum 1). At the start of its next turn after that, it drops to **0 hit points and is dying**.

> Example: Constitution 14 (+2) → holds breath 3 minutes, then has 2 rounds before dropping to 0.

### Vision and light (PHB p.183)

Obscurement and light determine what you can see.

- **Lightly obscured** (dim light, patchy fog, light foliage): creatures have **disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight**.
- **Heavily obscured** (darkness, opaque fog, dense foliage): vision is blocked entirely; a creature there is effectively **blinded** when trying to see into or through it.

Three levels of illumination:
- **Bright light** — most creatures see normally.
- **Dim light** (shadows) — creates a **lightly obscured** area; the band between bright light and darkness, plus twilight/dawn/bright moonlight.
- **Darkness** — creates a **heavily obscured** area; unlit interiors, moonless nights, magical darkness.

Special senses:
- **Blindsight** — perceive surroundings without sight within a radius (oozes, echolocators like bats and true dragons).
- **Darkvision** — within range, see in darkness as if it were dim light (so darkness is only lightly obscured for them), but only in **shades of gray** (no color).
- **Truesight** — within range, see in normal and magical darkness, see invisible creatures/objects, automatically detect and save against visual illusions, perceive a shapechanger's true form, and see into the Ethereal Plane.

### Food and water (PHB p.185)

Going without enough food or water causes **exhaustion** that can't be removed until the character eats and drinks the full required amount (PHB p.185).

- **Food:** 1 pound per day. Half rations count as half a day without food. A character can go **3 + Constitution modifier days** (minimum 1) without food before suffering one level of exhaustion at the end of each further day. One normal day of eating resets the count.
- **Water:** 1 gallon per day (2 if hot). Drinking only half that requires a **DC 15 Constitution save** or one level of exhaustion at day's end; less than that means automatic exhaustion. If the character already has exhaustion, water deprivation costs **two** levels instead of one.

## Resting

Adventurers recover by resting — short rests during the day and a long rest to end it (PHB p.186).

### Short rest (PHB p.186)

A **short rest** is at least **1 hour** of doing nothing more strenuous than eating, drinking, reading, and tending wounds.

- You may spend one or more **Hit Dice** (up to your level's worth). For each die spent, **roll it and add your Constitution modifier**; you regain that many hit points. You decide whether to spend another die after each roll.
- You recover some spent Hit Dice on a long rest.

### Long rest (PHB p.186)

A **long rest** is at least **8 hours** of sleep or light activity (reading, talking, eating, standing watch — no more than 2 hours of watch).

- If interrupted by **1+ hour of strenuous activity** (walking, fighting, casting spells, etc.), you must **start the rest over** to gain any benefit.
- At the end, you **regain all lost hit points** and recover spent **Hit Dice up to half your total** (rounded down). Example: a character with 8 Hit Dice regains **4** on a long rest.
- You **can't benefit from more than one long rest in a 24-hour period**, and you must have **at least 1 hit point** at the start of the rest to gain its benefits.

## Between adventures (PHB p.187)

When time passes between adventures, two player-facing things matter — both already detailed elsewhere in the KB, so this is a pointer:

- **Lifestyle expenses (PHB p.157).** Between adventures you pay an ongoing cost to live, picking a band from **Wretched** (free, dangerous) up to **Aristocratic** (≥10 gp/day). Your lifestyle colors the contacts you make and the trouble you attract. → Full table in [`05-equipment.md`](05-equipment.md#lifestyle-expenses-phb-p157158).
- **Downtime activities (PHB p.187).** Each day spent on a downtime activity needs at least **8 hours** (days needn't be consecutive). The five basic PHB activities are **Crafting · Practicing a Profession · Recuperating · Researching · Training**. → Each one's time/cost/check/outcome is summarized in [`../dungeon-masters-guide/06-between-adventures.md`](../dungeon-masters-guide/06-between-adventures.md#downtime-activities-dmg-p127131), which also lists the DMG's expanded optional set.

For a one-shot this rarely comes up; it matters most if you stitch the session into a short campaign (the MMOS **Hook** — see [`../quick-reference/one-shot-formula-mmos.md`](../quick-reference/one-shot-formula-mmos.md)).

## Common questions

**Q: How far can my character jump?**
A: Long jump = your Strength **score** in feet with a 10-ft. running start (half that standing). High jump = **3 + your Strength modifier** feet up with a running start (half standing) (PHB p.182).

**Q: How much falling damage, and is there a cap?**
A: 1d6 bludgeoning per 10 feet fallen, capped at **20d6** (200 feet). You also land prone unless you took no damage (PHB p.183).

**Q: How many hit points does a long rest restore?**
A: All of them. You also get back Hit Dice up to **half** your total (PHB p.186).

**Q: Can I take two long rests in one day to fully refill Hit Dice?**
A: No. You can benefit from only one long rest per 24-hour period (PHB p.186).

**Q: How long can a character hold their breath before drowning?**
A: 1 + Constitution modifier minutes (minimum 30 seconds). After that, Constitution-modifier rounds (minimum 1), then you drop to 0 HP and start dying (PHB p.183).

**Q: Does darkvision let me see normally in the dark?**
A: It treats darkness as dim light within its range, so the area is only lightly obscured for you — but you see only in shades of gray, not color. Dim light still imposes no special penalty beyond what light obscurement already does (PHB p.183).

**Q: What does traveling at a fast pace cost me?**
A: A −5 penalty to your passive Perception, making you easier to surprise. A slow pace lets the party use stealth while moving (PHB p.182).

**Q: Can my character map (or forage, or navigate) AND keep watch?**
A: No — a character focused on a travel job **doesn't add their passive Perception** to the group's chance of noticing danger. Each traveler does one thing; spread the jobs around and keep someone watching (PHB p.183).

**Q: Why bother setting a marching order?**
A: It decides who hits the trap, who might spot the ambush, and who's in reach when a fight starts — and the DM can rule that only a given rank gets a chance to notice a particular threat. In tight spaces it collapses toward single file (PHB p.182).

**Q: Who makes the check to not get lost / to track / to forage?**
A: All three are **Wisdom (Survival)** checks made by whoever took that job, when the DM calls for it; *Draw a Map* needs no check. The actual lost/tracking/foraging DCs live in the DMG (PHB p.183).
