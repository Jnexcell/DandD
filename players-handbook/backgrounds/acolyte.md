# Acolyte

A devoted servant of a temple — an intermediary between the divine and the mortal world who has spent their life performing sacred rites, tending shrines, and ministering to worshipers.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 127).

---

## Flavor & lore

You have given your life to the service of a temple, devoted to a specific god or to a whole pantheon. Your role is to stand between the holy and the mundane: leading rites, offering sacrifices, and guiding the faithful into the presence of the divine. Importantly, being an acolyte is **not** the same as being a cleric — performing sacred ceremonies is a matter of training and devotion, not channeled divine power. Plenty of acolytes never cast a spell in their lives.

Your past can take many shapes. Maybe you were a child raised inside temple walls to assist the priests, or a high priest who heard a call to serve in some new way. You might have led a small cult outside any established temple, or even an occult circle bound to a darker, quasi-divine master you have since renounced. Work with your DM to pin down which god, pantheon, or being you served (the gods in appendix B are a starting point) and what your service actually looked like.

This background suits a faithful, study-shaped character — comfortable with ritual, hierarchy, and the tenets of a religion — and pairs naturally with clerics and paladins but works just as well as a faith-grounded layer over any class.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Insight, Religion
- **Tool Proficiencies:** None
- **Languages:** Two of your choice
- **Equipment:** A holy symbol (a gift to you when you entered the priesthood), a prayer book or prayer wheel, 5 sticks of incense, vestments, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing **15 gp**.

---

## Feature: Shelter of the Faithful

As an acolyte you command the respect of those who share your faith, and you can perform your deity's religious ceremonies. You and your adventuring companions can expect **free healing and care** at any temple, shrine, or other established presence of your faith — though you still have to supply any material components a spell requires. Beyond that, fellow believers will support **you** (only you, not your companions) at a **modest lifestyle**.

You may also be tied to a particular temple of your deity and have a residence there — perhaps the temple where you once served, if you parted on good terms, or a new spiritual home. While near that temple you can ask its priests for assistance, so long as what you ask is **not hazardous** and you remain in good standing with them.

## Variants / specialties

The Acolyte has **no specialty or variant sub-table** in the PHB. You customize it through your choice of deity/pantheon and the suggested characteristics below.

## Suggested characteristics

Acolytes are shaped by their time in temples and religious communities. Their study of doctrine and their ties to shrines and hierarchies color their manners and ideals; their flaws often spring from a hidden hypocrisy, a heretical idea, or a virtue taken to extremes.

### Personality Traits (d8)

| d8 | Trait |
|----|-------|
| 1 | I idolize a particular hero of my faith, and constantly refer to that person's deeds and example. |
| 2 | I can find common ground between the fiercest enemies, empathizing with them and always working toward peace. |
| 3 | I see omens in every event and action. The gods try to speak to us, we just need to listen. |
| 4 | Nothing can shake my optimistic attitude. |
| 5 | I quote (or misquote) sacred texts and proverbs in almost every situation. |
| 6 | I am tolerant (or intolerant) of other faiths and respect (or condemn) the worship of other gods. |
| 7 | I've enjoyed fine food, drink, and high society among my temple's elite. Rough living grates on me. |
| 8 | I've spent so long in the temple that I have little practical experience dealing with people in the outside world. |

### Ideals (d6)

| d6 | Ideal |
|----|-------|
| 1 | **Tradition.** The ancient traditions of worship and sacrifice must be preserved and upheld. (Lawful) |
| 2 | **Charity.** I always try to help those in need, no matter what the personal cost. (Good) |
| 3 | **Change.** We must help bring about the changes the gods are constantly working in the world. (Chaotic) |
| 4 | **Power.** I hope to one day rise to the top of my faith's religious hierarchy. (Lawful) |
| 5 | **Faith.** I trust that my deity will guide my actions, I have faith that if I work hard, things will go well. (Lawful) |

## Common questions

**Q: Does Shelter of the Faithful give my whole party free upkeep?**
A: No. The **free healing and care at a temple/shrine** of your faith covers you and your companions (you still pay for spell material components). But the ongoing **modest-lifestyle support** from fellow believers is for **you alone** — your companions are on their own for living expenses. (PHB p.127)

**Q: Can I lean on my home temple for anything?**
A: Only while near it, only if you're in good standing, and only for **non-hazardous** help. You can't march the temple priests into a dungeon — but information, sanctuary, introductions, and routine aid are fair game. (PHB p.127)

**Q: Do I have to be a cleric (or even a caster) to be an Acolyte?**
A: No. The background explicitly notes that performing sacred rites is not the same as channeling divine power — an Acolyte can be any class. It defines your past and social standing, not your spellcasting. (PHB p.127)
