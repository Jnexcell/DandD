# Charlatan

A practiced confidence trickster — a reader of people who sells miracle cures, fixed games, forged papers, and bridges that just happen to be for sale, and makes every implausible promise sound like the real deal.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 128).

---

## Flavor & lore

You have always had a way with people. A few minutes of conversation and a couple of leading questions, and you can read someone like a children's book — you know what makes them tick and can tease out their heart's desire. It's a useful talent, and you have never been shy about turning it to your own profit.

Your trade is giving people what they want, or at least *promising* to. Common sense ought to steer folks away from things that sound too good to be true, but common sense seems to run short whenever you're in the room. That pink-colored bottle will surely cure the rash; this ointment — really just fat and a sprinkle of silver dust — restores youth and vigor; and there's a fine bridge in the city up for sale to the right buyer. The marvels are implausible, but you make them sound genuine.

This background suits a smooth, sociable, self-interested character who fights with words and wits — a natural fit for charisma-based classes (bard, sorcerer, warlock, rogue), and a colorful mask over any class that wants a silver tongue and a shady past.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Deception, Sleight of Hand
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit, forgery kit
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** A set of fine clothes, a disguise kit, **tools of the con of your choice** (ten stoppered bottles filled with colored liquid, a set of weighted dice, a deck of marked cards, or a signet ring of an imaginary duke), and a belt pouch containing **15 gp**.

---

## Feature: False Identity

You have built a complete second identity — documentation, established acquaintances, and disguises — that lets you step into that persona whenever you like. On top of that, you can **forge documents**, including official papers and personal letters, provided you've seen an example of the kind of document, or a sample of the handwriting, you're trying to reproduce.

Charlatans are colorful characters who hide their true selves behind the masks they construct. They reflect what people want to see, what they want to believe, and how they see the world — but underneath, they're sometimes plagued by an uneasy conscience, an old enemy, or deep-seated trust issues.

## Common questions

**Q: What exactly can I forge with False Identity?**
A: Official documents and personal letters — but only if you've **seen an example** of that document type or the handwriting you're copying. No sample, no forgery. The feature also maintains a fully fleshed-out second persona (papers, acquaintances, disguises) you can slip into. (PHB p.128)

**Q: Do I pick all the "tools of the con," or just one set?**
A: Just **one** of the listed options — colored-liquid bottles, weighted dice, marked cards, or the fake duke's signet ring — your choice. (PHB p.128)
