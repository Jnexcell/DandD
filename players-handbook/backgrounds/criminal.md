# Criminal

A seasoned lawbreaker with a history in the underworld — still wired into the network of thieves, smugglers, and killers that runs beneath civilized society, and still alive because they know how to flout the rules.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 129).

---

## Flavor & lore

You are an experienced criminal with a track record of breaking the law. You've spent plenty of time among other criminals and still keep contacts in the underworld. You live far closer than most people to the world of murder, theft, and violence that pervades the underbelly of civilization — and you've survived this long precisely by ignoring society's rules and regulations.

What that meant day to day is up to you and your DM: a guild burglar, a freelance smuggler, a leg-breaker for hire, a pickpocket working the markets. Whatever the role, the criminal underworld is the water you swim in, and its connections are still good.

This background suits a streetwise, morally flexible character who's at home in the shadows — a natural fit for rogues, but a strong layer for any class that wants underworld ties, larcenous skills, or a checkered past to atone for.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Deception, Stealth
- **Tool Proficiencies:** One type of gaming set, thieves' tools
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** A crowbar, a set of dark common clothes including a hood, and a belt pouch containing **15 gp**.

---

## Feature: Criminal Contact

You have a reliable, trustworthy contact who serves as your liaison to a wider network of criminals. You know how to get messages to and from this contact even across great distances — specifically, you know the local messengers, corrupt caravan masters, and seedy sailors who can carry word for you.

### Variant Criminal: Spy

Your capabilities aren't much different from a burglar's or smuggler's, but you learned and practiced them in a very different context — as an **espionage agent**. You might have been an officially sanctioned agent of the crown, or perhaps you sold the secrets you uncovered to the highest bidder. (Mechanically identical to the Criminal; the difference is purely background flavor.)

## Common questions

**Q: How far away can my Criminal Contact reach?**
A: As far as your message-runners can carry word — the feature explicitly works **over great distances**, relying on the messengers, corrupt caravan masters, and shady sailors you know. It's a slow, indirect channel, not instant communication, and it gets you in touch with the broader criminal network. (PHB p.129)

**Q: Is the Spy any different mechanically?**
A: No. The **Variant Criminal: Spy** uses the exact same skills, tools, equipment, and feature — it's a reflavoring (espionage agent rather than thief) to fit a different character concept. (PHB p.129)
