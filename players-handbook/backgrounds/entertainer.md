# Entertainer

A performer who lives for the crowd — a bard's-tale rogue's-gallery of actors, dancers, musicians, poets, and jesters whose art is also their livelihood and their passport from town to town.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed pages 130–131).

---

## Flavor & lore

You thrive in front of an audience. You know how to entrance, entertain, and even inspire a crowd — your words can stir grief or joy, your music lifts spirits or captures sorrow, your dance and humor captivate. Whatever your craft, your art *is* your life, and you've spent years honing it on stages, in taverns, at festivals, or in a traveling troupe.

An entertainer is rarely a stranger for long: a good performance buys a meal, a bed, and a town's goodwill. Decide what kind of performer you are (see the Entertainer Routines table) and where your art has taken you — the back rooms of inns, a circus, a theater, or even a noble's court. This background pairs obviously with bards but layers naturally onto any class as a charismatic, road-worn public face.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Acrobatics, Performance
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit, one type of musical instrument
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** A musical instrument (one of your choice), the favor of an admirer (love letter, lock of hair, or trinket), a costume, and a belt pouch containing **15 gp**.

## Feature: By Popular Demand

You can always find a place to perform — usually an inn or tavern, but possibly a circus, a theater, or even a noble's court. Wherever you perform, you receive **free lodging and food of a modest or comfortable standard** (depending on the quality of the establishment) for as long as you perform each night. Your performances also make you something of a **local figure**: when strangers recognize you in a town where you've performed, they typically take a liking to you. (PHB p.130)

## Variants / specialties

### Variant Entertainer: Gladiator (p.131)

A gladiator is an entertainer too — trained to turn the arts of combat into a spectacle the crowd can enjoy. Flashy fighting is your "routine" (you might also dabble as a tumbler or actor). Mechanically:

- Your **By Popular Demand** feature lets you find a place to perform anywhere that features combat for entertainment — a gladiatorial arena or a secret pit-fighting club.
- You may **replace the musical instrument** in your equipment package with an inexpensive but unusual weapon, such as a **trident or net**.

(All other mechanics — skills, tools, gold, characteristics — are identical to the base Entertainer.)

## Common questions

**Q: Do I really get free room and board everywhere?**
A: Only where you actually perform, and only as long as you perform **each night**. The quality (modest vs. comfortable) tracks the establishment. Stop performing and the deal ends. (PHB p.130)

**Q: Does By Popular Demand cover my whole party?**
A: The free lodging/food is written for **you** the performer. A generous DM may extend a meal to companions, but the feature is your benefit; it's not a party-wide upkeep waiver. (PHB p.130)

**Q: What's the difference between an Entertainer and a Gladiator?**
A: Purely flavor + two small mechanical swaps — the Gladiator performs in arenas/pit-fights instead of taverns, and may trade the instrument for a cheap unusual weapon (trident/net). Skills, tools, gold, and characteristics are the same. (PHB p.131)
