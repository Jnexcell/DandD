# Folk Hero

A champion risen from the common people — a farmer, miner, or shepherd whose courage in a single defining moment set them on the road to greatness. Grants proficiency in **Animal Handling** and **Survival**, and the **Rustic Hospitality** feature, which lets you find shelter and refuge among ordinary folk anywhere.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 131).

---

## Flavor & lore

You were born to a humble station — and you would not trade it for anything. Folk heroes come from the ranks of the peasantry: farmers, miners, servants, shepherds, woodcutters, gravediggers. Then something happened. You stood where no one else would, did what no one else could, and the people of your home village began to call you *theirs*. Where a noble sees their birth as a right, you see your origins as a virtue, and the community that raised you still matters more than any title.

That defining moment didn't just change how others see you — it pointed you down a road. Tyrants and monsters threaten common folk everywhere, and you feel called to stand between them and the people who can't fight back. You may be rough around the edges and short on book-learning, but you have heart, and an entire community ready to believe in you.

This background suits a character who is destined for more but never forgets where they came from: the reluctant champion, the people's defender, the unlikely hero whose greatest strength is that ordinary folk trust them on sight.

## Mechanics

*(All values from PHB p.131.)*

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Animal Handling, Survival
- **Tool Proficiencies:** One type of artisan's tools, vehicles (land)
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** A set of artisan's tools (one of your choice), a shovel, an iron pot, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing **10 gp**.

---

## Feature: Rustic Hospitality

Because you rose from the common people, you blend in among them effortlessly. You can always find a place to hide, rest, or recover among other commoners — unless you've given them reason to see you as a threat. Ordinary folk will shelter you from the law or anyone else hunting you, though their goodwill has limits: they will hide you, but they won't lay down their lives to do it.

## Common questions

**Does Rustic Hospitality cost anything?** No. It's free aid from commoners, not a paid service — but it only extends to ordinary people, and only as long as you haven't shown yourself to be dangerous to them. They'll shelter and feed you; they won't risk their lives.

**Can I pick any artisan's tools and any land vehicle?** You're proficient with **one** type of artisan's tools (your choice) plus **land** vehicles generally (carts, wagons, chariots, and the like). The starting kit then includes one set of artisan's tools matching your choice.
