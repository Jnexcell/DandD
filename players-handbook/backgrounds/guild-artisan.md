# Guild Artisan

A skilled craftsperson and a respected member of an artisans' guild — part of the mercantile world, freed by talent and earnings from the old feudal order. Grants proficiency in **Insight** and **Persuasion**, and the **Guild Membership** feature, which provides lodging, legal backing, and political connections through your guild.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 132).

---

## Flavor & lore

You belong to a guild — a tightly knit fellowship of artisans who share a trade, guard its secrets, and look after their own. You learned your craft the hard way: years as an apprentice under a master, sponsored by your guild, until your skill earned you the rank of master in your own right. That standing means something. Guilds wield real economic and political weight, and a member in good standing can call on lodging, legal defense, and access to powerful figures that an ordinary tradesperson never could.

Guild artisans are among the most ordinary people in the world — right up until they set down their tools and take up adventuring. You understand the value of hard work and the strength of community, but the same world that taught you those virtues also tempts you toward greed and covetousness. The open question of your life is whether you'll abandon the workshop for the road, or find a way to braid trade and adventure together.

This background suits the practical, grounded character: the smith, the brewer, the jeweler, the merchant with an eye for a deal — someone whose competence and connections open doors that swords and spells cannot.

## Mechanics

*(All values from PHB p.132.)*

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Insight, Persuasion
- **Tool Proficiencies:** One type of artisan's tools
- **Languages:** One of your choice
- **Equipment:** A set of artisan's tools (one of your choice), a letter of introduction from your guild, a set of traveler's clothes, and a belt pouch containing **15 gp**.

---

## Feature: Guild Membership

As an established and respected guild member, you enjoy real benefits. Fellow members will provide you with food and lodging when you need it, and will pay for your funeral if it comes to that. In many cities and towns a guildhall gives you a place to meet others in your profession — a good spot to find patrons, allies, or hirelings.

Guilds also carry considerable political clout. If you're accused of a crime, your guild will back you so long as a reasonable case for your innocence (or the act's justification) can be made. Membership in good standing can also open doors to influential political figures, though such favors may require donations of money or magic items to the guild's coffers.

The catch: you owe **dues of 5 gp per month**. Miss payments and you fall out of good standing until you've made up the back dues.

### Variant Guild Artisan: Guild Merchant

Instead of a crafters' guild, you might belong to a guild of traders, caravan masters, or shopkeepers. You don't make goods yourself — you earn your living buying and selling the work of others (or the raw materials artisans need). Your guild might be a large merchant consortium or family with interests across the region; perhaps you hauled goods by ship, wagon, or caravan, or bought from traveling traders to sell in your own little shop. In many ways the traveling merchant's life suits adventure even better than the artisan's.

For this variant, replace proficiency with artisan's tools with proficiency in **navigator's tools or an additional language**, and replace the starting artisan's tools with **a mule and a cart**.

## Common questions

**Do I have to keep paying the 5 gp dues while adventuring?** Yes, to stay in good standing — and good standing is what unlocks lodging, legal backing, and political connections. Fall behind and you owe back dues before the benefits resume. It's a small, flavorful gold sink the DM can use to keep the guild relevant.

**Can my guild get me out of any crime?** No. The guild backs you only when a good case for innocence can be made, or the act was justifiable. It won't shield a member who's obviously guilty of something indefensible.
