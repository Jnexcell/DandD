# Hermit

A seeker who spent a formative stretch of life in seclusion — a monastery, a wilderness hermitage, or total solitude — and emerged with quiet, hard-won wisdom and a single powerful Discovery.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed pages 134–135).

---

## Flavor & lore

You lived in seclusion for a formative part of your life — either in a sheltered community such as a monastery, or entirely alone. Away from the clamor of society you found quiet, solitude, and perhaps some of the answers you were looking for. Decide *why* you withdrew and *what* finally drew you back (see the Life of Seclusion table); the two questions together define your character's shape.

Some hermits are well suited to solitude; others chafed against it and longed for company. A few are left a little mad by their years apart. The common thread is perspective: you've had time to think, and you return to the world carrying something you learned out there.

> **Other hermits (PHB sidebar):** this background assumes a *contemplative* seclusion with room for study and prayer. If you want a rugged wilderness recluse who lives off the land, look at **Outlander**; for a more overtly religious bent, look at **Acolyte**; and a fraudster posing as a holy sage might be a **Charlatan**.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Medicine, Religion
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Herbalism kit
- **Languages:** One of your choice
- **Equipment:** A scroll case stuffed full of notes from your studies or prayers, a winter blanket, a set of common clothes, an herbalism kit, and **5 gp**.

### Life of Seclusion (d8)

Why you withdrew from the world:

| d8 | Reason |
|----|--------|
| 1 | I was searching for spiritual enlightenment. |
| 2 | I was partaking of communal living in accordance with the dictates of a religious order. |
| 3 | I was exiled for a crime I didn't commit. |
| 4 | I retreated from society after a life-altering event. |
| 5 | I needed a quiet place to work on my art, literature, music, or manifesto. |
| 6 | I needed to commune with nature, far from civilization. |
| 7 | I was the caretaker of an ancient ruin or relic. |
| 8 | I was a pilgrim in search of a person, place, or relic of spiritual significance. |

---

## Feature: Discovery

The quiet seclusion of your extended hermitage gave you access to a **unique and powerful discovery**, the nature of which depends on your seclusion. It might be a great truth about the cosmos, the gods, the powerful beings of the outer planes, or the forces of nature; a site no one else has ever seen; a long-forgotten fact or a relic that could rewrite history; or information damaging to those who exiled you (and the reason for your return). **Work with your DM** to determine the details and its impact on the campaign. (PHB p.134)

---

## Variants / specialties

The Hermit has **no variant sub-background** in the PHB. You customize it through your Life of Seclusion choice, the nature of your Discovery, and the characteristics below. (See the "Other hermits" sidebar above for adjacent backgrounds if Hermit isn't quite the fit.)

## Suggested characteristics

Whether they embraced solitude or longed to escape it, the solitary life shapes a hermit's attitudes and ideals — and a few are driven slightly mad by their years apart from society.

### Personality Traits (d8)

| d8 | Trait |
|----|-------|
| 1 | I've been isolated for so long that I rarely speak, preferring gestures and the occasional grunt. |
| 2 | I am utterly serene, even in the face of disaster. |
| 3 | The leader of my community had something wise to say on every topic, and I am eager to share that wisdom. |
| 4 | I feel tremendous empathy for all who suffer. |
| 5 | I'm oblivious to etiquette and social expectations. |
| 6 | I connect everything that happens to me to a grand, cosmic plan. |
| 7 | I often get lost in my own thoughts and contemplation, becoming oblivious to my surroundings. |
| 8 | I am working on a grand philosophical theory and love sharing my ideas. |

### Ideals (d6)

| d6 | Ideal |
|----|-------|
| 1 | **Greater Good.** My gifts are meant to be shared with all, not used for my own benefit. (Good) |
| 2 | **Logic.** Emotions must not cloud our sense of what is right and true, or our logical thinking. (Lawful) |
| 3 | **Free Thinking.** Inquiry and curiosity are the pillars of progress. (Chaotic) |
| 4 | **Power.** Solitude and contemplation are paths toward mystical or magical power. (Evil) |
| 5 | **Live and Let Live.** Meddling in the affairs of others only causes trouble. (Neutral) |
| 6 | **Self-Knowledge.** If you know yourself, there's nothing left to know. (Any) |

### Bonds (d6)

| d6 | Bond |
|----|-------|
| 1 | Nothing is more important than the other members of my hermitage, order, or association. |
| 2 | I entered seclusion to hide from the ones who might still be hunting me. I must someday confront them. |
| 3 | I'm still seeking the enlightenment I pursued in my seclusion, and it still eludes me. |
| 4 | I entered seclusion because I loved someone I could not have. |
| 5 | Should my discovery come to light, it could bring ruin to the world. |
| 6 | My isolation gave me great insight into a great evil that only I can destroy. |

### Flaws (d6)

| d6 | Flaw |
|----|-------|
| 1 | Now that I've returned to the world, I enjoy its delights a little too much. |
| 2 | I harbor dark, bloodthirsty thoughts that my isolation and meditation failed to quell. |
| 3 | I am dogmatic in my thoughts and philosophy. |
| 4 | I let my need to win arguments overshadow friendships and harmony. |
| 5 | I'd risk too much to uncover a lost bit of knowledge. |
| 6 | I like keeping secrets and won't share them with anyone. |

## Common questions

**Q: What exactly is my "Discovery"?**
A: It's deliberately open — the rules hand you a powerful secret (a cosmic truth, a hidden place, a forgotten fact, a campaign-shaking relic) and tell you to **define it with your DM**. It's a story hook, not a stat bonus. (PHB p.134)

**Q: Is a Hermit a religious character?**
A: It can be, but doesn't have to be — Religion is a granted skill, but your seclusion might be philosophical, artistic, or natural rather than devotional. For an explicitly faith-driven character, see **Acolyte**; for a wilderness loner, see **Outlander**. (PHB p.134–135)
