# Outlander

The wild-born wanderer — a survivor of the untamed places who knows the land, the weather, and the long road better than any city-dweller ever could.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 136).

---

## Flavor & lore

You grew up far from the comfort of walls and cobblestones — in the deep forest, the high mountains, the trackless plains, or some other wilderness that most people only cross with a guide and a prayer. You've seen strange lands and stranger things, and you've learned to read terrain the way a sage reads a book. Civilization, with its niceties and rules, often strikes you as soft and bewildering; the ties that matter to you are tribe, clan, family, and the natural world you came from.

This background suits the ranger, barbarian, or druid almost by default, but it fits any character whose story begins in the wild — a guide, a hunter, a pilgrim, an exile, or a marauder driven out of the lands they once raided. Think about the distant places you've been and how they shaped you.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Survival
- **Tool Proficiencies:** One type of musical instrument
- **Languages:** One of your choice
- **Equipment:** A staff, a hunting trap, a trophy from an animal you killed, a set of traveler's clothes, and a belt pouch containing **10 gp**.

---

## Feature: Wanderer

You have an exceptional memory for maps and the lay of the land. You can always call to mind the general layout of the terrain, settlements, and landmarks around you. On top of that, you can forage enough food and fresh water each day to keep yourself and up to **five other people** fed — as long as the surrounding country actually offers something to find (berries, small game, water, and the like). Barren wastes and lifeless dungeons won't feed anyone.

## Suggested characteristics

Often dismissed as rude and uncouth by civilized folk, outlanders have little patience for the niceties of city life. Their deepest bonds are to tribe, clan, family, and the natural world they belong to.

## Common questions

**How many people can Wanderer feed?** Yourself plus up to five others — six total — each day, but only where the land actually provides (berries, small game, water). It does nothing in a desert, on the open sea, or deep in a dungeon.

**Does Wanderer mean I never get lost?** Not exactly. You recall the *general* layout of terrain and settlements you know, which makes overland navigation far easier, but the DM still decides when truly unfamiliar or magically confounded terrain throws you off.
