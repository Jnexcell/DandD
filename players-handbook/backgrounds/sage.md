# Sage

The lifelong scholar — a master of lore who has spent years among manuscripts, scrolls, and the greatest minds of the age, and who can usually find out where any piece of knowledge is hidden.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 138).

---

## Flavor & lore

You spent years studying the lore of the multiverse. You pored over manuscripts, untangled ancient scrolls, and sat at the feet of the foremost experts in the subjects that fascinate you. That work has made you a genuine authority in your fields — an alchemist, an astronomer, a wizard's apprentice, a librarian, a professor, or a scribe. Knowledge is your domain, and you carry a colleague's unanswered question with you like an itch you can't quite scratch.

This background pairs naturally with Intelligence-based casters (especially the wizard) and any character whose power or purpose grows from books, secrets, and study. A sage values knowledge highly — sometimes for its own sake, sometimes as a path to other ends.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Arcana, History
- **Tool Proficiencies:** None
- **Languages:** Two of your choice
- **Equipment:** A bottle of black ink, a quill, a small knife, a letter from a dead colleague posing a question you have not yet been able to answer, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing **10 gp**.

---

## Feature: Researcher

When you set out to learn or recall a piece of lore and don't already know it, you usually know *where* and *from whom* you can find it. The trail typically leads to a library, a scriptorium, a university, or some learned person or creature. The knowledge itself isn't guaranteed — your DM may decide it's locked away somewhere nearly impossible to reach, or has been lost entirely. Prying the deepest secrets of the multiverse loose can become an adventure, or even an entire campaign, in its own right.

## Suggested characteristics

Sages are defined by their studies, and their characteristics reflect that life of scholarship. Devoted to learning, a sage prizes knowledge highly — sometimes purely for itself, sometimes as a means toward other ideals.

### Personality Traits (d8)

| d8 | Personality Trait |
|----|-------------------|
| 1 | I use polysyllabic words that convey the impression of great erudition. |
| 2 | I've read every book in the world's greatest libraries — or I like to boast that I have. |
| 3 | I'm used to helping out those who aren't as smart as I am, and I patiently explain anything and everything to others. |
| 4 | There's nothing I like more than a good mystery. |
| 5 | I'm willing to listen to every side of an argument before I make my own judgment. |

| 8 | I'm convinced that people are always trying to steal my secrets. |

## Common questions

**Does Researcher just hand me the answer?** No. It tells you where the knowledge can probably be found — a person, place, or institution — not the knowledge itself. Getting to it may be trivial or may be the whole quest, at the DM's discretion.

**The Sage gets no tool proficiency?** Correct. Unlike most backgrounds, Sage grants no tool proficiency; it grants two languages instead, reflecting a life spent reading rather than working with one's hands.
