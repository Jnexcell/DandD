# Sailor

The salt-stained mariner — years at sea behind you, storms and sea-monsters survived, and a network of ships and crews you can still call on when you need to move.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 139).

---

## Flavor & lore

You spent years aboard a seagoing vessel. In that time you weathered mighty storms, faced monsters of the deep, and stared down anyone who tried to send your ship to the bottom. The horizon is your first love, but the time has come to try your hand at something new. Talk with your DM about the ship you served on — was it a merchant ship, a naval vessel, a ship of discovery, or a pirate ship? How famous (or infamous) is it, and is it still afloat? What was your post — boatswain, captain, navigator, cook, or something else — and did you leave on good terms or on the run?

This background suits any character at home on the water, and its **Pirate** variant (below) opens it up to rogues and rogues-at-heart with a darker past.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Perception
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Navigator's tools, vehicles (water)
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** A belaying pin (club), 50 feet of silk rope, a lucky charm such as a rabbit foot or a small stone with a hole in the center (or you may roll for a random trinket on the Trinkets table in chapter 5), a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing **10 gp**.

---

## Feature: Ship's Passage

When you need it, you can arrange free passage aboard a sailing ship for yourself and your adventuring companions — perhaps on the ship you once served on, or on another vessel you're on good terms with (maybe one captained by a former crewmate). Because you're calling in a favor rather than booking a fare, you can't dictate the schedule or route; your DM decides how long it takes to reach where you're going. In exchange for the free ride, you and your companions are expected to pitch in and help work the ship during the voyage.

## Variants / specialties

### Variant Sailor: Pirate

You spent your youth under the sway of a dread pirate — a ruthless cutthroat who taught you how to survive in a world of sharks and savages. You've practiced larceny on the high seas and sent more than one deserving soul to a briny grave. Fear and bloodshed are old companions, and you've earned a distinctly unsavory reputation in many a port town.

If your sailing career involved piracy, you may take the **Bad Reputation** feature in place of **Ship's Passage**.

### Variant Feature: Bad Reputation

*(If your character has a sailor background, you may select this background feature instead of Ship's Passage.)*

No matter where you go, people are afraid of you because of your reputation. While you're in a civilized settlement, you can get away with minor criminal offenses — refusing to pay for a meal at a tavern, breaking down doors at a local shop, and the like — because most people won't report your activity to the authorities.

## Common questions

**Can I summon a ship anywhere, anytime?** No. Ship's Passage gets you a ride when a suitable ship is reasonably available, and the DM controls the timing and route. You're calling in a favor, not chartering on demand, and you're expected to crew during the trip.

**Should I take Ship's Passage or Bad Reputation?** Only Pirate-variant sailors may swap to Bad Reputation. Ship's Passage is about mobility; Bad Reputation is about intimidation and getting away with petty crimes. Pick the one that fits your character — you don't get both.
