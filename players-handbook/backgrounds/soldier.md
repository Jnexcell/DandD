# Soldier

The career warrior — trained from youth in weapons, armor, and survival, and still owed deference by those who served beneath you.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 140).

---

## Flavor & lore

War has been your life for as long as you care to remember. You trained as a youth, studied weapons and armor, and learned the basics of staying alive on a battlefield. You might have served in a standing national army, a town guard, a village militia, a noble's or merchant's private force, or a mercenary company. Work with your DM to decide which organization you belonged to, how far up its ranks you rose, and what you saw along the way.

This background is the natural home for fighters, paladins, and barbarians, but it suits any character whose past was shaped by drill, discipline, and the horrors of war.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Intimidation
- **Tool Proficiencies:** One type of gaming set, vehicles (land)
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** An insignia of rank, a trophy taken from a fallen enemy (a dagger, broken blade, or piece of a banner), a set of bone dice or deck of cards, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing **10 gp**.

---

## Feature: Military Rank

You hold a military rank earned during your service. Soldiers still loyal to your former organization recognize your authority and influence, and those of lower rank defer to you. You can invoke your rank to pull weight with other soldiers, requisition simple equipment or horses for temporary use, and generally gain entry to friendly military camps and fortresses where your rank carries weight.

> **On invoking your rank:** the feature works among soldiers of *your* former organization (or allied forces that honor it). It is influence, not absolute command — the DM decides how far your authority stretches, especially with troops who don't know you or owe you nothing.

## Suggested characteristics

The horrors of war combined with the rigid discipline of military service leave their mark on every soldier — shaping their ideals, forging strong bonds, and often leaving them scarred and vulnerable to fear, shame, and hatred.

## Common questions

**Can I order any soldier around?** No. Military Rank only commands deference from troops of *your* former organization (or allies who recognize it) who are of lower rank. It's leverage and access, not blanket authority over every guard you meet — the DM adjudicates the limits.

**What can I requisition?** Simple equipment and horses for temporary use, plus access to friendly military camps and fortresses. It doesn't conjure an army or supply you with magic gear.
