# Urchin

A street survivor — orphaned and poor, you grew up alone in the city's gutters and rooftops, and you lived through it on cunning, speed, and nerve.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 4, "Backgrounds" (printed page 141).

---

## Flavor & lore

You grew up on the streets alone, orphaned, and poor, with no one to watch over you or provide for you — so you learned to provide for yourself. You fought fiercely over food and kept a constant watch for other desperate souls who might steal from you. You slept on rooftops and in alleyways, exposed to the elements, and endured sickness without medicine or a place to recover. You survived against the odds through cunning, strength, speed, or some mix of all three.

You begin your adventuring career with enough money to live **modestly but securely for at least ten days**. Decide how you came by it, and what finally let you break free of those desperate circumstances to chase a better life — that turning point is a natural seed for your character's drive.

---

## Mechanics

- **Skill Proficiencies:** Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- **Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit, thieves' tools
- **Languages:** None
- **Equipment:** A small knife, a map of the city you grew up in, a pet mouse, a token to remember your parents by, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing **10 gp**.

---

## Feature: City Secrets

You know the secret patterns and flow of cities and can find passages through the urban sprawl that others would miss. **When you are not in combat, you (and companions you lead) can travel between any two locations in the city twice as fast as your speed would normally allow.** (PHB p.141)

---

## Variants / specialties

The Urchin has **no variant sub-background** in the PHB. You customize it through your choice of home city, how you escaped poverty, and the characteristics below.

---

## Suggested characteristics

Urchins are shaped by lives of desperate poverty, for good and for ill. They tend to be driven either by loyalty to the people they shared the streets with, or by a burning desire for a better life — and maybe some payback on the rich folk who treated them badly.

## Common questions

**Q: How fast does City Secrets actually move us?**
A: **Double speed** between any two points in the city, but only **out of combat** and only while you're leading. It's a navigation/shortcut feature for getting across town quickly (and for losing a tail), not a combat movement boost. (PHB p.141)

**Q: What's the "ten days of money" line mean?**
A: It's flavor for your starting gold — you've scraped together enough to live modestly but securely for about ten days as you begin adventuring. It doesn't add coin beyond your listed equipment/starting wealth; it frames *how* you got out of the gutter. (PHB p.141)
