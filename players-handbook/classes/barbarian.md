# Barbarian

A fierce melee warrior who channels primal fury into **rage**, gaining bonus damage, resistance to physical damage, and superhuman resilience. High Hit Die, heavy hitter, hard to drop.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Barbarian (printed pages 46–50).

---

## Flavor & lore

A barbarian is a warrior who fights from a place of fury rather than discipline — the wandering tribesperson, the exiled berserker, the wrathful avenger, the savage outlander whose strength comes from somewhere wild and primal inside them. Where a fighter wins through trained precision, the barbarian wins by going harder than anyone else can survive. They wear little armor and don't need it: with the biggest Hit Die in the game (d12) and a body forged by a harsh life, they simply have too much grit and too much rage to be put down.

In a party the barbarian is the frontline anchor — equal parts tank and wrecking ball. The engine is **Rage**: a bonus action that floods them with combat fury, granting **bonus damage** on melee attacks and, crucially, **resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage** — halving the most common damage in the game and making them maddeningly hard to drop. **Reckless Attack** lets them throw caution aside for advantage on their swings (and hand it back to enemies, which they're durable enough not to mind), while **Danger Sense**, **Relentless Rage**, and eventually flat-out **Indomitable Might** keep them standing long past the point where anyone else would fall. They draw fire, hold the line, and turn the enemy's biggest hits into glancing blows.

The fantasy is the unstoppable juggernaut — the one who walks into the worst of the fight, takes everything the enemy has, and keeps swinging. A barbarian doesn't out-clever the battlefield; they become the most dangerous thing on it. Pick a subclass to shape that fury: the **Path of the Berserker**, raw and frenzied; or the **Path of the Totem Warrior**, whose rage is bound to primal animal spirits.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d12 (per barbarian level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 12 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d12 (or 7) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Strength, Constitution.
- **Armor proficiencies:** Light armor, medium armor, shields.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Simple weapons, martial weapons.
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival.
- **Primary ability:** Strength (Constitution secondary).

### Starting equipment (PHB p.46)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a greataxe **or** (b) any martial melee weapon
- (a) two handaxes **or** (b) any simple weapon
- An explorer's pack and four javelins

## Class features

### Rage (1st level) (PHB p.48)

On your turn you can enter a rage as a **bonus action**. While raging — provided you **aren't wearing heavy armor** — you gain:

- **Advantage** on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
- A bonus to melee weapon **damage** when you attack using Strength (+2/+3/+4 per the Rage Damage column).
- **Resistance** to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage (i.e., most physical attacks deal half).

You **can't cast spells or concentrate** on spells while raging.

**Duration & ending:** A rage lasts up to **1 minute** (10 rounds). It ends early if you are knocked unconscious, or if your turn ends and you **haven't attacked a hostile creature since your last turn nor taken damage since then**. You can also end it voluntarily as a bonus action. Once you've raged the number of times shown in the **Rages** column, you must finish a **long rest** before raging again.

### Unarmored Defense (1st level) (PHB p.48)

While wearing no armor, your AC = **10 + Dexterity modifier + Constitution modifier**. You can still use a **shield** and keep this benefit.

### Reckless Attack (2nd level) (PHB p.48)

On your **first attack** of your turn you may attack recklessly. You gain **advantage** on melee weapon attack rolls using Strength this turn, but **attack rolls against you have advantage** until your next turn.

### Danger Sense (2nd level) (PHB p.48)

You have **advantage on Dexterity saving throws** against effects you can **see** (traps, spells, etc.). You don't get this benefit while blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.

### Primal Path (3rd level) (PHB p.48)

Choose a path that shapes your rage — **Path of the Berserker** or **Path of the Totem Warrior** (see Subclasses). It grants features at **3rd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level**.

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.49)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that optional rule.

### Extra Attack (5th level) (PHB p.49)

You attack **twice** instead of once whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

### Fast Movement (5th level) (PHB p.49)

Your speed increases by **10 feet** while you aren't wearing heavy armor.

### Feral Instinct (7th level) (PHB p.49)

You have **advantage on initiative** rolls. Additionally, if you're surprised at the start of combat and aren't incapacitated, you can **act normally on your first turn** — but only if you **enter your rage first**, before doing anything else.

### Brutal Critical (9th, 13th, 17th level) (PHB p.49)

On a **critical hit** with a **melee** attack, roll **one extra weapon damage die** for the extra crit damage. This increases to **two** extra dice at 13th level and **three** at 17th level.

### Relentless Rage (11th level) (PHB p.49)

If you drop to 0 HP while raging and don't die outright, make a **DC 10 Constitution save**; on a success you drop to **1 HP** instead. Each subsequent use before resting raises the **DC by 5** (10, 15, 20, …). The DC **resets to 10** after a short or long rest.

### Persistent Rage (15th level) (PHB p.49)

Your rage now ends early **only** if you fall unconscious or choose to end it — it no longer drops for lack of attacking/taking damage.

### Indomitable Might (18th level) (PHB p.49)

If your total for a **Strength check** is less than your Strength **score**, you can use your score in place of the total (so a Str 20 barbarian treats any Str check as at least 20).

### Primal Champion (20th level) (PHB p.49)

Your **Strength and Constitution scores increase by 4**, and your maximum for those two scores becomes **24**.

## Subclasses (Primal Paths)

Chosen at 3rd level.

### Path of the Berserker (PHB p.49)

A path of untrammeled fury, heedless of one's own health.

- **Frenzy (3rd level).** When you rage, you can choose to go into a frenzy. For the rage's duration you can make a **single melee weapon attack as a bonus action** on each of your turns after this one. When the rage ends, you suffer **one level of exhaustion** (PHB Appendix A).
- **Mindless Rage (6th level).** You **can't be charmed or frightened while raging**. If you're already charmed or frightened when you start raging, that effect is **suspended** for the rage's duration.
- **Intimidating Presence (10th level).** Use your **action** to frighten one creature within **30 feet** that can see or hear you. It must succeed on a **Wisdom save (DC 8 + proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier)** or be frightened until the end of your next turn. On later turns you can use your action again to **extend** the effect; it ends if the target ends its turn out of line of sight or more than **60 feet** away. On a successful save, you can't target that creature again for **24 hours**.
- **Retaliation (14th level).** When you take damage from a creature within **5 feet**, you can use your **reaction** to make one melee weapon attack against it.

### Path of the Totem Warrior (PHB p.49–50)

A spiritual path; a totem animal lends supernatural might to your rage. You must make or acquire a physical totem object (fur, feathers, claws, teeth, or bones).

- **Spirit Seeker (3rd level).** You can cast **beast sense** and **speak with animals**, but only as **rituals**.
- **Totem Spirit (3rd level).** Choose a totem animal and gain its benefit:
  - **Bear.** While raging, you have **resistance to all damage except psychic**. (Extremely durable.)
  - **Eagle.** While raging and not in heavy armor, other creatures have **disadvantage on opportunity attacks** against you, and you can use the **Dash action as a bonus action** on your turn.
  - **Wolf.** While raging, your allies have **advantage on melee attack rolls** against any enemy within 5 feet of you.
- **Aspect of the Beast (6th level).** Gain a magical benefit from a totem animal (same or different choice):
  - **Bear.** Your **carrying capacity (including max load and lift) is doubled**, and you have advantage on Strength checks to push, pull, lift, or break objects.
  - **Eagle.** You can see up to **1 mile** with no difficulty, discerning fine detail as if within 100 feet, and **dim light doesn't impose disadvantage** on your Wisdom (Perception) checks.
  - **Wolf.** You can **track** at a fast pace and **move stealthily** at a normal pace.
- **Spirit Walker (10th level).** You can cast **commune with nature** as a **ritual**; a spiritual form of one of your totem animals appears to convey the information.
- **Totemic Attunement (14th level).** Gain a magical benefit from a totem animal (same or different choice):
  - **Bear.** While raging, any hostile creature within **5 feet** of you has **disadvantage on attack rolls** against targets other than you (or another character with this feature). Immune if it can't see/hear you or can't be frightened.
  - **Eagle.** While raging, you gain a **flying speed equal to your current walking speed** — in short bursts only; you **fall** if you end your turn aloft with nothing holding you up.
  - **Wolf.** While raging, you can use a **bonus action to knock a Large or smaller creature prone** when you hit it with a melee weapon attack.

## Common questions

**Q: Can a barbarian rage while wearing heavy armor?**
A: You *can* rage, but you **lose all rage benefits** (damage bonus, resistance, advantage on Str checks/saves) while in heavy armor. Light or medium armor is fine. (PHB p.48)

**Q: Does Reckless Attack apply to ranged attacks?**
A: No. The advantage it grants applies only to **melee weapon attack rolls using Strength**. The downside (enemies get advantage to hit you) applies to all attacks against you until your next turn. (PHB p.48)

**Q: How does Bear Totem interact with Brutal Critical and resistance?**
A: Bear Totem gives resistance to **all damage except psychic** while raging — making a Totem Bear barbarian extremely tanky. It doesn't change damage you deal; Brutal Critical and the rage damage bonus are separate offensive features. (PHB p.48, p.50)

**Q: Can a Berserker frenzy every fight without penalty?**
A: No. Each frenzy costs **one level of exhaustion** when the rage ends, and exhaustion only clears with a long rest (one level per long rest). Frenzying repeatedly stacks exhaustion fast and can be crippling or lethal. (PHB p.49, Appendix A)

**Q: Does rage stop me from casting spells from another class (multiclass)?**
A: Yes. While raging you **can't cast or concentrate** on any spells, regardless of source. This is a notable cost for barbarian multiclasses. (PHB p.48)
