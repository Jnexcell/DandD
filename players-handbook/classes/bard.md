# Bard

An inspiring spellcaster whose magic flows from the music of creation. A **full Charisma caster** who buffs allies with **Bardic Inspiration**, is the skill master of the game (**Jack of All Trades**, **Expertise**), and can poach spells from *any* class with **Magical Secrets**.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Bard (printed pages 51–55).

---

## Flavor & lore

The bard treats magic as performance. Where a wizard pores over formulae and a cleric prays for power, a bard pulls on the threads of the song that runs underneath all things — the Words of Creation — and reshapes the world by playing it back. A clever turn of phrase, a chord struck at the right moment, a story told just so: in a bard's hands these are spells. The fantasy here is the charismatic generalist who walks into any room, learns its secrets, and leaves it changed.

In the party, the bard is the glue and the force multiplier. You rarely win the fight by yourself, but everyone around you fights better because you're there. **Bardic Inspiration** hands an ally a die to save a botched roll at the worst possible moment; your spells buff, heal, charm, and disrupt; and **Jack of All Trades** plus **Expertise** make you the face and the problem-solver out of combat — the one who talks the guard down, spots the lie, and picks the lock. With **Magical Secrets** you eventually raid every other class's spellbook, so no two bards play quite the same.

How it feels at the table:

- **You enable, then capitalize.** Drop Inspiration and a control spell, let the party land the hits, mop up with the rest.
- **You are the conversation.** High Charisma, broad skills, and silver-tongued spells (charm, suggestion, illusion) make social and exploration scenes your arena, not just combat.
- **You're a flexible spellbook with a fixed repertoire.** You *know* a handful of spells rather than preparing fresh each day, so picks matter — but Magical Secrets lets you bend the bard list toward healing, blasting, or utility as your table needs.

A College of Lore bard leans into skills, secrets, and battlefield disruption; a College of Valor bard straps on armor and trades blows on the front line while still singing the party up.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d8 (per bard level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Dexterity, Charisma.
- **Armor proficiencies:** Light armor.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords.
- **Tool proficiencies:** Three musical instruments of your choice.
- **Skills:** Choose **any three**.
- **Primary ability:** Charisma (Dexterity and Constitution secondary).
- **Spellcasting focus:** A musical instrument.

### Starting equipment (PHB p.51)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a rapier, (b) a longsword, **or** (c) any simple weapon
- (a) a diplomat's pack **or** (b) an entertainer's pack
- (a) a lute **or** (b) any other musical instrument
- Leather armor and a dagger

## Class features

### Spellcasting (1st level) (PHB p.51–52)

You cast **bard** spells using **Charisma** as your spellcasting ability. Bards are a **spells-known** full caster — you don't prepare from a list; you have a fixed repertoire that you swap one spell of at a time when you level.

- **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.**
- **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.**
- **Cantrips:** Start with **two** bard cantrips; scales to 3 at 4th and 4 at 10th (see table).
- **Spells known:** Start with **four** 1st-level spells; learn more per the Spells Known column. Each new spell must be of a level you have slots for. When you gain a bard level, you may **replace one** known spell with another from the bard list.
- **Ritual casting:** You can cast any bard spell you know as a **ritual** if it has the ritual tag (no spell slot needed).
- **Spellcasting focus:** Use a musical instrument.
- Spell descriptions are not reproduced here — see `../spells/` and the bard spell list (PHB p.207).

### Bardic Inspiration (1st level) (PHB p.53–54)

As a **bonus action**, choose one creature within **60 feet** that can hear you. It gains one **Bardic Inspiration die** (a d6 at 1st level). Within the next **10 minutes**, that creature can roll the die and add it to **one** ability check, attack roll, or saving throw. The roll can be made **after** the d20 is rolled but **before** the DM says whether it succeeds or fails. A creature can hold only **one** Bardic Inspiration die at a time.

- **Uses = your Charisma modifier** (minimum 1). You **regain all uses on a long rest** (a short or long rest once you have **Font of Inspiration** at 5th).
- **Die scales:** d6 → **d8 at 5th** → **d10 at 10th** → **d12 at 15th**.

### Jack of All Trades (2nd level) (PHB p.54)

Add **half your proficiency bonus (rounded down)** to any ability check you make that **doesn't already** include your proficiency bonus. (Also helps initiative, which is a Dex check.)

### Song of Rest (2nd level) (PHB p.54)

During a **short rest**, if you or friendly creatures who can hear your performance spend Hit Dice to regain HP at the end of the rest, each of those creatures regains an **extra 1d6** HP. The bonus die scales: **1d8 at 9th**, **1d10 at 13th**, **1d12 at 17th**.

### Bard College (3rd level) (PHB p.54)

Choose **College of Lore** or **College of Valor** (see Subclasses). Grants features at **3rd, 6th, and 14th level**.

### Expertise (3rd & 10th level) (PHB p.54)

Choose **two** of your skill proficiencies; your **proficiency bonus is doubled** for any ability check using either. Choose **two more** at **10th level** (four skills total).

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.54)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that option.

### Font of Inspiration (5th level) (PHB p.54)

You now **regain all expended uses of Bardic Inspiration** on a **short or long rest** (instead of only a long rest).

### Countercharm (6th level) (PHB p.54)

As an **action**, start a performance lasting until the end of your next turn. While it lasts, you and friendly creatures within **30 feet** who can hear you have **advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed**. Ends early if you are incapacitated, silenced, or you end it.

### Magical Secrets (10th, 14th, 18th level) (PHB p.54)

Choose **two spells from any class's spell list** (including bard), of a level you can cast (or a cantrip). They count as bard spells for you and **count against your Spells Known**. You get another **two** at **14th** and again at **18th** (six total from this feature). (College of Lore's Additional Magical Secrets at 6th is separate and does *not* count against Spells Known.)

### Superior Inspiration (20th level) (PHB p.54)

When you **roll initiative** and have **no uses of Bardic Inspiration** left, you regain **one** use.

## Subclasses (Bard Colleges)

Chosen at 3rd level.

### College of Lore (PHB p.54–55)

The skill-and-utility college: knows a little about everything, leans into spell breadth and disruption.

- **Bonus Proficiencies (3rd level).** Gain proficiency with **three skills** of your choice.
- **Cutting Words (3rd level).** When a creature you can see within **60 feet** makes an **attack roll, ability check, or damage roll**, use your **reaction** to expend one use of Bardic Inspiration and **subtract the rolled die** from the creature's roll. You can decide to use it **after** the creature rolls, but before the DM declares success/failure (or before damage is dealt). No effect if the creature can't hear you or is **immune to being charmed**.
- **Additional Magical Secrets (6th level).** Learn **two spells from any class**, of a level you can cast (or a cantrip). These count as bard spells but **do not count against your Spells Known** — bonus spells on top of the normal Magical Secrets.
- **Peerless Skill (14th level).** When you make an **ability check**, you can expend one use of Bardic Inspiration and **add the die to the check**. You can decide **after** rolling the die, but before the DM says whether you succeed or fail.

### College of Valor (PHB p.55)

The martial college: fights alongside its allies and can pull double-duty as a front-liner.

- **Bonus Proficiencies (3rd level).** Gain proficiency with **medium armor, shields, and martial weapons**.
- **Combat Inspiration (3rd level).** A creature holding a Bardic Inspiration die from you can use it in two new ways: add the rolled die to a **weapon damage roll** it just made, **or** (as a **reaction** when an attack is made against it) add the die to its **AC** against that attack — deciding after seeing the roll but before knowing whether it hits.
- **Extra Attack (6th level).** Attack **twice**, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.
- **Battle Magic (14th level).** When you use your **action to cast a bard spell**, you can make **one weapon attack as a bonus action**.

## Common questions

**Q: Can a creature have two Bardic Inspiration dice at once, or hold one indefinitely?**
A: No on both. A creature can hold only **one** die at a time, and it lasts only **10 minutes** before it's lost. (PHB p.53)

**Q: When exactly can the recipient decide to use a Bardic Inspiration die?**
A: After the d20 is rolled, but **before the DM announces** whether the roll succeeded or failed. So you can wait to see if you "barely" missed. (PHB p.53)

**Q: Do Magical Secrets spells count toward my Spells Known?**
A: The class feature **Magical Secrets** (10/14/18) spells **do** count against Spells Known. The College of Lore's **Additional Magical Secrets** (6th) spells **do not** — they're bonus. (PHB p.54–55)

**Q: Does College of Valor's Extra Attack stack with the Fighter's, or let me cast a spell and attack twice?**
A: Extra Attack features **don't stack** (you don't get more than the highest grants). And Extra Attack only triggers on the **Attack action** — casting a spell isn't an attack action, so you can't cast a leveled spell and also make your two weapon attacks the same turn (except via **Battle Magic** at 14th, which adds one weapon attack as a bonus action after casting). (PHB p.55, p.198)

**Q: Can Cutting Words reduce a damage roll to negate damage?**
A: It subtracts the Bardic Inspiration die from one attack roll, ability check, **or** damage roll. Applied to damage, it reduces that instance of damage by the rolled amount (it doesn't retroactively turn a hit into a miss). The target must be able to hear you and not be immune to being charmed. (PHB p.54–55)
