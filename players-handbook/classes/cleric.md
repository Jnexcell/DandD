# Cleric

A divine spellcaster who channels a deity's power — a **prepared** full caster (Wisdom) with strong healing, support, and undead control, plus a **Divine Domain** subclass chosen at 1st level that adds spells, Channel Divinity options, and martial or magical punch.

**Source:** PHB Ch.3 (p.56–63)

---

## Flavor & lore

A cleric is a deity's agent in the world — not just a believer, but a conduit. The power you wield isn't yours; it's lent to you by a god (or a pantheon, a force, a principle) in exchange for service, and it shows up as healing light, searing radiance, blessings, and the authority to command the dead to flee. The fantasy is the holy warrior-priest: equal parts battlefield medic, divine spellcaster, and armored champion of a cause.

In the party, the cleric is the most reliable support pillar in the game. You keep people upright with **Cure Wounds** and **Healing Word**, but reducing a cleric to "the healer" sells the class short — clerics buff (Bless), control (Hold Person, Spirit Guardians), blast (Spiritual Weapon, Flame Strike), and turn the tide against undead. Because you **prepare** from the entire cleric list each day, the same character can show up as a frontline tank one session and a ranged support caster the next, just by re-choosing spells after a long rest.

What really defines a cleric is the **Divine Domain** chosen at 1st level — it's the single biggest decision and effectively a built-in subclass that adds spells, **Channel Divinity** powers, and a distinct flavor:

- **Healer / protector:** Life leans hard into restoring HP; Light wards allies and throws fire and radiance.
- **Warrior-priest:** War and Tempest grant heavy armor and martial weapons and reward wading into melee with **Divine Strike**.
- **Specialist:** Knowledge (lore and divination), Nature (a druid-flavored hybrid), and Trickery (illusion and stealth) each pull the class in a very different direction.

Across all of them you channel **Channel Divinity** — most famously **Turn Undead** — and at high levels can literally petition your god for help with **Divine Intervention**.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d8 (per cleric level). (PHB p.57)
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Wisdom, Charisma.
- **Armor proficiencies:** Light armor, medium armor, shields. (Several domains add **heavy armor** at 1st level.)
- **Weapon proficiencies:** All simple weapons. (Some domains add **martial weapons**.)
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from History, Insight, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion.
- **Primary ability:** Wisdom (Constitution and Strength secondary).

### Starting equipment (PHB p.57)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a mace **or** (b) a warhammer (if proficient)
- (a) scale mail, (b) leather armor, **or** (c) chain mail (if proficient)
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts **or** (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a priest's pack **or** (b) an explorer's pack
- A shield and a holy symbol

## Class features

### Spellcasting (1st level) (PHB p.58)

The cleric is a **prepared full caster** using **Wisdom**.

- **Cantrips:** Know **three** cleric cantrips at 1st level; gain more per the table (4 at 4th, 5 at 10th).
- **Spell slots:** Per the table above; regain all on a **long rest**. To cast a spell, expend a slot of the spell's level or higher.
- **Spells prepared:** Each day you prepare a number of cleric spells equal to **your Wisdom modifier + your cleric level** (minimum 1). Choose from the **entire cleric spell list** — clerics don't have a fixed "spells known." The spells must be of a level for which you have slots. You can **change the whole prepared list after a long rest** (prep takes at least 1 minute per spell level, per spell).
- **Spell save DC** = 8 + proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.
- **Spell attack modifier** = proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.
- **Ritual casting:** You can cast a cleric spell as a **ritual** if it has the ritual tag and you have it **prepared**.
- **Spellcasting focus:** You can use a **holy symbol** as your focus (PHB Ch.5).

Domain spells (below) are **always prepared** and **don't count** against your prepared total.

Do not reproduce spell text here — for individual spell descriptions, see `../spells/` (not yet built; fall back to the PDF per CLAUDE.md).

### Divine Domain (1st level) (PHB p.58)

Choose one domain: **Knowledge, Life, Light, Nature, Tempest, Trickery, or War.** Your choice grants **domain spells** and feature(s) at 1st level, a **Channel Divinity option** at 2nd level, and further features at **6th, 8th, and 17th level**. See **Divine Domains** below.

### Channel Divinity (2nd level) (PHB p.58–59)

You channel divine energy to fuel magical effects. You start with **two** options: **Turn Undead** (always available) and an option set by your domain. Some domains add more options at higher levels.

- **Uses:** **one** use, regained on a **short or long rest**. **Two** uses at 6th level, **three** at 18th level.
- Save DC for Channel Divinity effects from this class equals your **cleric spell save DC**.

**Channel Divinity: Turn Undead.** As an **action**, present your holy symbol and pray. Each **undead** that can see or hear you within **30 feet** must make a **Wisdom save**. On a failure, it is **turned** for **1 minute or until it takes any damage**. A turned creature must spend its turns moving as far from you as it can, can't willingly end its move within 30 feet of you, can't take reactions, and can only **Dash** or try to escape; if it can't move, it **Dodges**.

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.59)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a **feat** instead if your DM uses that option.

### Destroy Undead (5th level) (PHB p.59)

When an undead **fails its save** against your **Turn Undead**, it is **instantly destroyed** if its **challenge rating** is at or below the threshold for your level:

| Cleric Level | Destroys undead of CR |
|------|------|
| 5th | 1/2 or lower |
| 8th | 1 or lower |
| 11th | 2 or lower |
| 14th | 3 or lower |
| 17th | 4 or lower |

### Divine Intervention (10th level) (PHB p.59)

Use your **action** to implore your deity's aid; describe the help you seek and **roll percentile dice (d100)**. If you roll **equal to or lower than your cleric level**, your deity intervenes — the **DM** chooses the nature of the help (the effect of any cleric spell or cleric domain spell is appropriate). On success you can't use the feature again for **7 days**; on failure, you can retry after a **long rest**. At **20th level**, your call **succeeds automatically** (no roll needed).

## Divine Domains

Each domain grants its **domain spells** (always prepared, free of your prep limit) at the listed cleric levels, plus the features below. A domain spell that isn't on the cleric list still counts as a **cleric spell** for you.

### Knowledge Domain (PHB p.59)

Gods of learning and understanding (Oghma, Boccob, Gilean, Aureon, Thoth).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | command, identify |
| 3rd | augury, suggestion |
| 5th | nondetection, speak with dead |
| 7th | arcane eye, confusion |
| 9th | legend lore, scrying |

- **Blessings of Knowledge (1st).** Learn **two languages** of your choice. Become proficient in **two** of: Arcana, History, Nature, Religion. Your **proficiency bonus is doubled** for any ability check using either of those skills.
- **Channel Divinity: Knowledge of the Ages (2nd).** As an action, choose one **skill or tool**; for **10 minutes** you have proficiency with it.
- **Channel Divinity: Read Thoughts (6th).** As an action, target one creature within **60 feet**; it makes a **Wisdom save**. On a success you can't use this on it again until you finish a long rest. On a failure you can read its **surface thoughts** while it's within 60 feet for **1 minute**, and during that time you can use your action to end the effect and cast **suggestion** on it (no slot); it **automatically fails** its save against that spell.
- **Potent Spellcasting (8th).** Add your **Wisdom modifier** to the damage of any **cleric cantrip**.
- **Visions of the Past (17th).** After at least 1 minute of meditation (concentration required, up to a number of minutes equal to your Wisdom **score**), receive shadowy glimpses of the past. **Object Reading:** learn how an object's owner acquired/lost it and a recent significant event involving it (and, with more time, prior owners within a number of days equal to your Wis score). **Area Reading:** see significant recent events in your vicinity (up to a 50-foot cube), going back a number of days equal to your Wis score, one event per minute. Once used, recharge on a **short or long rest**.

### Life Domain (PHB p.60)

The default for most non-evil and agricultural/sun/healing deities (Chauntea, Lathander, Pelor, Ilmater, Hestia).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | bless, cure wounds |
| 3rd | lesser restoration, spiritual weapon |
| 5th | beacon of hope, revivify |
| 7th | death ward, guardian of faith |
| 9th | mass cure wounds, raise dead |

- **Bonus Proficiency (1st).** Gain **heavy armor** proficiency.
- **Disciple of Life (1st).** Whenever you use a spell of **1st level or higher** to restore HP, the creature regains **extra HP = 2 + the spell's level**.
- **Channel Divinity: Preserve Life (2nd).** As an action, present your holy symbol and restore HP equal to **five times your cleric level**, divided among creatures within **30 feet**. Can't raise any creature above **half** its HP maximum, and **doesn't work on undead or constructs**.
- **Blessed Healer (6th).** When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher that restores HP to a creature **other than you**, **you also regain HP = 2 + the spell's level**.
- **Divine Strike (8th).** Once per turn, when you hit with a **weapon attack**, deal an extra **1d8 radiant** damage. Increases to **2d8** at 14th level.
- **Supreme Healing (17th).** When you would roll dice to restore HP with a spell, instead use the **maximum** for each die (e.g., a 2d6 heal restores 12).

### Light Domain (PHB p.60–61)

Gods of light, renewal, and vigilance (Helm, Lathander, Pholtus, the Silver Flame, Apollo).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | burning hands, faerie fire |
| 3rd | flaming sphere, scorching ray |
| 5th | daylight, fireball |
| 7th | guardian of faith, wall of fire |
| 9th | flame strike, scrying |

- **Bonus Cantrip (1st).** Gain the **light** cantrip if you don't already know it.
- **Warding Flare (1st).** When attacked by a creature within **30 feet** that you can see, use your **reaction** to impose **disadvantage** on that attack roll. A creature that can't be blinded is immune. Uses **= Wisdom modifier** (minimum 1); regain all on a **long rest**.
- **Channel Divinity: Radiance of the Dawn (2nd).** As an action, dispel **magical darkness** within 30 feet; each hostile creature within 30 feet makes a **Constitution save**, taking **2d10 + cleric level** radiant damage (half on a success). Creatures with total cover are unaffected.
- **Improved Flare (6th).** You can also use **Warding Flare** when a creature you can see within 30 feet attacks a **creature other than you**.
- **Potent Spellcasting (8th).** Add your **Wisdom modifier** to the damage of any **cleric cantrip**.
- **Corona of Light (17th).** As an action, emit an aura of sunlight for **1 minute** (or until dismissed with an action): bright light in a **60-foot** radius and dim light 30 feet beyond. Your enemies in the bright light have **disadvantage on saves** against spells that deal **fire or radiant** damage.

### Nature Domain (PHB p.61–62)

Gods of the natural world (Silvanus, Obad-Hai, Eldath, Pan).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | animal friendship, speak with animals |
| 3rd | barkskin, spike growth |
| 5th | plant growth, wind wall |
| 7th | dominate beast, grasping vine |
| 9th | insect plague, tree stride |

- **Acolyte of Nature (1st).** Learn **one druid cantrip** of your choice, and gain proficiency in one of: **Animal Handling, Nature, or Survival**.
- **Bonus Proficiency (1st).** Gain **heavy armor** proficiency.
- **Channel Divinity: Charm Animals and Plants (2nd).** As an action, each **beast or plant creature** within **30 feet** that can see you makes a **Wisdom save**; on a failure it is **charmed** for **1 minute or until it takes damage**, becoming friendly to you and your designated allies.
- **Dampen Elements (6th).** When you or a creature within **30 feet** takes **acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder** damage, use your **reaction** to grant that creature **resistance** to that instance of damage.
- **Divine Strike (8th).** Once per turn, when you hit with a **weapon attack**, deal an extra **1d8 cold, fire, or lightning** (your choice). Increases to **2d8** at 14th level.
- **Master of Nature (17th).** While creatures are charmed by your **Charm Animals and Plants** feature, you can use a **bonus action** to verbally command what each will do on its next turn.

### Tempest Domain (PHB p.62)

Gods of storms, sea, and sky (Talos, Umberlee, Kord, Zeus, Thor).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | fog cloud, thunderwave |
| 3rd | gust of wind, shatter |
| 5th | call lightning, sleet storm |
| 7th | control water, ice storm |
| 9th | destructive wave, insect plague |

- **Bonus Proficiencies (1st).** Gain **martial weapons** and **heavy armor** proficiency.
- **Wrath of the Storm (1st).** When a creature within **5 feet** that you can see hits you with an attack, use your **reaction** to force a **Dexterity save**; it takes **2d8 lightning or thunder** (your choice), half on a success. Uses **= Wisdom modifier** (minimum 1); regain all on a **long rest**.
- **Channel Divinity: Destructive Wrath (2nd).** When you roll **lightning or thunder** damage, use your Channel Divinity to deal **maximum** damage instead of rolling.
- **Thunderbolt Strike (6th).** When you deal **lightning** damage to a **Large or smaller** creature, you can also **push it up to 10 feet** away from you.
- **Divine Strike (8th).** Once per turn, when you hit with a **weapon attack**, deal an extra **1d8 thunder** damage. Increases to **2d8** at 14th level.
- **Stormborn (17th).** You have a **flying speed** equal to your current walking speed whenever you aren't underground or indoors.

### Trickery Domain (PHB p.62–63)

Gods of mischief and subterfuge (Tymora, Beshaba, Olidammara, Garl Glittergold, Loki).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | charm person, disguise self |
| 3rd | mirror image, pass without trace |
| 5th | blink, dispel magic |
| 7th | dimension door, polymorph |
| 9th | dominate person, modify memory |

- **Blessing of the Trickster (1st).** Use your **action** to touch a willing creature **other than yourself** and give it **advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks**. Lasts **1 hour** or until you use this feature again.
- **Channel Divinity: Invoke Duplicity (2nd).** As an action, create a **perfect illusory duplicate** of yourself within **30 feet** for **1 minute** (concentration). As a **bonus action**, move the illusion up to **30 feet** (must stay within 120 feet). You can **cast spells as though in the illusion's space** (using your own senses). When you and the illusion are **both within 5 feet** of a creature that can see the illusion, you have **advantage on attack rolls** against that creature.
- **Channel Divinity: Cloak of Shadows (6th).** As an action, become **invisible until the end of your next turn**. You become visible if you **attack or cast a spell**.
- **Divine Strike (8th).** Once per turn, when you hit with a **weapon attack**, deal an extra **1d8 poison** damage. Increases to **2d8** at 14th level.
- **Improved Duplicity (17th).** When you use **Invoke Duplicity**, create up to **four** duplicates instead of one. As a bonus action, move **any number** of them up to 30 feet (max range 120 feet).

### War Domain (PHB p.63)

Gods of war, honor, conquest, and destruction (Torm, Heironeous, Bane, Gruumsh, Tempus, Ares).

**Domain spells:**

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | divine favor, shield of faith |
| 3rd | magic weapon, spiritual weapon |
| 5th | crusader's mantle, spirit guardians |
| 7th | freedom of movement, stoneskin |
| 9th | flame strike, hold monster |

- **Bonus Proficiencies (1st).** Gain **martial weapons** and **heavy armor** proficiency.
- **War Priest (1st).** When you take the **Attack action**, you can make **one weapon attack as a bonus action**. Uses **= Wisdom modifier** (minimum 1); regain all on a **long rest**.
- **Channel Divinity: Guided Strike (2nd).** When you make an **attack roll**, use your Channel Divinity to gain a **+10 bonus** to it. You choose this **after seeing the roll** but before the DM says whether it hits.
- **Channel Divinity: War God's Blessing (6th).** When a creature within **30 feet** makes an attack roll, use your **reaction** and Channel Divinity to grant **that creature** a **+10 bonus** (decide after seeing the roll, before hit/miss is announced).
- **Divine Strike (8th).** Once per turn, when you hit with a **weapon attack**, deal an extra **1d8** damage of the **same type the weapon deals**. Increases to **2d8** at 14th level.
- **Avatar of Battle (17th).** You gain **resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing** damage from **nonmagical** weapons.

## Common questions

**Q: How many spells can a cleric prepare?**
A: **Wisdom modifier + cleric level** (minimum 1), re-chosen from the whole cleric list after each long rest. Clerics don't have "spells known" — only cantrips are fixed. Domain spells are always prepared **on top of** this number and don't count against it. (PHB p.58)

**Q: Can I swap my prepared spells freely?**
A: Only when you finish a **long rest**, and it costs prep time (at least 1 minute per spell level, per spell). You cannot change prepared spells mid-day. Note this is preparation, not "spells known" — the cantrips you know are permanent. (PHB p.58)

**Q: Does Turn Undead destroy undead automatically?**
A: No. Turn Undead forces a Wisdom save; failure **turns** the creature (it flees for 1 minute or until it takes damage). **Destroy Undead** (5th+) only **destroys** a turned creature on a failed save if its **CR is at or below your threshold** (1/2 at 5th, scaling to 4 at 17th). A turned creature that takes any damage stops being turned. (PHB p.59)

**Q: Does Divine Strike give an extra attack?**
A: No. It adds extra damage **once per turn** to a weapon attack you already make — it does not grant an additional attack. (Most clerics lack Extra Attack, so this rides on a normal single attack.) Domain determines the damage type: Life/Knowledge-adjacent radiant, Tempest cold/fire/lightning then thunder, Trickery poison, War same-type, etc. (PHB p.60–63)

**Q: How does Divine Intervention's chance work?**
A: Roll **d100**; you succeed if the result is **≤ your cleric level** (so a 10th-level cleric succeeds on a roll of 10 or less, about 10%). Success locks the feature for 7 days; failure lets you retry after a long rest. At **20th level it always succeeds** with no roll. (PHB p.59)

**Q: Which domains get heavy armor and martial weapons?**
A: **Heavy armor:** Life, Nature, Tempest, War (and Knowledge via its Bonus Proficiency). **Martial weapons:** Tempest and War. Light, Trickery, and (weapon-wise) Knowledge clerics stay with simple weapons and up to medium armor. (PHB p.59–63)
