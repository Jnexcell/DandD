# Druid

A Wisdom-based full caster and shapeshifter who channels the divine essence of nature — versatile prepared spells, the signature **Wild Shape** transformation, and a choice of two circles (Land for spell breadth, Moon for combat-focused beast forms).

**Source:** PHB Ch.3 (p.64–69)

---

## Flavor & lore

A druid draws power not from a god but from nature itself — the primal life-force humming through forest, storm, and stone. Druids are the keepers of the Old Faith: they revere the balance of the natural world and the cycle of life and death, and treat the line between civilization and wilderness as something to be respected rather than crossed carelessly. The signature fantasy is the shapeshifter who *is* the wild — one moment a robed mystic calling down lightning, the next a wolf or a bear tearing through the enemy line.

In the party, the druid is the most versatile nature caster. Like the cleric you **prepare** spells from the whole druid list each day, so you can retool from session to session — healing and support one day, battlefield control (Entangle, Spike Growth, Call Lightning) and summoning the next. What sets the druid apart is **Wild Shape**: turning into beasts for scouting, mobility, infiltration, or a second pool of hit points to soak a fight. There's also the class's famous quirk — druids **won't wear metal armor or use metal shields**, a taboo of the Old Faith rather than a mechanical penalty.

How it plays depends heavily on your circle, chosen at 2nd level:

- **Circle of the Land** is the caster-first druid: extra spell slots via Natural Recovery, a bonus cantrip, and a bank of "always prepared" circle spells themed to your terrain. You shapeshift for utility, but your power is your spell list.
- **Circle of the Moon** is the brawler: Wild Shape as a bonus action, beefier beast forms at lower levels, and the ability to heal while transformed — a druid who spends most of the fight as a bear and means it.

At the very top of the class you stop being limited at all: you cast spells while transformed (**Beast Spells**), shrug off material components (**Archdruid**), and Wild Shape without limit.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d8 (per druid level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Intelligence, Wisdom.
- **Armor proficiencies:** Light armor, medium armor, shields. **Druids will not wear armor or use shields made of metal** (see Common questions). (PHB p.65)
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Clubs, daggers, darts, javelins, maces, quarterstaffs, scimitars, sickles, slings, spears.
- **Tool proficiencies:** Herbalism kit.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, Survival.
- **Primary ability:** Wisdom (Constitution secondary).

### Starting equipment (PHB p.65)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a wooden shield **or** (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a scimitar **or** (b) any simple melee weapon
- Leather armor, an explorer's pack, and a druidic focus

## Class features

### Druidic (1st level) (PHB p.66)

You know **Druidic**, the secret language of druids. You can speak it and use it to leave hidden messages. You and others who know Druidic automatically spot such a message; anyone else can detect that a message is present with a successful **DC 15 Wisdom (Perception)** check but can't decipher it without magic.

### Spellcasting (1st level) (PHB p.66)

You draw on the divine essence of nature to cast druid spells (general rules in Ch.10; druid spell list in Ch.11). Individual spell descriptions live in `../spells/` — they are not reproduced here.

- **Spellcasting ability:** Wisdom.
  - **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.**
  - **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.**
- **Cantrips:** Know **two** druid cantrips at 1st level; learn more as the Cantrips Known column shows (3 at 4th, 4 at 10th).
- **Prepared spells:** This is a **prepared caster**. After a **long rest**, choose a number of druid spells equal to **your Wisdom modifier + your druid level** (minimum of one), of levels for which you have slots. Preparing a new list takes at least **1 minute per spell level** for each spell on it. Casting a spell doesn't remove it from your prepared list.
- **Spell slots:** Expend a slot of the spell's level or higher to cast a prepared spell; regain all slots on a **long rest**. Slots follow the full-caster column above.
- **Ritual casting:** You can cast a druid spell as a **ritual** if it has the ritual tag **and you have it prepared**.
- **Spellcasting focus:** You can use a **druidic focus** (Ch.5) as your focus for druid spells.

### Wild Shape (2nd level) (PHB p.66)

Use your **action** to magically assume the shape of a **beast you have seen before**. You have **two uses**, regained on a **short or long rest** (and unlimited at 20th level via Archdruid). Your druid level sets which beasts you can become:

#### Wild Shape limits — Beast Shapes table (PHB p.66)

| Druid Level | Max CR | Limitations | Example |
|------|------|------|------|
| 2nd | 1/4 | No flying or swimming speed | Wolf |
| 4th | 1/2 | No flying speed | Crocodile |
| 8th | 1 | — (none) | Giant eagle |

- **Duration:** You can stay transformed for hours equal to **half your druid level (rounded down)**. You then revert unless you spend another use. You can revert early as a **bonus action**, and **automatically** revert if you fall unconscious, drop to 0 HP, or die.
- **Hit points:** You take on the beast's HP and Hit Dice. When you revert, you return to the HP you had before transforming. If you revert because you dropped to **0 HP in beast form**, any **excess damage carries over** to your normal form (e.g., take 10 with 1 HP left → revert and take 9). If the excess doesn't reduce your normal form to 0, you aren't knocked unconscious.
- **Statistics:** Your game stats are replaced by the beast's, **except** you keep your alignment, personality, and **Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma** scores. You keep all your **skill and saving throw proficiencies** and gain the creature's; if both have the same proficiency, **use the higher bonus**. You **can't use** the creature's legendary or lair actions.
- **Actions and spells:** You **can't cast spells** (until Beast Spells at 18th), and speech / hands-required actions are limited to the form's capabilities. Transforming **doesn't break concentration** and doesn't stop you taking actions that are part of an already-cast spell (e.g., *call lightning*).
- **Class features and senses:** You keep features from class, race, or other sources and can use them if the form is physically capable. You **can't use your special senses** (e.g., darkvision) unless the new form has that sense too.
- **Equipment:** You choose whether your gear falls to the ground, **merges into** the new form, or is **worn** by it. Worn gear works normally if the DM rules it practical for the form's shape/size; gear that can't be worn falls or merges. Merged equipment has **no effect** until you leave the form.

### Druid Circle (2nd level) (PHB p.66)

Choose the **Circle of the Land** or the **Circle of the Moon** (see Druid Circles below). Your choice grants features at **2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th** level.

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.67)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that option.

### Timeless Body (18th level) (PHB p.67)

Primal magic slows your aging: for every **10 years** that pass, your body ages only **1 year**.

### Beast Spells (18th level) (PHB p.67)

You can cast many of your druid spells **while in Wild Shape**. You can perform the **verbal and somatic** components of a druid spell in beast form, but you **can't provide material components**.

### Archdruid (20th level) (PHB p.67–68)

You can use **Wild Shape an unlimited number of times**. Additionally, you can **ignore the verbal and somatic components** of your druid spells, as well as any **material components that lack a cost and aren't consumed** by the spell — in both your normal form and your beast form.

## Druid Circles

Chosen at 2nd level.

### Circle of the Land (PHB p.68–69)

Mystics and sages who safeguard ancient knowledge; their magic is shaped by the land where they were initiated.

- **Bonus Cantrip (2nd level).** Learn **one additional** druid cantrip of your choice.
- **Natural Recovery (2nd level).** During a **short rest**, recover expended spell slots with a combined level **≤ half your druid level (rounded up)**; none can be **6th level or higher**. Usable once per **long rest**. (E.g., a 4th-level druid recovers up to 2 levels of slots — one 2nd-level slot or two 1st-level slots.)
- **Circle Spells (3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th level).** Choose your land type at 2nd level. You gain the listed spells at the indicated druid levels; they are **always prepared** and **don't count** against your prepared limit. A circle spell that isn't on the druid list still counts as a druid spell for you.

  | Land type | 3rd | 5th | 7th | 9th |
  |------|------|------|------|------|
  | **Arctic** | hold person, spike growth | sleet storm, slow | freedom of movement, ice storm | commune with nature, cone of cold |
  | **Coast** | mirror image, misty step | water breathing, water walk | control water, freedom of movement | conjure elemental, scrying |
  | **Desert** | blur, silence | create food and water, protection from energy | blight, hallucinatory terrain | insect plague, wall of stone |
  | **Forest** | barkskin, spider climb | call lightning, plant growth | divination, freedom of movement | commune with nature, tree stride |
  | **Grassland** | invisibility, pass without trace | daylight, haste | divination, freedom of movement | dream, insect plague |
  | **Mountain** | spider climb, spike growth | lightning bolt, meld into stone | stone shape, stoneskin | passwall, wall of stone |
  | **Swamp** | darkness, Melf's acid arrow | water walk, stinking cloud | freedom of movement, locate creature | insect plague, scrying |
  | **Underdark** | spider climb, web | gaseous form, stinking cloud | greater invisibility, stone shape | cloudkill, insect plague |

- **Land's Stride (6th level).** Moving through **nonmagical difficult terrain** costs no extra movement; you pass through nonmagical plants without being slowed and without taking damage from thorns/spines/similar hazards. You also have **advantage on saving throws** against plants that are magically created/manipulated to impede movement (e.g., *entangle*).
- **Nature's Ward (10th level).** You **can't be charmed or frightened by elementals or fey**, and you are **immune to poison and disease**.
- **Nature's Sanctuary (14th level).** When a **beast or plant creature** attacks you, it must make a **Wisdom saving throw** against your druid spell save DC. On a failure, it must choose a **different target** or the attack **automatically misses**; on a success, it's immune to this effect for **24 hours**. The creature is aware of the effect before it attacks.

### Circle of the Moon (PHB p.69)

Fierce guardians of the wilds who specialize in combat-focused, ever-changing beast forms.

- **Combat Wild Shape (2nd level).** Use **Wild Shape as a bonus action** (instead of an action). Additionally, while transformed, you can use a **bonus action to expend a spell slot** to regain **1d8 HP per level of the slot** expended.
- **Circle Forms (2nd level).** Starting at 2nd level you can Wild Shape into a beast of **CR as high as 1** (you ignore the Max CR column of the Beast Shapes table but still obey its **other** limitations — e.g., no flying speed at low level). Starting at **6th level**, you can transform into a beast of **CR up to your druid level ÷ 3 (rounded down)**.
- **Primal Strike (6th level).** Your **attacks in beast form count as magical** for overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.
- **Elemental Wild Shape (10th level).** You can **expend two uses of Wild Shape at once** to transform into an **air, earth, fire, or water elemental**.
- **Thousand Forms (14th level).** You can cast **alter self at will**.

#### Circle of the Moon CR-by-level (derived from Circle Forms)

CR cap = max(1, floor(level ÷ 3)) from 6th level on; the "no flying/swimming" beast-shape limits still apply by druid level.

| Druid Level | Circle of the Moon Max CR |
|------|------|
| 2–5 | 1 |
| 6–8 | 2 |
| 9–11 | 3 |
| 12–14 | 4 |
| 15–17 | 5 |
| 18–20 | 6 |

## Common questions

**Q: Why can't a druid wear metal armor — is it a hard rule or flavor?**
A: The PHB states druids "will not wear armor or use shields made of metal" (PHB p.65). It is a class taboo, not a mechanical penalty — the book gives no stated consequence for breaking it, so enforcement is ultimately a **DM ruling**. By the rules as written there's no spell-failure or damage; most tables treat it as a roleplay restriction the druid won't violate. (PHB p.65)

**Q: How many spells can a druid prepare, and how is it different from "known"?**
A: A druid **prepares** spells each long rest — **Wisdom modifier + druid level** spells (minimum one) from the entire druid list, plus all cantrips known. There is no fixed "spells known" list; you can swap your whole prepared list on a long rest. Circle of the Land circle spells are **always prepared on top of** that limit. (PHB p.66)

**Q: Can I cast spells while in Wild Shape?**
A: Not until **18th level (Beast Spells)**. Before then you can't cast while transformed, though you can keep **concentration** on a spell cast beforehand and finish actions that are part of an already-cast spell (like *call lightning*). With Beast Spells you can provide verbal/somatic components but **not material** ones. (PHB p.66–67)

**Q: What happens to my hit points when I revert from Wild Shape?**
A: You return to the HP you had before transforming. If you reverted because the **beast form** hit 0 HP, **excess damage** carries over to your normal form; if that excess doesn't drop you to 0, you stay conscious. Reverting voluntarily or on the timer doesn't change your normal-form HP. (PHB p.66–67)

**Q: How high a CR can a Circle of the Moon druid's beast form be?**
A: CR 1 at levels 2–5, then **druid level ÷ 3 rounded down** from 6th on (CR 2 at 6th, CR 3 at 9th, … CR 6 at 18th+). The "no flying/swimming speed" beast-shape limits by level **still apply** even though the Max CR column is ignored. (PHB p.69)

**Q: Can I use Natural Recovery to get back a high-level slot?**
A: No — the recovered slots' **combined level** can't exceed half your druid level (rounded up), and **none** can be **6th level or higher**. It's once per long rest, on a short rest. (PHB p.68)
