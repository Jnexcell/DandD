# Fighter

The master of weapons and armor — the most flexible martial class. Built around a chosen **Fighting Style**, burst actions (**Action Surge**, **Second Wind**), the most **Extra Attacks** of any class, and the most **Ability Score Improvements**.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Fighter (printed pages 70–75).

---

## Flavor & lore

A fighter is anyone who has made a life's study of combat itself — the soldier and the duelist, the knight and the mercenary, the gladiator and the bandit captain alike. Where other warriors lean on rage or divine oaths or arcane tricks, the fighter's edge is sheer mastery of arms and armor: there is no weapon they can't pick up and wield well, no piece of armor they can't fight in, no battlefield situation they haven't drilled for. They are the most martially flexible class in the game, and the most customizable — more Ability Score Improvements (and thus more feats) than anyone, and a Fighting Style chosen on day one to lean into archery, dueling, two-weapon work, or stalwart defense.

In a party the fighter is the dependable front line — the one who holds the chokepoint, soaks the hits, and reliably puts a weapon into whatever needs to die. They scale into a flurry of blows that no one else can match: more Extra Attacks than any class (two, then three, then four swings on the Attack action), backed by burst tools that turn a single round into something devastating. **Action Surge** lets them cram an entire extra action into a turn — twice the attacks, a clutch second spell for an Eldritch Knight, or a sudden Dash to close the gap. **Second Wind** patches them up mid-fight without help, and **Indomitable** lets them shrug off a save that would have floored anyone else.

The fantasy is competence under pressure. A fighter doesn't win because the gods favor them or because they tapped some inner well of power — they win because they are simply *better at this* than the person across the steel from them, and they keep getting better every level. Pick a subclass to flavor that mastery: the **Champion** who hits harder and crits more often, the **Battle Master** who fights like a chess grandmaster with a toolbox of maneuvers, or the **Eldritch Knight** who weaves a sword arm and a spellbook into one.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d10 (per fighter level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 10 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d10 (or 6) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Strength, Constitution.
- **Armor proficiencies:** All armor, shields.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Simple weapons, martial weapons.
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, Survival.
- **Primary ability:** Strength **or** Dexterity (Constitution secondary; Intelligence if Eldritch Knight).

### Starting equipment (PHB p.72)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) chain mail **or** (b) leather armor, longbow, and 20 arrows
- (a) a martial weapon and a shield **or** (b) two martial weapons
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts **or** (b) two handaxes
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack **or** (b) an explorer's pack

## Class features

### Fighting Style (1st level) (PHB p.72)

Choose one specialty. You can't pick the same option twice, even if a later feature lets you choose again.

- **Archery.** +2 bonus to attack rolls with **ranged** weapons.
- **Defense.** +1 to **AC** while wearing armor.
- **Dueling.** +2 to **damage** rolls when wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapon.
- **Great Weapon Fighting.** When you roll a **1 or 2** on a damage die for a melee weapon wielded with **two hands** (must have the two-handed or versatile property), reroll it and take the new result (even if it's another 1 or 2).
- **Protection.** When a creature attacks a target other than you within **5 feet** of you, use your **reaction** to impose **disadvantage** on that attack roll. Requires a **shield**.
- **Two-Weapon Fighting.** Add your **ability modifier to the damage** of your off-hand (second) attack.

### Second Wind (1st level) (PHB p.72)

On your turn, use a **bonus action** to regain **1d10 + fighter level** hit points. Once used, you must finish a **short or long rest** before using it again.

### Action Surge (2nd level) (PHB p.72)

On your turn, take **one additional action** on top of your regular action (and possible bonus action). One use; recharges on a **short or long rest**. At **17th level** you get **two uses**, but only **one** per turn.

### Martial Archetype (3rd level) (PHB p.72)

Choose **Champion**, **Battle Master**, or **Eldritch Knight** (see Subclasses). Grants features at **3rd, 7th, 10th, 15th, and 18th level**.

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 6th, 8th, 12th, 14th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.72)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that option. (Seven ASIs total — the most of any class.)

### Extra Attack (5th level) (PHB p.72)

Attack **twice** when you take the Attack action. Increases to **three** attacks at 11th level and **four** at 20th level.

### Indomitable (9th level) (PHB p.72)

When you fail a **saving throw**, you can **reroll** it (must use the new roll). One use; recharges on a **long rest**. **Two** uses at 13th level, **three** at 17th level.

## Subclasses (Martial Archetypes)

Chosen at 3rd level.

### Champion (PHB p.72–73)

Focuses on raw physical power and reliable, low-overhead lethality.

- **Improved Critical (3rd level).** Your weapon attacks score a **critical hit on a 19 or 20**.
- **Remarkable Athlete (7th level).** Add **half your proficiency bonus (rounded up)** to any **Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution** check that doesn't already use your proficiency bonus. Also, your running long jump distance increases by a number of feet equal to your **Strength modifier**.
- **Additional Fighting Style (10th level).** Choose a **second** option from the Fighting Style feature.
- **Superior Critical (15th level).** Your weapon attacks score a **critical hit on 18–20**.
- **Survivor (18th level).** At the start of each of your turns, regain **5 + your Constitution modifier** HP if you have **no more than half** your HP left (and more than 0). No benefit at 0 HP.

### Battle Master (PHB p.73–74)

A tactician who fuels special combat **maneuvers** with **superiority dice**.

- **Combat Superiority (3rd level).**
  - **Maneuvers:** Learn **three** maneuvers (list below). Only one maneuver per attack. Learn **two more** at 7th, 10th, and 15th level (8 known at 15th), and you may **swap one** known maneuver each time you learn new ones.
  - **Superiority dice:** Four **d8s**. A die is expended when used; regain all on a **short or long rest**. Gain **one more** die at 7th level and one more at 15th (six dice total).
  - **Maneuver save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier** (your choice).
- **Student of War (3rd level).** Gain proficiency with one type of **artisan's tools** of your choice.
- **Know Your Enemy (7th level).** Spend **1 minute** observing/interacting with a creature outside combat to learn whether it is your **equal, superior, or inferior** in **two** of: Strength score, Dexterity score, Constitution score, AC, current HP, total class levels, fighter class levels.
- **Improved Combat Superiority (10th level).** Superiority dice become **d10s**. They become **d12s** at **18th level**.
- **Relentless (15th level).** When you roll initiative and have **no superiority dice left**, you regain **one**.

**The 16 Maneuvers (PHB p.74):** Each adds the superiority die to the attack's damage (unless noted). Save-based maneuvers use your Maneuver save DC.

- **Commander's Strike** — forgo one attack; spend a bonus action to let an ally use its reaction to make one weapon attack (adds the die to that attack's damage).
- **Disarming Attack** — on hit, target makes a **Str save** or drops one held item (lands at its feet).
- **Distracting Strike** — on hit, the next attack against the target by another attacker (before your next turn) has **advantage**.
- **Evasive Footwork** — when you move, add the die roll to your **AC** until you stop moving.
- **Feinting Attack** — bonus action; gain **advantage** on your next attack this turn against a creature within 5 feet (adds the die to that attack's damage on a hit).
- **Goading Attack** — on hit, target makes a **Wis save** or has **disadvantage** on attacks against anyone but you until your next turn.
- **Lunging Attack** — when you make a melee attack, **increase reach by 5 feet** for it.
- **Maneuvering Attack** — on hit, an ally can use its reaction to move up to **half its speed** without provoking opportunity attacks from your target.
- **Menacing Attack** — on hit, target makes a **Wis save** or is **frightened** of you until your next turn.
- **Parry** — reaction when a melee attack damages you; **reduce the damage** by the die roll + your **Dexterity modifier**.
- **Precision Attack** — add the die to a weapon **attack roll** (before or after rolling, before effects apply).
- **Pushing Attack** — on hit, a Large-or-smaller target makes a **Str save** or is pushed up to **15 feet** away.
- **Rally** — bonus action; an ally gains **temporary HP** equal to the die roll + your **Charisma modifier**.
- **Riposte** — reaction when a creature **misses** you with a melee attack; make a melee weapon attack against it.
- **Sweeping Attack** — on a melee hit, choose a second creature within 5 feet of the target and in reach; if your original roll would hit it, it takes damage equal to the die roll (same type as the original attack).
- **Trip Attack** — on hit, a Large-or-smaller target makes a **Str save** or is knocked **prone**.

### Eldritch Knight (PHB p.74–75)

Blends martial skill with **wizard** magic, focused on **abjuration** and **evocation**.

- **Spellcasting (3rd level).** You cast **wizard** spells using **Intelligence** as your spellcasting ability (third-caster progression).
  - **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier.**
  - **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier.**
  - **Cantrips:** Learn **two** wizard cantrips at 3rd; a third at 10th level.
  - **Spells known:** Start with **three** 1st-level wizard spells, **two** of which must be **abjuration or evocation**. Most spells learned thereafter must be abjuration/evocation; the spells gained at **8th, 14th, and 20th level** can be from **any school**. You may swap one known spell when you gain a fighter level.
  - **Spell slots:** Per the Eldritch Knight Spellcasting table; recharge on a **long rest**. Reaches up to **4th-level slots** by 19th level.

  > **Which spells?** The Eldritch Knight draws from the **wizard** spell list (Intelligence-based, starting at fighter level 3), so there's no separate EK list — you pick from the wizard's spells, weighted heavily toward **abjuration & evocation** (the warding and blasting schools), with a couple of free-choice picks from any school at 8th/14th/20th level. As a one-third caster you only ever reach **1st–4th-level** spells. For options and descriptions see the **Wizard** class file's spell list in [`wizard.md`](wizard.md) and the spell entries in [`../spells/`](../spells/) (e.g. [`../spells/cantrips.md`](../spells/cantrips.md), filtering to 1st–4th level).
- **Weapon Bond (3rd level).** Through a 1-hour ritual (doable during a short rest) you bond with a weapon. You **can't be disarmed** of a bonded weapon unless incapacitated, and you can **summon it as a bonus action** (it teleports to your hand if on the same plane). You can bond **up to two** weapons but summon only one per bonus action.
- **War Magic (7th level).** When you use your **action to cast a cantrip**, you can make **one weapon attack as a bonus action**.
- **Eldritch Strike (10th level).** When you **hit** a creature with a weapon attack, it has **disadvantage on the next saving throw** it makes against a spell you cast before the end of your next turn.
- **Arcane Charge (15th level).** When you use **Action Surge**, you can **teleport up to 30 feet** to an unoccupied space you can see (before or after the extra action).
- **Improved War Magic (18th level).** When you use your **action to cast a spell**, you can make **one weapon attack as a bonus action**.

## Common questions

**Q: Can I use Action Surge to cast two spells in one turn?**
A: You get an extra action, but the **one-leveled-spell-per-turn rule** still applies: if you cast a leveled spell with one action, the other action can only cast a **cantrip** (with a casting time of 1 action). Two cantrips, or a leveled spell + cantrip, is fine. (PHB p.202 spellcasting rules; mainly relevant to Eldritch Knight.)

**Q: Does Two-Weapon Fighting style let me make an extra attack?**
A: No. The bonus-action off-hand attack comes from the **two-weapon fighting rules**, not the style. The style only **adds your ability modifier to the off-hand damage** (normally you'd add nothing). (PHB p.72, p.195)

**Q: Can a Battle Master use a maneuver and Action Surge together?**
A: Yes — they're independent. Action Surge gives extra attacks; you can attach a maneuver to attacks (one maneuver per attack, limited by your superiority dice). (PHB p.72–73)

**Q: How many attacks does a Fighter get at high level, and do maneuvers/spells replace them?**
A: Extra Attack gives 2 attacks at 5th, **3 at 11th, 4 at 20th**. Maneuvers modify attacks you already make rather than adding attacks (except Commander's Strike, which *replaces* one of your attacks with an ally's). (PHB p.72, p.73)

**Q: Does Improved Critical stack with a magic weapon or other crit-range effects?**
A: Improved Critical (19–20) and Superior Critical (18–20) set your crit range; they don't stack with each other (Superior replaces Improved). Crit-range effects from other sources generally don't stack to expand the range further unless explicitly stated. (PHB p.72–73; DM discretion on third-party stacking.)
