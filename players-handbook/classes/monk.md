# Monk

A disciplined martial artist who channels the mystical energy of **ki** to strike with blinding speed, deflect attacks, move with supernatural agility, and bend body and mind to perfection.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes," Monk (printed pages 76–81).

---

## Flavor & lore

A monk is a martial artist who has trained body and spirit into a single weapon — the wandering ascetic, the temple-trained warrior, the disciplined seeker who has learned to tap **ki**, the mystical energy that flows through all living things. They don't rely on heavy armor, big weapons, or magic spells; their power comes from precision, speed, and self-mastery. Striking with fists, feet, and simple weapons, a well-trained monk turns their own body into a blur of blows that armored fighters can scarcely keep up with.

In a party the monk is the fast, mobile skirmisher — the one who darts across the battlefield, harasses spellcasters, and locks down dangerous enemies. **Unarmored Defense** and a Dexterity-and-Wisdom build let them go without armor while staying nimble and hard to hit; **Martial Arts** turns their unarmed strikes deadly and grants a bonus-action flurry of attacks; and **Ki** fuels a versatile toolkit — extra attacks (**Flurry of Blows**), defensive footwork (**Patient Defense**), and a burst of speed (**Step of the Wind**). Climbing speed, ever-growing movement, **Deflect Missiles** to swat arrows out of the air, and **Stunning Strike** to drop a key foe make them slippery in and out of melee both.

The fantasy is the disciplined, supernaturally agile warrior who needs no sword and no spellbook — only a perfected body and a calm mind. A monk wins by being everywhere the enemy isn't ready for, striking faster than they can answer and walking through hazards that would stop anyone else. Pick a subclass to focus that discipline: the **Way of the Open Hand**, the pure pinnacle of martial-arts technique; the **Way of Shadow**, a ninja-like path of stealth and darkness; or the **Way of the Four Elements**, channeling ki into elemental power.

## Class basics

| | |
|---|---|
| **Hit Die** | d8 per monk level |
| **HP at 1st level** | 8 + your Constitution modifier |
| **HP at higher levels** | 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per level after 1st |
| **Armor** | None |
| **Weapons** | Simple weapons, shortswords |
| **Tools** | Choose one type of artisan's tools or one musical instrument |
| **Saving throws** | Strength, Dexterity |
| **Skills** | Choose **two** from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, and Stealth |

**Starting equipment** (in addition to background; PHB p.78):

- (a) a shortsword or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- 10 darts

> **Monk weapons** are shortswords and any simple **melee** weapons that lack the **two-handed** or **heavy** property. Many monk benefits apply only while you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons, and wear no armor and no shield.

## Class features

### Unarmored Defense (1st) (PHB p.78)

While you wear **no armor and no shield**, your AC equals **10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Wisdom modifier**.

### Martial Arts (1st) (PHB p.78)

While you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons and wear no armor or shield, you gain these benefits:

- **Dexterity for attacks/damage.** You can use **Dexterity instead of Strength** for the attack and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons.
- **Martial Arts die.** You can roll a **d4** in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strike or monk weapon. This die grows with your level (see the Martial Arts column).
- **Bonus unarmed strike.** When you take the **Attack action** with an unarmed strike or a monk weapon, you can make **one unarmed strike as a bonus action** (assuming you haven't already used your bonus action this turn).

### Ki (2nd) (PHB p.78)

Your training lets you harness **ki**, represented by a pool of **ki points equal to your monk level**. You start knowing three ki features: **Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, and Step of the Wind**, and learn more as you level. Spent ki returns when you finish a **short or long rest**, provided you spend at least **30 minutes of the rest meditating**.

Some ki features force a saving throw. The **ki save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier**.

- **Flurry of Blows.** Immediately after you take the **Attack action**, spend **1 ki point** to make **two unarmed strikes as a bonus action**.
- **Patient Defense.** Spend **1 ki point** to take the **Dodge** action as a bonus action.
- **Step of the Wind.** Spend **1 ki point** to take the **Disengage or Dash** action as a bonus action, and your **jump distance is doubled** for the turn.

### Unarmored Movement (2nd) (PHB p.78)

Your speed increases by **10 feet** while you wear no armor and no shield; this bonus grows at higher levels (up to **+30 ft.** at 18th). At **9th level**, you also gain the ability to **move along vertical surfaces and across liquids** on your turn without falling during the move.

### Monastic Tradition (3rd) (PHB p.78)

You commit to a tradition — **Way of the Open Hand, Way of Shadow, or Way of the Four Elements** (detailed below). Your tradition grants features at **3rd, 6th, 11th, and 17th level**.

### Deflect Missiles (3rd) (PHB p.78)

When hit by a **ranged weapon attack**, you can use your **reaction** to deflect or catch the missile, reducing the damage by **1d10 + your Dexterity modifier + your monk level**. If you reduce the damage to **0** and the missile is small enough to hold in one hand (and you have a free hand), you catch it. You can then spend **1 ki point** to make a **ranged attack** with the caught weapon/ammunition as part of the same reaction (proficient, and the missile counts as a monk weapon).

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.78)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). A feat can be taken instead if your DM uses that optional rule.

### Slow Fall (4th) (PHB p.78)

You can use your **reaction** when you fall to reduce the falling damage you take by **five times your monk level**.

### Extra Attack (5th) (PHB p.79)

You can **attack twice**, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

### Stunning Strike (5th) (PHB p.79)

When you hit a creature with a **melee weapon attack**, you can spend **1 ki point** to attempt a stunning strike. The target must succeed on a **Constitution saving throw** or be **stunned until the end of your next turn**.

### Ki-Empowered Strikes (6th) (PHB p.79)

Your **unarmed strikes count as magical** for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

### Evasion (7th) (PHB p.79)

When subjected to an effect that allows a **Dexterity saving throw for half damage**, you take **no damage on a success** and **only half on a failure**.

### Stillness of Mind (7th) (PHB p.79)

You can use your **action** to end one effect on yourself that is causing you to be **charmed or frightened**.

### Purity of Body (10th) (PHB p.79)

Your mastery of ki makes you **immune to disease and poison**.

### Tongue of the Sun and Moon (13th) (PHB p.79)

You **understand all spoken languages**, and any creature that can understand a language can understand what you say.

### Diamond Soul (14th) (PHB p.79)

You gain **proficiency in all saving throws**. Whenever you fail a saving throw, you can spend **1 ki point to reroll it** and take the second result.

### Timeless Body (15th) (PHB p.79)

You suffer none of the frailty of old age and can't be aged magically (you can still die of old age). You also **no longer need food or water**.

### Empty Body (18th) (PHB p.79)

You can use your **action** to spend **4 ki points** to become **invisible for 1 minute**, during which you also have **resistance to all damage but force damage**. Additionally, you can spend **8 ki points** to cast **astral projection** (no material components), but you can't take any other creatures with you.

### Perfect Self (20th) (PHB p.79)

When you roll for initiative and have **no ki points remaining**, you **regain 4 ki points**.

## Subclasses

A monk chooses a Monastic Tradition at 3rd level, gaining features at 3rd, 6th, 11th, and 17th level.

### Way of the Open Hand (PHB p.79)

The ultimate masters of martial-arts combat, armed or unarmed.

- **Open Hand Technique (3rd).** Whenever you hit a creature with one of the attacks granted by your **Flurry of Blows**, you can impose one of these effects on it:
  - It must succeed on a **Dexterity saving throw** or be **knocked prone**.
  - It must make a **Strength saving throw**; on a failure you **push it up to 15 feet** away from you.
  - It **can't take reactions** until the end of your next turn.
- **Wholeness of Body (6th).** As an **action**, you regain hit points equal to **three times your monk level**. You must finish a **long rest** before using this again.
- **Tranquility (11th).** At the end of a **long rest**, you gain the effect of a **sanctuary** spell that lasts until the start of your next long rest (it can end early as normal). The spell's save DC equals **8 + your Wisdom modifier + your proficiency bonus**.
- **Quivering Palm (17th).** When you hit a creature with an **unarmed strike**, you can spend **3 ki points** to set up imperceptible vibrations that last a number of days equal to your monk level. While they persist, you can use your **action** to end them (you and the target must be on the same plane): the target makes a **Constitution saving throw**, dropping to **0 hit points** on a failure or taking **10d10 necrotic damage** on a success. You can have only one creature affected at a time, and can end the vibrations harmlessly without an action.

### Way of Shadow (PHB p.80)

Stealth-and-subterfuge monks — ninjas and shadowdancers who serve as spies and assassins.

- **Shadow Arts (3rd).** As an **action**, spend **2 ki points** to cast **darkness, darkvision, pass without trace, or silence** (no material components). You also gain the **minor illusion** cantrip if you don't already know it.
- **Shadow Step (6th).** While in **dim light or darkness**, you can use a **bonus action** to **teleport up to 60 feet** to an unoccupied space you can see that is also in dim light or darkness. You then have **advantage on the first melee attack** you make before the end of the turn.
- **Cloak of Shadows (11th).** While in an area of **dim light or darkness**, you can use your **action** to become **invisible**. You remain invisible until you make an attack, cast a spell, or are in an area of bright light.
- **Opportunist (17th).** Whenever a creature within **5 feet** of you is hit by an attack made by **someone other than you**, you can use your **reaction** to make a **melee attack** against that creature.

### Way of the Four Elements (PHB p.80)

Monks who bend the four elements to their will by focusing their ki.

#### Disciple of the Elements (3rd) (PHB p.81)

You learn magical **elemental disciplines** that spend ki points each time they're used. You know **Elemental Attunement** plus **one** other discipline of your choice, and learn an **additional discipline at 6th, 11th, and 17th level**. When you learn a new discipline, you may also **replace one** known discipline with another.

- **Casting elemental spells.** Some disciplines let you cast spells (using the spell's casting time and rules, but no material components needed).
- **Augmenting at higher levels.** From **5th level**, if a discipline's spell has an enhanced effect at higher levels (like *burning hands*), you can spend **extra ki points** to raise the spell's level — **+1 spell level per additional ki point**. The maximum total ki you can put into one spell scales with monk level: **3 ki (5th–8th), 4 ki (9th–12th), 5 ki (13th–16th), 6 ki (17th–20th)**.

## Common questions

**Q: Can a monk Flurry of Blows after using a monk weapon, or only after unarmed strikes?**
A: Flurry triggers off the **Attack action** generally. Take the Attack action (with unarmed strikes or monk weapons), then spend 1 ki for Flurry of Blows to make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action. Note the bonus unarmed strike from Martial Arts and Flurry of Blows both use your **bonus action**, so you choose one per turn (PHB p.78).

**Q: How does the bonus unarmed strike from Martial Arts work each turn?**
A: When you take the Attack action with an unarmed strike or monk weapon, you may make **one** unarmed strike as a bonus action — provided you haven't already spent your bonus action (e.g., on Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, or Step of the Wind) (PHB p.78).

**Q: Does Stunning Strike work on a ranged attack or once per turn only?**
A: Stunning Strike requires a **melee weapon attack** hit, and you can attempt it on **every qualifying hit** as long as you have ki to spend — there's no per-turn cap. Each attempt costs **1 ki point** (PHB p.79).

**Q: Can a monk wield a quarterstaff or spear with Martial Arts benefits?**
A: A quarterstaff and spear are simple melee weapons. A quarterstaff used one-handed qualifies as a monk weapon, but if you wield it two-handed (or use a heavy weapon), it loses monk-weapon status. Spears are fine (simple, not heavy/two-handed). Weapons with the **heavy** or **two-handed** property are never monk weapons (PHB p.78).

**Q: When does a monk get ki back?**
A: Spent ki returns at the end of a **short or long rest**, as long as you spend at least **30 minutes of that rest meditating**. This makes the monk far more rest-efficient than long-rest casters (PHB p.78).
