# Ranger

A wilderness warrior who hunts the monsters that threaten the edges of civilization. Built around **Favored Enemy** and **Natural Explorer** (terrain/quarry expertise), a **Fighting Style**, and nature magic drawn from the druid tradition. A **half-caster** that learns a fixed list of **spells known** using Wisdom, and at 3rd level emulates either the **Hunter** or **Beast Master** archetype.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Ranger (printed pages 89–93).

---

## Flavor & lore

The ranger is the party's **wilderness hunter** — a tracker and skirmisher who patrols the dangerous frontier between civilization and the monsters that prowl beyond it. Equal parts woodsman and warrior, the ranger knows the land in their bones: they read trails, name the creatures by their spoor, and move through hostile terrain like it's home ground. **Favored Enemy** sharpens their edge against a chosen kind of foe, and **Natural Explorer** turns a favored landscape into a place where they can't get lost, can't be slowed, and always travel with the upper hand.

In play the ranger is a **martial-caster hybrid** built around precision and mobility. They pick a **Fighting Style** (archery, two-weapon, dueling, or defense) and dish out steady, reliable damage — the iconic opener is **Hunter's Mark**, a concentration spell that tacks bonus damage onto every hit against a single target you've zeroed in on. At 3rd level they commit to an archetype: the **Hunter** specializes in techniques for cutting down whatever the wild throws at them, while the **Beast Master** bonds with an animal companion that fights at their side. Either way the ranger thrives on the flanks — striking from cover, controlling the field, picking targets apart.

As a **half-caster**, the ranger draws on the **same nature magic as the druid** but keeps it modest. Spellcasting only switches on at character level 2, the slot list tops out at 5th level, and rather than preparing from a big list the ranger learns a **fixed, small set of spells known** (chosen from the ranger list, Wisdom-based, swappable one at a time on level-up). The magic is utility and edge — tracking, terrain control, a damage buff or two — layered onto a fundamentally martial hunter. The fantasy is the lone scout who always sees the ambush coming, and is already drawing back the bowstring.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d10 (per ranger level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 10 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d10 (or 6) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Strength, Dexterity. *(Note: the brief said Dex & Wis — the PHB grants **Strength & Dexterity** saves.)* <!-- VERIFY: PHB p.89 lists Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity -->
- **Armor proficiencies:** Light armor, medium armor, shields.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Simple weapons, martial weapons.
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **three** from Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, Survival.
- **Primary ability:** Dexterity (or Strength for two-weapon builds); **Wisdom** for spells.

### Starting equipment (PHB p.89)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) scale mail **or** (b) leather armor
- (a) two shortswords **or** (b) two simple melee weapons
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack **or** (b) an explorer's pack
- A longbow and a quiver of 20 arrows

## Class features

### Favored Enemy (1st level) (PHB p.91)

Choose a creature type: **aberrations, beasts, celestials, constructs, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, giants, monstrosities, oozes, plants, or undead** — or **two humanoid races** (e.g., gnolls and orcs). You have **advantage on Wisdom (Survival)** checks to track them and on **Intelligence checks** to recall information about them. You also learn one **language** spoken by that enemy (if any). Choose an **additional** favored enemy at **6th** and **14th** level (with an associated language each time).

### Natural Explorer (1st level) (PHB p.91)

Choose a **favored terrain**: arctic, coast, desert, forest, grassland, mountain, swamp, or the Underdark. When making an **Intelligence or Wisdom check** related to that terrain with a skill you're proficient in, your **proficiency bonus is doubled**. While traveling for an hour or more in favored terrain:

- Difficult terrain doesn't slow your group.
- You can't become lost except by magic.
- You stay alert to danger even while doing another travel task.
- You can move stealthily at normal pace when traveling alone.
- You forage twice as much food.
- While tracking, you learn the creatures' exact number, sizes, and how long ago they passed.

Choose **additional** favored terrains at **6th** and **10th** level.

### Fighting Style (2nd level) (PHB p.91)

Choose one (can't pick the same option twice):

- **Archery.** +2 to attack rolls with **ranged** weapons.
- **Defense.** +1 to **AC** while wearing armor.
- **Dueling.** +2 to **damage** with a melee weapon wielded in one hand and no other weapon.
- **Two-Weapon Fighting.** Add your ability modifier to the **damage of the off-hand (second) attack**.

### Spellcasting (2nd level) (PHB p.91)

You cast ranger spells using **Wisdom** (drawing on attunement to nature, like a druid). Spells are **known**, not prepared.

- **Spells known:** Start with **two** 1st-level spells; learn more per the table. When you gain a ranger level you can **swap one** known spell for another (of a level you have slots for).
- **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.**
- **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.**
- No cantrips. Slots per the table above (half-caster). See `../spells/` and `../10-spellcasting.md` for slot/upcasting rules and the ranger spell list.

### Primeval Awareness (3rd level) (PHB p.92)

Use your **action** and expend a **ranger spell slot** to sense, for **1 minute per slot level**, whether **aberrations, celestials, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, or undead** are present within **1 mile** (or **6 miles** in favored terrain). It does **not** reveal their location or number.

### Extra Attack (5th level) (PHB p.92)

Attack **twice** when you take the Attack action.

### Land's Stride (8th level) (PHB p.92)

Moving through **nonmagical difficult terrain** costs no extra movement, and you can pass through nonmagical plants without being slowed or taking damage from thorns/spines. You have **advantage on saves** against plants magically created or manipulated to impede movement (e.g., *entangle*).

### Hide in Plain Sight (10th level) (PHB p.92)

Spend **1 minute** creating camouflage (needs natural materials). While pressed motionless against a solid surface at least as tall and wide as you, you gain a **+10 bonus to Dexterity (Stealth)** checks. The benefit ends the moment you move or take an action/reaction; you must re-camouflage to use it again.

### Vanish (14th level) (PHB p.92)

Use the **Hide** action as a **bonus action**. You also can't be **tracked by nonmagical means** unless you choose to leave a trail.

### Feral Senses (18th level) (PHB p.92)

Attacking a creature you can't see doesn't impose **disadvantage** on your attack rolls. You also automatically know the location of any **invisible creature within 30 feet**, provided it isn't hidden from you and you aren't blinded or deafened.

### Foe Slayer (20th level) (PHB p.92)

**Once per turn**, add your **Wisdom modifier** to the **attack roll or damage roll** of an attack against one of your **favored enemies** (decide before or after rolling, but before effects apply).

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.92)

Increase one ability by 2, or two by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that rule.

## Subclasses (Ranger Archetypes)

Chosen at 3rd level; grants features at **3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th** level.

### Hunter (PHB p.93)

A bulwark against the terrors of the wild, specialized in techniques tuned to the threats you face. Each feature lets you pick **one option**.

- **Hunter's Prey (3rd) — choose one:**
  - **Colossus Slayer.** When you hit a creature that is **below its HP maximum**, deal an extra **1d8** damage (once per turn).
  - **Giant Killer.** When a **Large or larger** creature within 5 ft hits or misses you with an attack, use your **reaction** to attack it immediately after (if you can see it).
  - **Horde Breaker.** Once per turn when you make a weapon attack, make **another attack** with the same weapon against a **different creature** within 5 ft of the original target and within range.
- **Defensive Tactics (7th) — choose one:**
  - **Escape the Horde.** Opportunity attacks against you have **disadvantage**.
  - **Multiattack Defense.** When a creature hits you, gain **+4 AC** against all its further attacks that turn.
  - **Steel Will.** Advantage on saves against being **frightened**.
- **Multiattack (11th) — choose one:**
  - **Volley.** Action: make a **ranged** attack against any number of creatures within **10 feet** of a point in range (need ammunition and a separate roll for each).
  - **Whirlwind Attack.** Action: make a **melee** attack against any number of creatures within **5 feet** of you (separate roll for each).
- **Superior Hunter's Defense (15th) — choose one:**
  - **Evasion.** On a Dexterity save for half damage, take **no damage** on a success and **half** on a failure.
  - **Stand Against the Tide.** When a hostile creature **misses** you with a melee attack, use your **reaction** to force it to repeat that attack against another creature of your choice.
  - **Uncanny Dodge.** When an attacker you can see hits you, use your **reaction** to **halve** that attack's damage.

### Beast Master (PHB p.93)

A partnership between ranger and an animal companion that fights as one with you.

- **Ranger's Companion (3rd).** Gain a **beast companion** no larger than **Medium** with a **challenge rating of 1/4 or lower** (examples: hawk, mastiff, panther). Add your **proficiency bonus** to the beast's **AC, attack rolls, and damage rolls**, and to any **saving throws and skills** it's proficient in. Its **HP maximum** is its normal max **or four times your ranger level**, whichever is higher.
  - The beast acts on **your initiative** but takes no action unless you command it. You can verbally tell it where to move (no action). Use your **action** to command it to take the **Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help** action. Once you have **Extra Attack**, you can make **one weapon attack yourself** when you command the beast to Attack.
  - In favored terrain with only the beast, you can move stealthily at normal pace. If the beast dies, spend **8 hours** bonding with a new non-hostile beast.
- **Exceptional Training (7th).** On any turn the beast doesn't attack, use a **bonus action** to command it to take the **Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help** action.
- **Bestial Fury (11th).** The beast can make **two attacks** when you command it to take the Attack action.
- **Share Spells (15th).** When you cast a spell **targeting yourself**, you can also affect the beast if it's within **30 feet**.

## Common questions

**Q: Does Favored Enemy give bonus damage?**
A: No. In the 2014 PHB, Favored Enemy grants only **advantage to track** them and **Intelligence checks** to recall lore, plus a bonus language. Bonus damage against favored enemies only arrives at 20th level via **Foe Slayer**. (PHB p.91, p.92)

**Q: How do the Beast Master companion's attacks scale, and can it attack the same turn I do?**
A: You spend your **action** to command an Attack; before Extra Attack (level 5) you do **not** also attack that turn. From level 5, commanding the beast to Attack lets you make **one** weapon attack yourself. At 11th level the beast makes **two** attacks per Attack command (Bestial Fury). (PHB p.93)

**Q: Does the beast companion add the ranger's proficiency bonus to everything?**
A: It adds your proficiency bonus to **AC, attack rolls, damage rolls**, and to **saves and skills it is already proficient in** — not to saves/skills it isn't proficient in. (PHB p.93)

**Q: Does Foe Slayer's Wisdom bonus apply to every attack?**
A: No — **once per turn**, to either the attack roll or the damage roll (your choice), and only against a **favored enemy**. (PHB p.92)

**Q: Can a ranger swap out known spells, like a sorcerer?**
A: Yes — each time you gain a ranger level you may replace **one** known spell with another from the ranger list (of a level you have slots for). Rangers don't prepare spells; the known list is otherwise fixed. (PHB p.91)
