# Rogue

A skill-and-stealth specialist who exploits enemy vulnerabilities for devastating precision strikes, slips past danger, and brings unmatched versatility to any party.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes," Rogue (printed pages 94–98).

---

## Flavor & lore

A rogue is a specialist in the things that don't come down to brute force — stealth, deception, sleight of hand, lock-picking, trap-spotting, and the perfectly timed strike at an enemy's weak point. Scouts, burglars, spies, assassins, charming con artists, and street-smart survivors all fall under the banner. What unites them is precision and breadth: rogues have more skill proficiencies than anyone, and **Expertise** doubles their bonus on the ones that matter most, turning a good lockpicker or negotiator into a genuinely world-class one. They don't overpower problems — they out-think and out-maneuver them.

In a party the rogue is the answer-to-everything utility piece: the one who scouts ahead unseen, disarms the trap, picks the lock, reads the room in a tense negotiation, and finds the secret door. In a fight they're a precision striker rather than a tank — light on armor and HP, but lethal when they line up a shot. **Sneak Attack** is the heart of it: once per turn, when they have advantage or a flanking ally, they bury a pile of extra dice into a single hit, and that pile only grows with level. **Cunning Action** keeps them slippery, letting them Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a bonus action every turn, so they dart in, strike, and vanish before the enemy can answer — backed later by **Uncanny Dodge** and **Evasion** to slip the worst of what does land.

The fantasy is being the person who always has an out and always knows where to put the knife. A rogue wins by setting the terms of the fight: striking from the shadows, hitting where it hurts, and never being where the danger expects them. Pick a subclass to color that edge — the **Thief**, master of larceny and quick hands; the **Assassin**, who turns surprise into a death sentence; or the **Arcane Trickster**, who laces stealth and theft with a thread of wizardly magic.

|---|---|
| **Hit Die** | d8 per rogue level |
| **HP at 1st level** | 8 + your Constitution modifier |
| **HP at higher levels** | 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per level after 1st |
| **Armor** | Light armor |
| **Weapons** | Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords |
| **Tools** | Thieves' tools |
| **Saving throws** | Dexterity, Intelligence |
| **Skills** | Choose **four** from Acrobatics, Athletics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth |

**Starting equipment** (in addition to background; PHB p.95):

- (a) a rapier or (b) a shortsword
- (a) a shortbow and quiver of 20 arrows or (b) a shortsword
- (a) a burglar's pack, (b) a dungeoneer's pack, or (c) an explorer's pack
- Leather armor, two daggers, and thieves' tools

## Class features

### Expertise (1st, again at 6th) (PHB p.96)

At 1st level, choose **two** of your skill proficiencies — or one skill proficiency and your proficiency with thieves' tools. Your proficiency bonus is **doubled** for any ability check that uses one of the chosen proficiencies. At 6th level you choose **two more** proficiencies (skills or thieves' tools) to gain this benefit.

### Sneak Attack (1st) (PHB p.96)

**Once per turn**, you can deal extra damage to one creature you hit with an attack, provided you have **advantage** on the attack roll. The attack must use a **finesse weapon or a ranged weapon**.

You don't need advantage if a **different enemy** of the target is within **5 feet** of it, that enemy isn't incapacitated, and you **don't have disadvantage** on the attack roll.

The bonus damage is the value in the Sneak Attack column (1d6 at 1st, scaling to 10d6 at 19th). Key limits: once per turn (not once per round — you can Sneak Attack on someone else's turn via a reaction attack), and it requires a qualifying weapon plus either advantage or the flanking-ally condition above.

### Thieves' Cant (1st) (PHB p.96)

You learn **thieves' cant**, a secret mix of dialect, jargon, and code that hides messages inside ordinary-sounding conversation. Only another creature that knows the cant understands such messages; conveying a hidden message takes **four times longer** than saying the idea plainly. You also understand a set of secret signs and symbols (chalk marks, gestures) that warn of danger, mark a thieves' guild's territory, indicate nearby loot, or flag easy marks and safe houses.

### Cunning Action (2nd) (PHB p.96)

Your quickness lets you take a **bonus action** on each of your turns in combat, used only to **Dash, Disengage, or Hide**.

### Roguish Archetype (3rd) (PHB p.96)

You choose an archetype — **Thief, Assassin, or Arcane Trickster** (detailed below). Your archetype grants features at **3rd, 9th, 13th, and 17th level**.

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 10th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.96)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). Rogues get an **extra ASI at 10th**, more than most classes. A feat can be taken instead if your DM uses that optional rule.

### Uncanny Dodge (5th) (PHB p.96)

When an **attacker you can see** hits you with an attack, you can use your **reaction** to **halve** that attack's damage against you.

### Evasion (7th) (PHB p.96)

When you're subjected to an effect that allows a **Dexterity saving throw for half damage** (e.g., a *fireball* or a dragon's breath), you take **no damage on a success** and **only half on a failure**.

### Reliable Talent (11th) (PHB p.96)

Whenever you make an ability check that lets you add your proficiency bonus, you treat a **d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10**. (Applies only to checks where you're proficient.)

### Blindsense (14th) (PHB p.96)

If you can hear, you are aware of the **location of any hidden or invisible creature within 10 feet** of you.

### Slippery Mind (15th) (PHB p.96)

You gain **proficiency in Wisdom saving throws**.

### Elusive (18th) (PHB p.96)

No attack roll has **advantage** against you while you aren't incapacitated.

### Stroke of Luck (20th) (PHB p.96)

If your **attack misses** a target within range, you can turn the miss into a **hit**. Alternatively, if you **fail an ability check**, you can treat the d20 roll as a **20**. Once used, you can't use it again until you finish a **short or long rest**.

## Subclasses

A rogue chooses a Roguish Archetype at 3rd level, gaining features at 3rd, 9th, 13th, and 17th level.

### Thief (PHB p.97)

Larcenous specialists — burglars, treasure hunters, and ruin-delvers.

- **Fast Hands (3rd).** You can use the bonus action from Cunning Action to make a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check, use thieves' tools to disarm a trap or open a lock, or take the **Use an Object** action.
- **Second-Story Work (3rd).** Climbing no longer costs extra movement. When you make a running jump, the distance you cover increases by a number of feet equal to your **Dexterity modifier**.
- **Supreme Sneak (9th).** You have **advantage on a Dexterity (Stealth) check** if you move no more than **half your speed** on the same turn.
- **Use Magic Device (13th).** You **ignore all class, race, and level requirements** on the use of magic items.
- **Thief's Reflexes (17th).** You can take **two turns during the first round** of any combat: one at your normal initiative and a second at your initiative **minus 10**. You can't use this when you are surprised.

### Assassin (PHB p.97)

Trained in the grim art of death — hired killers, spies, and bounty hunters using stealth, poison, and disguise.

- **Bonus Proficiencies (3rd).** You gain proficiency with the **disguise kit** and the **poisoner's kit**.
- **Assassinate (3rd).** You have **advantage on attack rolls** against any creature that **hasn't taken a turn** in the combat yet. In addition, any hit you score against a **surprised** creature is a **critical hit**.
- **Infiltration Expertise (9th).** You can unfailingly create a false identity, spending **seven days and 25 gp** to establish its history, profession, and affiliations. Thereafter, while disguised as that identity, other creatures believe you to be that person until given an obvious reason not to.
- **Impostor (13th).** You can unerringly mimic another person's **speech, writing, and behavior** after studying them for at least **three hours**. The ruse fools casual observers; against a wary creature, you have **advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks** to avoid detection.
- **Death Strike (17th).** When you attack and hit a **surprised** creature, it must make a **Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your Dexterity modifier + your proficiency bonus)**. On a failure, you **double the damage** of your attack against it.

### Arcane Trickster (PHB p.98)

Rogues who enhance stealth and agility with enchantment and illusion magic.

#### Spellcasting (3rd) (PHB p.98)

You gain the ability to cast **wizard spells**, using **Intelligence** as your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.
- **Spell attack modifier** = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.
- **Cantrips:** You learn **three cantrips** — **mage hand** plus two others of your choice from the wizard list (a fourth wizard cantrip at 10th level).
- **Spells known:** You know **three 1st-level wizard spells**, at least **two** of which must be **enchantment or illusion** spells. Each new spell you learn must be enchantment or illusion **except** the spells gained at **8th, 14th, and 20th level**, which can be from any school.
- You can swap one known spell for another wizard spell when you gain a rogue level (subject to the same enchantment/illusion restriction except for the 8th/14th/20th picks).

> **Which spells?** The Arcane Trickster draws from the **wizard** spell list (Intelligence-based, starting at rogue level 3), so there's no separate AT list — you pick from the wizard's spells, weighted toward **enchantment & illusion** (the mind-bending and deceiving schools that suit a trickster), with a couple of free-choice picks from any school at 8th/14th/20th level. As a one-third caster you only ever reach **1st–4th-level** spells. For options and descriptions see the **Wizard** class file's spell list in [`wizard.md`](wizard.md) and the spell entries in [`../spells/`](../spells/) (e.g. [`../spells/cantrips.md`](../spells/cantrips.md), filtering to 1st–4th level).

#### Mage Hand Legerdemain (3rd) (PHB p.98)

When you cast **mage hand**, you can make the spectral hand **invisible** and perform extra tasks with it: stow an object in a container worn or carried by another creature, retrieve an object from such a container, or use **thieves' tools to pick locks and disarm traps at range**. You can perform one of these tasks unnoticed by succeeding on a **Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check contested by the creature's Wisdom (Perception) check**. You can also use the bonus action from **Cunning Action** to control the hand.

#### Magical Ambush (9th) (PHB p.98)

If you are **hidden** from a creature when you cast a spell on it, that creature has **disadvantage** on any saving throw it makes against the spell that turn.

#### Versatile Trickster (13th) (PHB p.98)

As a **bonus action**, you can designate a creature within **5 feet of your mage hand**, gaining **advantage on attack rolls** against that creature until the end of the turn.

#### Spell Thief (17th) (PHB p.98)

Immediately after a creature casts a spell that targets you or includes you in its area, you can use your **reaction** to force it to make a saving throw using its spellcasting ability (DC equals **your** spell save DC). On a failed save, you **negate the spell's effect against you** and **steal the spell** if it is at least 1st level and of a level you can cast (it need not be a wizard spell). For the next **8 hours** you know that spell and can cast it with your slots, and the creature can't cast it. Once used, you must finish a **long rest** to use this again.

## Common questions

**Q: Can a rogue Sneak Attack more than once per round?**
A: Yes. Sneak Attack is limited to **once per turn**, not once per round. Since you can make a reaction attack on another creature's turn (e.g., an opportunity attack, or via a feature like Commander's Strike), you can Sneak Attack again then, as long as all the usual conditions are met (PHB p.96).

**Q: Does Sneak Attack work with a thrown dagger or a shortbow?**
A: Yes. Sneak Attack needs a **finesse or ranged weapon** — daggers (finesse) and bows/crossbows (ranged) all qualify. A longsword or warhammer would not, because they are neither finesse nor ranged (PHB p.96).

**Q: How does Sneak Attack interact with a critical hit?**
A: On a crit you roll **all the Sneak Attack dice twice** (along with the weapon dice), since a critical doubles all damage dice from the attack (PHB p.196, "Critical Hits").

**Q: Can you use Cunning Action to Hide and then Sneak Attack the same turn?**
A: Yes, in the right setup. Hiding as a bonus action can give you the **unseen-attacker advantage** on your attack, satisfying Sneak Attack's advantage condition. Note that attacking reveals your position (PHB p.96, p.194–195).

**Q: Does Uncanny Dodge halve the damage before or after resistance?**
A: Uncanny Dodge halves the attack's damage as a reaction; apply it together with any resistance — the order doesn't change the result for a single halving, but stacking Uncanny Dodge with resistance means halve, then halve again (round down each step) (PHB p.96, p.197).
