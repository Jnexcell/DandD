# Sorcerer

A spellcaster who draws innate magic from a bloodline or cosmic accident. A **full Charisma caster** with a **small spells-known list** but unmatched flexibility through **Sorcery Points**: convert points into spell slots (and back) via **Font of Magic**, and reshape spells with **Metamagic**.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Sorcerer (printed pages 99–104).

---

## Flavor & lore

Sorcerers don't study magic and they don't pray for it — they simply *have* it, the way a fire has heat. The power is woven into their blood, whether from a draconic ancestor, a brush with raw chaos in the womb, a planar accident, or some quirk nobody can fully explain. To a wizard, magic is a craft learned from books; to a sorcerer it's an instinct, closer to breathing than to scholarship. That intuitive bond is why sorcerers know far fewer spells than a wizard ever will, yet bend the ones they do know in ways no spellbook could teach.

**Role in the party.** The sorcerer is the party's burst-damage and control specialist — the caster who turns a single *fireball* into a battlefield-shaping moment, or twins a *haste* across two allies before anyone else has acted. With a tight, hand-picked list of spells and a reservoir of Sorcery Points to spend, you trade a wizard's breadth for sharp, repeatable spikes of power exactly when they matter.

**The fantasy.** You are living magic — a person whose temper might spark static, whose anger could crackle, whose bloodline whispers through every spell. Draconic sorcerers grow scales, breathe a little smoke, and stare down dragons as kin; Wild Magic sorcerers are walking dice rolls who never quite know what their own power will do next.

**How they play.** Pick your handful of spells deliberately, then make each one do more. **Font of Magic** lets you spin Sorcery Points into extra slots (or burn slots back into points), and **Metamagic** reshapes spells on the fly — casting silently with **Subtle Spell**, doubling range, hitting two targets with **Twinned Spell**, or firing a leveled spell as a bonus action via **Quickened Spell**. The skill ceiling is in resource management: knowing *when* to spend points for maximum effect, not just *that* you can.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d6 (per sorcerer level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 6 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d6 (or 4) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Constitution, Charisma.
- **Armor proficiencies:** None.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows.
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, Religion.
- **Primary ability:** Charisma (Constitution secondary).
- **Spellcasting focus:** An arcane focus.

### Starting equipment (PHB p.100)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts **or** (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a component pouch **or** (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack **or** (b) an explorer's pack
- Two daggers

## Class features

### Spellcasting (1st level) (PHB p.100–101)

You cast **sorcerer** spells using **Charisma** as your spellcasting ability. Sorcerers are a **spells-known** full caster — a small fixed repertoire (capping at **15 known**), swapping one spell at a time on level-up.

- **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.**
- **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.**
- **Cantrips:** Start with **four** sorcerer cantrips; scales to 5 at 4th and 6 at 10th (see table).
- **Spells known:** Start with **two** 1st-level spells; learn more per the Spells Known column. Each must be of a level you have slots for. When you gain a sorcerer level, you may **replace one** known spell with another from the sorcerer list.
- **No ritual casting** (sorcerers cannot cast spells as rituals).
- **Spellcasting focus:** An arcane focus.
- Spell descriptions are not reproduced here — see `../spells/` and the sorcerer spell list (PHB p.207).

### Sorcerous Origin (1st level) (PHB p.100)

Choose **Draconic Bloodline** or **Wild Magic** (see Subclasses). Grants features at **1st, 6th, 14th, and 18th level**.

### Font of Magic (2nd level) (PHB p.101)

You gain **Sorcery Points** (equal to your sorcerer level; max as shown on the table). You regain **all spent sorcery points on a long rest**. They power Metamagic and **Flexible Casting**:

- **Creating Spell Slots.** As a **bonus action**, convert sorcery points into **one spell slot** (max 5th level). Costs:

  | Spell Slot Level | Sorcery Point Cost |
  |---|---|
  | 1st | 2 |
  | 2nd | 3 |
  | 3rd | 5 |
  | 4th | 6 |
  | 5th | 7 |

- **Converting a Spell Slot to Sorcery Points.** As a **bonus action**, expend one spell slot to gain sorcery points **equal to the slot's level**.

### Metamagic (3rd, 10th, 17th level) (PHB p.101–102)

Learn **two** Metamagic options at 3rd, plus **one more at 10th and at 17th** (four total). You can use only **one** Metamagic option per spell cast — **except Empowered Spell**, which can stack with one other option.

| Metamagic | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Careful Spell** | 1 SP | On a spell forcing a save, choose up to your **Cha mod** creatures (min 1); they **auto-succeed** on the save against it. |
| **Distant Spell** | 1 SP | **Double** the range of a spell (range 5 ft+); or make a **touch** spell's range **30 feet**. |
| **Empowered Spell** | 1 SP | **Reroll** up to your **Cha mod** damage dice (min 1); use the new rolls. **Can combine** with one other Metamagic on the same spell. |
| **Extended Spell** | 1 SP | **Double** a spell's duration (1 min+), max **24 hours**. |
| **Heightened Spell** | **3 SP** | One target has **disadvantage** on its first save against the spell. |
| **Quickened Spell** | **2 SP** | Change a spell's casting time from **1 action to 1 bonus action**. |
| **Subtle Spell** | 1 SP | Cast a spell with **no verbal or somatic** components. |
| **Twinned Spell** | SP = **spell's level** (1 for a cantrip) | A single-target, non-self spell targets a **second** creature in range. |

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.101)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that option.

### Sorcerous Restoration (20th level) (PHB p.102)

You regain **4 expended sorcery points** whenever you finish a **short rest**.

## Subclasses (Sorcerous Origins)

Chosen at 1st level.

### Draconic Bloodline (PHB p.102–103)

Innate magic from draconic ancestry — durable, with elemental damage synergy.

- **Dragon Ancestor (1st level).** Choose a dragon type; its **damage type** matters for later features. You learn to speak/read/write **Draconic**, and your **proficiency bonus is doubled** on Charisma checks when interacting with dragons.

  | Dragon | Damage | Dragon | Damage |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Black | Acid | Gold | Fire |
  | Blue | Lightning | Green | Poison |
  | Brass | Fire | Red | Fire |
  | Bronze | Lightning | Silver | Cold |
  | Copper | Acid | White | Cold |

- **Draconic Resilience (1st level).** Your **hit point maximum increases by 1** at 1st level and **by 1 again every sorcerer level** (effectively +1 HP/level). Also, while not wearing armor, your **AC = 13 + your Dexterity modifier**.
- **Elemental Affinity (6th level).** When you cast a spell dealing damage of your ancestry's type, add your **Charisma modifier** to one damage roll of that spell. You may also spend **1 sorcery point** to gain **resistance to that damage type for 1 hour**.
- **Dragon Wings (14th level).** As a **bonus action**, sprout dragon wings gaining a **flying speed equal to your current speed**; dismiss them as a bonus action. Can't be used while wearing armor not made to accommodate them.
- **Draconic Presence (18th level).** As an **action**, spend **5 sorcery points** to project an aura of **awe or fear** (your choice) out to **60 feet** for **1 minute** (concentration). Each hostile creature that starts its turn in the aura must succeed on a **Wisdom save** or be **charmed** (awe) or **frightened** (fear) until the aura ends; success grants immunity to the aura for 24 hours.

### Wild Magic (PHB p.103–104)

Chaotic, unpredictable magic — high-risk, high-reward luck manipulation.

- **Wild Magic Surge (1st level).** Immediately after you cast a sorcerer spell of **1st level or higher**, the DM **may** have you roll a **d20**; on a **1**, roll on the **Wild Magic Surge table** (d100, PHB p.104) for a random magical effect. See that table in the PHB — effects range from harmless to fireball-on-yourself.
- **Tides of Chaos (1st level).** Gain **advantage** on one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. Once used, you need a **long rest** to use it again — **but** before then, the DM can have you roll on the Wild Magic Surge table after you cast a 1st-level-or-higher spell, which then **restores** the use.
- **Bend Luck (6th level).** When another creature you can see makes an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, use your **reaction** and spend **2 sorcery points** to roll **1d4** and apply it as a **bonus or penalty** (your choice) to that roll — decided after the roll but before its effects occur.
- **Controlled Chaos (14th level).** Whenever you roll on the **Wild Magic Surge table**, **roll twice** and use **either** result.
- **Spell Bombardment (18th level).** When you roll damage for a spell and roll the **highest possible number** on any die, choose one of those dice, **roll it again**, and add it to the damage. Once per turn.

## Common questions

**Q: Can I make a 6th-level-or-higher spell slot with Font of Magic?**
A: No. Creating Spell Slots caps at **5th level**, and the cost table only goes to 5th (7 SP). You also can't create a slot higher than your sorcerer level allows. (PHB p.101)

**Q: Can I convert points to a slot and that slot back to points in a loop for free spells?**
A: No — it's a net loss. Creating a 1st-level slot costs **2 SP**, but converting a 1st-level slot back gives only **1 SP**. The conversion is intentionally lossy. (PHB p.101)

**Q: Can I use Quickened Spell to cast two leveled spells in one turn?**
A: Not normally. The **one-leveled-spell-per-turn** rule means if you cast a leveled spell as a bonus action (via Quickened), your action that turn can only cast a **cantrip** with a 1-action casting time. Two cantrips, or quickened-leveled + cantrip, is fine. (PHB p.202)

**Q: Can I stack two Metamagic options on one spell?**
A: Generally no — only **one Metamagic per casting**, except **Empowered Spell**, which explicitly can be added even if you already used another Metamagic option on that spell. (PHB p.101–102)

**Q: Does Twinned Spell work on spells like *fireball* or *magic missile*?**
A: No to both. Twinned requires a spell that **targets only one creature** and isn't range self. *Fireball* targets an area (not a single creature), and *magic missile* creates multiple darts (treated as targeting more than one creature), so neither qualifies. (PHB p.102)
