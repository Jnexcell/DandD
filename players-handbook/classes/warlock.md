# Warlock

A Charisma-based occult caster who trades raw spell volume for a small pool of always-max-level slots that **recharge on a short rest**, plus a deeply customizable kit of **Eldritch Invocations**, a **Pact Boon**, and once-per-day **Mystic Arcanum** spells. The warlock's magic comes from a bargain with an **Otherworldly Patron** — the Archfey, the Fiend, or the Great Old One. Built around **Pact Magic** (not normal spellcasting), the warlock is the only full-ish caster whose slots reset on a short or long rest.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Warlock (printed pages 105–110).

---

## Flavor & lore

A warlock's magic is bought, not earned. Somewhere in your past you struck a deal with a being of immense, alien power — an **Archfey** of the twilight courts, a **Fiend** of the lower planes, or a **Great Old One** whose very thoughts could crack a sane mind. In exchange for service, devotion, or some price you may not fully understand, your patron loaned you a sliver of its power. That sliver is yours to keep, and it grows as you do, but the bargain colors everything: warlocks are creatures of debt and ambition, walking a line between gift and leash.

**Role in the party.** The warlock is a relentless, short-rest-fueled striker and utility caster. Where other casters ration their slots across a whole day, you spend yours freely and get them back after a breather — so you're rarely tapped out. Your **eldritch blast** keeps firing all day for free, your handful of always-max-level slots deliver outsized control and burst, and your toolkit of invocations lets you fill almost any niche the party lacks.

**The fantasy.** You are the one who paid a terrible price for power and made it work. Maybe you wear the deal lightly, maybe it haunts you — but you cast spells no library taught you, see in the dark, whisper to familiars, conjure a pact weapon from nothing, or read a grimoire of stolen secrets. Every warlock build feels distinct, because invocations and pact boons let you assemble a character no two players design the same way.

**How they play.** Master **Pact Magic** first: you have very few slots, but they all cast at the highest level you can reach and **recharge on a short rest**, so plan your day around resting and spend boldly. Lean on **eldritch blast** (almost always paired with **Agonizing Blast**) as your reliable damage, pick **Eldritch Invocations** to customize your kit, choose a **Pact Boon** — Chain, Blade, or Tome — to set your style, and unlock once-per-day **Mystic Arcanum** spells at high level to reach magic your slots never could.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d8 (per warlock level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Wisdom, Charisma.
- **Armor proficiencies:** Light armor.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Simple weapons.
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from Arcana, Deception, History, Intimidation, Investigation, Nature, Religion.
- **Primary ability:** Charisma (Constitution secondary).
- **Spellcasting ability:** Charisma.

### Starting equipment (PHB p.107)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts **or** (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a component pouch **or** (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a scholar's pack **or** (b) a dungeoneer's pack
- Leather armor, any simple weapon, and two daggers

## Class features

### Otherworldly Patron (1st level) (PHB p.107)

You strike a bargain with an otherworldly being of your choice — the **Archfey**, the **Fiend**, or the **Great Old One** (detailed in [Otherworldly Patrons](#otherworldly-patrons) below). Your choice grants features at **1st, 6th, 10th, and 14th level**, and gives you an **Expanded Spells** list (extra options added to the warlock spell list specifically for you).

### Pact Magic (1st level) (PHB p.107)

This is the warlock's signature, and it works **differently from normal spellcasting**. Read it carefully.

- **Cantrips.** You know **two** warlock cantrips at 1st level, growing to **3** (4th level) and **4** (10th level) per the Cantrips Known column.
- **Spell slots — few, but always max level.** The Warlock table's **Spell Slots** column is a single small number: **1** slot at 1st level, **2** at 2nd, **3** at 11th, and **4** at 17th. That's the *total* number of Pact Magic slots you have at any one time.
- **All slots are the same level.** The **Slot Level** column shows what level those slots are — and *every* slot is that level. It's 1st level (levels 1–2), 2nd (3–4), 3rd (5–6), 4th (7–8), then **5th from level 9 onward**. When you cast a 1st-level spell like *thunderwave*, you spend a slot and automatically cast it as if at the slot's level (so at level 5, *thunderwave* is cast as a 3rd-level spell).
- **Recharge on a SHORT or long rest.** You regain **all** expended Pact Magic slots when you finish a **short OR long rest** — the defining warlock advantage. Two slots that come back every short rest add up to far more casting per adventuring day than the raw count suggests.
- **Spells Known.** At 1st level you know **two** 1st-level warlock spells. The Spells Known column shows when you learn more (up to **15** at level 20). A spell you learn must be of a level you have slots for (i.e., no higher than your current Slot Level). When you gain a warlock level, you may **swap one** known spell for another warlock spell of a level you can cast.
- **Spellcasting ability = Charisma.**
  - **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.**
  - **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.**
- **Spellcasting focus.** You can use an arcane focus for your warlock spells.

> **Pact Magic vs. multiclass spell slots:** Pact Magic slots are tracked **separately** from the spell slots you get from other classes when multiclassing. You can use Pact Magic slots to cast spells you learned from another class and vice versa, but the slots themselves don't merge into one pool. (PHB p.164)

Spell descriptions live in `../spells/` (e.g., `../spells/eldritch-blast.md`, `../spells/hex.md`, `../spells/thunderwave.md`).

### Eldritch Invocations (2nd level) (PHB p.107)

At 2nd level you gain **two** eldritch invocations — fragments of forbidden knowledge that grant lasting magical abilities (see [Eldritch Invocations](#eldritch-invocations) below). The **Invocations Known** column shows the total you have at each level:

| Warlock level | 2 | 5 | 7 | 9 | 12 | 15 | 18 |
|------|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| **Invocations known** | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |

(The count holds steady between those break points — e.g., you have 2 at levels 2–4, 3 at levels 5–6, and so on, ending at **8 by level 18**.) Whenever you gain a warlock level, you may **swap one** invocation you know for another you qualify for.

### Pact Boon (3rd level) (PHB p.107)

Your patron bestows a gift: choose **Pact of the Chain**, **Pact of the Blade**, or **Pact of the Tome** (detailed in [Pact Boons](#pact-boons) below).

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.107)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that option. (Five ASIs total — the standard amount.)

### Mystic Arcanum (11th, 13th, 15th, 17th level) (PHB p.108)

Your patron grants you access to high-level spells your slots could never reach.

- At **11th level**, choose one **6th-level** spell from the warlock spell list as an arcanum.
- At **13th level**, add a **7th-level** spell; at **15th**, an **8th-level** spell; at **17th**, a **9th-level** spell.
- You can cast each arcanum spell **once without expending a spell slot**. You regain all uses of Mystic Arcanum when you finish a **long rest** (not a short rest, unlike your slots).
- These do **not** use Pact Magic slots and can't be upcast or cast more than once per long rest.

### Eldritch Master (20th level) (PHB p.108)

You can spend **1 minute** entreating your patron to regain **all** expended Pact Magic spell slots. Once you use this, you must finish a **long rest** before doing so again. (Effectively a third "short rest's worth" of slots on demand each long rest.)

## Otherworldly Patrons

Chosen at 1st level. Each patron adds an **Expanded Spells** list to your warlock options and grants features at **1st, 6th, 10th, and 14th level**.

### The Archfey (PHB p.108–109)

A fey lord or lady — the Prince of Frost, the Queen of Air and Darkness, Titania, Oberon, Hyrsam, or an ancient hag. A patron of charm, fear, and illusion.

**Archfey Expanded Spells:**

| Spell Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | *faerie fire*, *sleep* |
| 2nd | *calm emotions*, *phantasmal force* |
| 3rd | *blink*, *plant growth* |
| 4th | *dominate beast*, *greater invisibility* |
| 5th | *dominate person*, *seeming* |

- **Fey Presence (1st level).** As an action, each creature in a **10-foot cube** originating from you must make a **Wisdom save** vs. your warlock spell save DC. Those that fail are **charmed or frightened** (your choice) by you until the end of your next turn. Recharges on a **short or long rest**.
- **Misty Escape (6th level).** When you take damage, use your **reaction** to turn **invisible** and **teleport up to 60 feet** to an unoccupied space you can see. You stay invisible until the start of your next turn or until you attack or cast a spell. Recharges on a **short or long rest**.
- **Beguiling Defenses (10th level).** You're **immune to being charmed**. When another creature tries to charm you, you can use your **reaction** to turn it back: that creature must succeed on a **Wisdom save** vs. your spell save DC or be **charmed by you for 1 minute** (or until it takes any damage).
- **Dark Delirium (14th level).** As an action, choose a creature within 60 feet. It makes a **Wisdom save** vs. your spell save DC; on a failure it's **charmed or frightened** (your choice) for **1 minute** or until your **concentration** breaks (as if concentrating on a spell), ending early if it takes any damage. While the illusion lasts, the creature believes it's lost in a misty realm of your design and can see/hear only itself, you, and the illusion. Recharges on a **short or long rest**.

### The Fiend (PHB p.109)

A devil or demon from the lower planes — Demogorgon, Orcus, Asmodeus, Mephistopheles, pit fiends, balors, and the like. A patron of destruction, durability, and dark luck.

**Fiend Expanded Spells:**

| Spell Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | *burning hands*, *command* |
| 2nd | *blindness/deafness*, *scorching ray* |
| 3rd | *fireball*, *stinking cloud* |
| 4th | *fire shield*, *wall of fire* |
| 5th | *flame strike*, *hallow* |

- **Dark One's Blessing (1st level).** When you **reduce a hostile creature to 0 HP**, you gain **temporary HP equal to your Charisma modifier + your warlock level** (minimum 1).
- **Dark One's Own Luck (6th level).** When you make an **ability check or saving throw**, you can add a **d10** to the roll — after seeing the initial roll but before effects apply. Recharges on a **short or long rest**.
- **Fiendish Resilience (10th level).** When you finish a **short or long rest**, choose one **damage type**; you gain **resistance** to it until you choose a different one. Damage from **magical or silver weapons** ignores this resistance.
- **Hurl Through Hell (14th level).** When you **hit a creature with an attack**, you can banish it through the lower planes. It vanishes and, at the **end of your next turn**, returns to its space (or nearest unoccupied space). If the target **isn't a fiend**, it takes **10d10 psychic damage**. Recharges on a **long rest**.

### The Great Old One (PHB p.109–110)

An alien entity from the Far Realm or an elder god — Ghaunadar, Tharizdun, Dendar, Zargon, Great Cthulhu, and other unfathomable beings. A patron of telepathy, mind defense, and domination.

**Great Old One Expanded Spells:**

| Spell Level | Spells |
|------|------|
| 1st | *dissonant whispers*, *Tasha's hideous laughter* |
| 2nd | *detect thoughts*, *phantasmal force* |
| 3rd | *clairvoyance*, *sending* |
| 4th | *dominate beast*, *Evard's black tentacles* |
| 5th | *dominate person*, *telekinesis* |

- **Awakened Mind (1st level).** You can communicate **telepathically** with any creature you can see within **30 feet**. It doesn't need to share a language with you, but it must understand at least one language. (One-way: you speak to its mind; it doesn't automatically reply.)
- **Entropic Ward (6th level).** When a creature makes an **attack roll against you**, use your **reaction** to impose **disadvantage** on it. If the attack **misses**, your **next** attack roll against that creature before the end of your next turn has **advantage**. Recharges on a **short or long rest**.
- **Thought Shield (10th level).** Your thoughts **can't be read** by telepathy or other means unless you allow it. You also gain **resistance to psychic damage**, and whenever a creature deals psychic damage to you, **it takes the same amount** you would have taken.
- **Create Thrall (14th level).** As an action, touch an **incapacitated humanoid**. It becomes **charmed by you** until *remove curse* is cast on it, the charmed condition is removed, or you use this feature again. You can communicate telepathically with the thrall while on the same plane.

## Pact Boons

Chosen at 3rd level. Each produces a special creature or object reflecting your patron's nature.

### Pact of the Chain (PHB p.107)

- You learn the **find familiar** spell and can cast it as a **ritual**; it doesn't count against your spells known.
- When you cast it, you can choose a normal familiar form **or** one of these special forms: **imp, pseudodragon, quasit, or sprite** (creatures otherwise far above a normal familiar). Thematically, sprites/pseudodragons suit the Archfey, imps/quasits the Fiend, and any form suits the Great Old One.
- **Familiar attacks:** When you take the **Attack action**, you can **forgo one of your own attacks** to let your familiar make **one attack of its own** (using its reaction, per the *find familiar* rules — your familiar can't normally attack on its own initiative). Pairs well with the **Voice of the Chain Master** and **Investment of the Chain Master**-style invocations (see below).

### Pact of the Blade (PHB p.107)

- Use your **action** to create a **pact weapon** (any melee weapon form you choose) in your empty hand. You're **proficient** with it while you wield it, and it counts as **magical** for overcoming resistance/immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.
- The weapon disappears if it's **more than 5 feet from you for 1 minute or more**, if you create another, if you dismiss it (no action), or if you die.
- **Bonding a magic weapon:** Spend a **1-hour ritual** (doable during a short rest) holding a magic weapon to make it your pact weapon. You can then **dismiss it into an extradimensional space** and summon it whenever you create your pact weapon. You **can't** bond an **artifact** or a **sentient weapon**. The bond ends if you die, if you bond a different weapon, or if you spend 1 hour breaking it; the weapon reappears at your feet if it was stashed when the bond breaks.
- Key supporting invocations: **Thirsting Blade** (extra attack) and **Lifedrinker** (Cha-mod necrotic damage).

### Pact of the Tome (PHB p.108)

- Your patron gives you a grimoire, the **Book of Shadows**. Choose **three cantrips from any class's spell list**; while the book is on your person you can cast them **at will**, and they don't count against your cantrips known.
- If you lose the book, a **1-hour ceremony** (during a short or long rest) gets a replacement and destroys the old one. The book turns to ash when you die.
- Pairs with **Book of Ancient Secrets** to add ritual spells from any class.

## Eldritch Invocations

Invocations are **customizable upgrades** — you pick them as you level (see the Invocations Known count above) and can **swap one** each time you gain a warlock level. Some have a **prerequisite** (a minimum warlock level and/or a specific Pact Boon); you must meet it to learn the invocation, and you can take it the same level you qualify. **The full list is on PHB p.110–111.** Below are the most-used and notable options.

### Eldritch Blast enhancers (the warlock's core damage)

These require the **eldritch blast** cantrip and turn it from "a cantrip" into the warlock's primary weapon.

- **Agonizing Blast** *(req: eldritch blast)* — Add your **Charisma modifier** to the damage *eldritch blast* deals on a hit (per beam). The single most-taken invocation. (PHB p.110)
- **Repelling Blast** *(req: eldritch blast)* — When you hit a creature with *eldritch blast*, you can **push it up to 10 feet** away in a straight line (per beam). (PHB p.110)
- **Eldritch Spear** *(req: eldritch blast)* — *Eldritch blast*'s range becomes **300 feet**. (PHB p.110)

### Always-on / at-will utility

- **Armor of Shadows** — Cast **mage armor** on yourself at will, no slot or material components. (PHB p.110)
- **Beast Speech** — Cast **speak with animals** at will. (PHB p.110)
- **Devil's Sight** — You **see normally in darkness**, both magical and nonmagical, out to **120 feet**. (Excellent paired with the *darkness* spell.) (PHB p.110)
- **Eldritch Sight** — Cast **detect magic** at will, no slot. (PHB p.110)
- **Eyes of the Rune Keeper** — You can **read all writing**. (PHB p.110)
- **Fiendish Vigor** — Cast **false life** on yourself at will as a 1st-level spell, no slot/components. (PHB p.110)
- **Mask of Many Faces** — Cast **disguise self** at will. (PHB p.110)
- **Misty Visions** — Cast **silent image** at will, no slot/components. (PHB p.111)
- **Whispers of the Grave** *(req: 9th level)* — Cast **speak with dead** at will. (PHB p.111)

### Movement, stealth & senses

- **One with Shadows** *(req: 5th level)* — While in **dim light or darkness**, use your action to become **invisible** until you move or take an action or reaction. (PHB p.111)
- **Ascendant Step** *(req: 9th level)* — Cast **levitate** on yourself at will, no slot/components. (PHB p.110)
- **Otherworldly Leap** *(req: 9th level)* — Cast **jump** on yourself at will, no slot/components. (PHB p.111)
- **Gaze of Two Minds** — Use your action to touch a **willing humanoid** and perceive through its senses (and use its special senses) until the end of your next turn; you can extend the link on later turns while on the same plane. You're blinded and deafened to your own surroundings while doing so. (PHB p.110)
- **Witch Sight** *(req: 15th level)* — You see the **true form** of any shapechanger or creature concealed by illusion/transmutation magic within **30 feet** and in line of sight. (PHB p.111)
- **Visions of Distant Realms** *(req: 15th level)* — Cast **arcane eye** at will. (PHB p.111)
- **Master of Myriad Forms** *(req: 15th level)* — Cast **alter self** at will. (PHB p.111)

### Once-per-long-rest spell invocations (cast using a warlock slot)

Each lets you cast a spell **once** using a warlock spell slot, then recharge on a **long rest**:

- **Thief of Five Fates** — **bane**. (PHB p.111)
- **Bewitching Whispers** — **compulsion**. (PHB p.110)
- **Dreadful Word** *(req: 7th level)* — **confusion**. (PHB p.110)
- **Sign of Ill Omen** *(req: 5th level)* — **bestow curse**. (PHB p.111)
- **Mire the Mind** *(req: 5th level)* — **slow**. (PHB p.111)
- **Sculptor of Flesh** *(req: 7th level)* — **polymorph**. (PHB p.111)
- **Minions of Chaos** *(req: 9th level)* — **conjure elemental**. (PHB p.111)

### Skill & social

- **Beguiling Influence** — Gain **proficiency** in **Deception and Persuasion**. (PHB p.110)

### Pact-Boon-gated invocations

- **Thirsting Blade** *(req: 5th level, Pact of the Blade)* — You can **attack twice**, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action with your pact weapon (the warlock's version of Extra Attack). (PHB p.111)
- **Lifedrinker** *(req: 12th level, Pact of the Blade)* — When you hit with your pact weapon, the target takes **extra necrotic damage equal to your Charisma modifier** (minimum 1). (PHB p.111)
- **Book of Ancient Secrets** *(req: Pact of the Tome)* — Inscribe **two 1st-level ritual spells from any class** into your Book of Shadows and cast them as rituals; you can transcribe more ritual spells you find (level ≤ half your warlock level rounded up; 2 hours and 50 gp per spell level). (PHB p.110)
- **Voice of the Chain Master** *(req: Pact of the Chain)* — Communicate telepathically with your familiar and perceive through its senses on the same plane; you can also **speak through your familiar** in your own voice, even if it can't normally speak. (PHB p.111)
- **Chains of Carceri** *(req: 15th level, Pact of the Chain)* — Cast **hold monster** at will against a celestial, fiend, or elemental without a slot/components (long rest before reusing on the same creature). (PHB p.110)

*(This covers the most-used invocations; the complete ~30-entry list is on PHB p.110–111.)*

## Common questions

**Q: How many spells can a warlock actually cast per day?**
A: More than the tiny slot count implies, because slots **recharge on a short rest**. At level 5 you have 2 third-level slots; take two short rests in a day and that's effectively 6 third-level castings. The trade-off is you can never have more than 4 slots *at once*, and (apart from Mystic Arcanum) they top out at 5th level. Plan around short rests. (PHB p.107)

**Q: What level does *eldritch blast* count as, and how does Agonizing Blast scale?**
A: *Eldritch blast* is a **cantrip**, so it never uses a slot. It fires **1 beam at levels 1–4, 2 at 5–10, 3 at 11–16, and 4 at 17+** (each beam 1d10 force, separate attack roll). **Agonizing Blast** adds your **Charisma modifier to each beam's damage**, and **Repelling Blast**'s push applies **per beam** — so both scale with the number of beams. (PHB p.110; eldritch blast in `../spells/`)

**Q: Can I cast a Mystic Arcanum spell more than once per day, or upcast it?**
A: No. Each Mystic Arcanum spell is cast **once per long rest** at its listed level, **without** using a spell slot — you can't spend a Pact Magic slot to recast it, and you can't upcast it. (Eldritch Master at 20th only refreshes your *Pact Magic slots*, not your Arcanum uses.) (PHB p.108)

**Q: Does Thirsting Blade work with two-weapon fighting or a second Pact-of-the-Blade weapon?**
A: Thirsting Blade gives you **two attacks with the Attack action** using your pact weapon — it functions like Extra Attack. You can use a bonus action for an off-hand attack under the two-weapon fighting rules if you're actually dual-wielding **light** weapons, but Pact of the Blade creates **one** pact weapon at a time, so you'd need a second (non-pact) light weapon to do so, and you'd add your ability mod to the off-hand damage only with the Two-Weapon Fighting style (which warlocks don't get). **Q: Do warlock Pact Magic slots combine with slots from other classes when I multiclass?**
A: No — they're tracked **separately**. You determine Pact Magic slots solely from your warlock level, and your other class's slots from the multiclass spellcaster table. You can cast any spell you know using either type of slot (if the level fits), but the two pools don't merge. Slots from each pool still recharge on their own schedule (Pact Magic on a short or long rest). (PHB p.164)

**Q: Is being charmed/frightened by Fey Presence or Dark Delirium a "save each turn" effect?**
A: No. Fey Presence lasts only **until the end of your next turn** (no repeat save). Dark Delirium lasts **up to 1 minute** but requires **your concentration** and ends if the target **takes any damage** — there's no built-in repeating save, so damaging the target (e.g., with your own eldritch blast) breaks it. (PHB p.109)
