# Wizard

The archetypal arcane scholar — a **full caster** with the broadest, most flexible spell list in the game, drawn from a personal **spellbook**. Wizards prepare spells from that book each day, recover slots with **Arcane Recovery**, and specialize in one of **eight Arcane Traditions** (schools of magic). Fragile (d6 Hit Die, no armor) but unmatched in versatility and high-level magical power.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 3, "Classes" — Wizard (printed pages 112–119).

---

## Flavor & lore

Where a sorcerer is born to magic and a warlock bargains for it, a wizard *earns* it — one painstaking page at a time. Wizards treat the arcane as a science: the world runs on hidden laws, and with enough study, the right gestures, and the right words, those laws can be rewritten. Your power lives in a **spellbook**, a record of every formula you've decoded, copied, or invented. Lose the book and you lose your library (though a careful wizard keeps backups); fill it, and there is almost nothing you cannot learn to do.

**Role in the party.** The wizard is the toolbox — the caster with an answer for every situation, prepared fresh each morning. Need to blast a room, fly the party over a chasm, charm a guard, dispel a curse, or scry on a distant foe? A wizard can usually have the spell ready, because the book holds far more than any one day's loadout. Few in any single fight, but unmatched across a whole adventure for sheer flexibility and ceiling.

**The fantasy.** You are the scholar-mage: ink-stained, sharp-eyed, and quietly the most dangerous person in the room. Your edge isn't muscle or bravado — it's that you've done the reading. An Evoker incinerates; an Abjurer shrugs off magic that would fell anyone else; a Diviner seems to know what's coming because, with **Portent**, they literally do.

**How they play.** Build your spellbook deliberately (two free spells each level, plus any you copy from scrolls and rival wizards), then **prepare** a daily subset from it — the heart of the class is choosing the right loadout for the day ahead. Lean on **ritual casting** to get utility spells for free out of the book, recover slots mid-day with **Arcane Recovery**, and specialize in one of **eight Arcane Traditions** to define your style. You're fragile (d6, no armor), so positioning and smart prep matter as much as raw firepower.

## Class basics

- **Hit Die:** d6 (per wizard level).
- **Hit Points at 1st level:** 6 + your Constitution modifier.
- **Hit Points at higher levels:** 1d6 (or 4) + Con modifier per level after 1st.
- **Saving throw proficiencies:** Intelligence, Wisdom.
- **Armor proficiencies:** None.
- **Weapon proficiencies:** Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows.
- **Tool proficiencies:** None.
- **Skills:** Choose **two** from Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Religion.
- **Primary ability:** Intelligence (Constitution and Dexterity secondary for survivability).

### Starting equipment (PHB p.114)

Plus whatever your background grants:

- (a) a quarterstaff **or** (b) a dagger
- (a) a component pouch **or** (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a scholar's pack **or** (b) an explorer's pack
- **A spellbook** (containing **six 1st-level wizard spells** of your choice)

## Class features

### Spellcasting (1st level) (PHB p.114)

The wizard is a **prepared** full caster who works from a **spellbook**. See the general spellcasting rules in `../10-spellcasting.md` and the wizard spell list / spell entries in `../spells/`.

- **Spellcasting ability:** **Intelligence**.
  - **Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier.**
  - **Spell attack modifier = proficiency bonus + Intelligence modifier.**
- **Cantrips:** Know **three** wizard cantrips at 1st level; more at higher levels per the Cantrips Known column (4 at 4th, 5 at 10th).
- **Spellbook:** Start with a spellbook holding **six 1st-level wizard spells** of your choice. Your spellbook is your repertoire — you can only prepare spells written in it (see "Your spellbook" below).
- **Preparing spells:** After a long rest, choose a number of spells from your spellbook equal to **Intelligence modifier + wizard level** (minimum 1), each of a level for which you have slots. *Casting a prepared spell does not remove it from your prepared list.* Changing the list requires a **long rest** and study time (**at least 1 minute per spell level** for each spell on the new list). (PHB p.114) Do **not** confuse "prepared" with "in your spellbook" — your spellbook usually holds far more spells than you can prepare at once.
- **Casting:** Expend a slot of the spell's level or higher. Slots recharge on a **long rest** (plus Arcane Recovery, below).
- **Ritual casting:** You can cast a wizard spell as a **ritual** if it has the ritual tag and the spell is **in your spellbook** — you do **not** need it prepared. (PHB p.114)
- **Spellcasting focus:** You can use an **arcane focus** (or a component pouch) for your wizard spells. (PHB p.114)

**Your spellbook — copying & learning spells (PHB p.114):**

- **Free spells on level-up:** Each time you gain a wizard level, add **two** wizard spells of your choice to your spellbook (each of a level for which you have spell slots). This represents your own research and is free.
- **Copying found spells:** When you find a wizard spell (on a scroll, in another wizard's book, etc.) of a level for which you have slots, you can copy it into your spellbook. Cost: **2 hours and 50 gp per spell level** (inks and material components). Once copied, you can prepare it like any other.
- **Backup / replacing the book:** Copying a spell from your *own* book into another book costs only **1 hour and 10 gp per spell level**. If you lose your spellbook, you can re-transcribe spells you currently have **prepared** using that same backup procedure; the rest must be re-found as normal. (Many wizards keep a backup book somewhere safe.)
- **School Savant features** (see Arcane Traditions) **halve the gp cost and time** to copy spells **of your school** into your book.

### Arcane Recovery (1st level) (PHB p.115)

Once per day, when you finish a **short rest**, you can recover expended spell slots. The recovered slots can have a **combined level equal to or less than half your wizard level (rounded up)**, and **none of the slots can be 6th level or higher**.

- Example: a 4th-level wizard recovers up to **2 levels** worth of slots — either one 2nd-level slot or two 1st-level slots.
- "Once per day" means once between long rests, on a short rest of your choosing.

### Arcane Tradition (2nd level) (PHB p.115)

Choose one of **eight schools**: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, or Transmutation (detailed below). Your choice grants features at **2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level**.

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th) (PHB p.115)

Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each (max 20). May take a feat instead if your DM uses that option. (Five ASIs total — the standard amount.)

### Spell Mastery (18th level) (PHB p.115)

Choose **one 1st-level** and **one 2nd-level** wizard spell in your spellbook. You can cast those two spells **at their lowest level without expending a spell slot** whenever you have them prepared. To cast either at a higher level, expend a slot as normal. By spending 8 hours of study you can swap one or both for different spells of the same levels.

### Signature Spells (20th level) (PHB p.115)

Choose **two 3rd-level** wizard spells in your spellbook as signature spells. You **always have them prepared** (they don't count against your prepared limit), and you can cast **each one once at 3rd level without expending a slot**. Once you cast one this way, you can't do so again with that spell until you finish a **short or long rest**. Casting at a higher level requires a slot as normal.

## Arcane Traditions

Chosen at 2nd level. Each school grants a **School Savant** feature (halve the gp cost and time to copy spells **of that school** into your spellbook) plus features at **2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level**.

### School of Abjuration (PHB p.115–116)

Protective magic that blocks, banishes, and shields.

- **Abjuration Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **abjuration** spells into your book.
- **Arcane Ward (2nd level).** When you cast an **abjuration spell of 1st level or higher**, you can create a magical ward on yourself that lasts until you finish a long rest. The ward has **HP = twice your wizard level + your Intelligence modifier**. While active, damage you take hits the ward first; overflow damage hits you. While the ward is at 0 HP it can't absorb damage, but its magic remains: whenever you cast a 1st-level-or-higher abjuration spell, the ward **regains HP equal to twice the spell's level**. Once created, you can't create it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Projected Ward (6th level).** When a creature you can see within **30 feet** takes damage, use your **reaction** to have your Arcane Ward absorb that damage instead (overflow hits the warded creature).
- **Improved Abjuration (10th level).** When you cast an abjuration spell that requires an **ability check** as part of casting it (e.g., *counterspell*, *dispel magic*), add your **proficiency bonus** to that check.
- **Spell Resistance (14th level).** You have **advantage on saving throws against spells**, and **resistance to the damage of spells**.

### School of Conjuration (PHB p.116)

Spells that produce objects and creatures out of thin air; teleportation.

- **Conjuration Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **conjuration** spells.
- **Minor Conjuration (2nd level).** Use your **action** to conjure an inanimate, nonmagical object (no larger than **3 feet** on a side, **≤10 lb.**) in your hand or in an unoccupied space within **10 feet** you can see. It's visibly magical, sheds dim light to 5 feet, and disappears after **1 hour**, when you use the feature again, or if it takes any damage.
- **Benign Transposition (6th level).** Use your **action** to teleport up to **30 feet** to an unoccupied space you can see, **or** swap places with a willing Small/Medium creature in range. Recharges on a **long rest** *or* by casting a 1st-level-or-higher conjuration spell.
- **Focused Conjuration (10th level).** While concentrating on a **conjuration** spell, your concentration **can't be broken by taking damage**.
- **Durable Summons (14th level).** Any creature you summon or create with a **conjuration** spell gains **30 temporary HP**.

### School of Divination (PHB p.116–117)

Discernment, remote viewing, foresight.

- **Divination Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **divination** spells.
- **Portent (2nd level).** After a **long rest**, roll **two d20s** and record them. You can **replace** any attack roll, saving throw, or ability check made by you or a creature you can see with one of these foretelling rolls — chosen **before** the roll, **once per turn**. Each foretelling roll is used once; unused rolls are lost at your next long rest.
- **Expert Divination (6th level).** When you cast a **divination spell of 2nd level or higher** using a slot, you **regain one expended spell slot**. The regained slot must be a **lower level** than the spell cast and **no higher than 5th level**.
- **The Third Eye (10th level).** Use your **action** to gain one benefit until you're incapacitated or take a short or long rest (can't reuse until you finish a rest): **Darkvision 60 ft.**, **Ethereal Sight** (see into the Ethereal Plane within 60 ft.), **Greater Comprehension** (read any language), or **See Invisibility** (within 10 ft., in line of sight).
- **Greater Portent (14th level).** You roll **three d20s** for Portent instead of two.

### School of Enchantment (PHB p.117)

Charming and beguiling minds.

- **Enchantment Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **enchantment** spells.
- **Hypnotic Gaze (2nd level).** As an **action**, choose a creature within **5 feet** that can see or hear you; it must succeed on a **Wisdom save** (vs. your spell save DC) or be **charmed** until the end of your next turn — its speed drops to 0 and it's incapacitated and dazed. On later turns you can use your action to maintain it (extends to end of your next turn). The effect ends if you move >5 ft. away, the creature can't see/hear you, or it takes damage. Once it ends (or the target succeeds on the initial save), you can't use this on that creature again until you finish a **long rest**.
- **Instinctive Charm (6th level).** When a creature you can see within **30 feet** makes an **attack roll against you**, use your **reaction** to force a **Wisdom save**; on a failure it must redirect the attack to the **nearest other creature** (not you or itself). On a success, you can't use this on that attacker again until a long rest. Choose to use it **before** knowing if the attack hits. Creatures immune to being charmed are immune to this.
- **Split Enchantment (10th level).** When you cast a **1st-level-or-higher enchantment spell that targets only one creature**, you can have it target a **second** creature.
- **Alter Memories (14th level).** When you cast an enchantment spell to **charm** one or more creatures, you can make one of them **unaware** it was charmed. Additionally, once before the spell ends, you can use your action to try to make that creature **forget** time spent charmed: **Intelligence save** or it loses memories of **1 + your Charisma modifier hours** (minimum 1), up to the spell's duration.

### School of Evocation (PHB p.117–118)

Elemental blasting — fire, cold, lightning, thunder, acid.

- **Evocation Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **evocation** spells.
- **Sculpt Spells (2nd level).** When you cast an **evocation spell that affects other creatures you can see**, choose **1 + the spell's level** of them; the chosen creatures **automatically succeed** on their saves against the spell and take **no damage** if they'd normally take half on a success. (Lets you protect allies inside your own AoE.)
- **Potent Cantrip (6th level).** When a creature **succeeds** on a save against one of your cantrips, it still takes **half the cantrip's damage** (if any) but suffers no additional cantrip effect.
- **Empowered Evocation (10th level).** Add your **Intelligence modifier** to the **damage roll** of any wizard **evocation** spell you cast.
- **Overchannel (14th level).** When you cast a **wizard spell of 5th level or lower that deals damage**, you can deal **maximum damage** with it. The first time per long rest has no cost. Each subsequent use before a long rest deals **2d12 necrotic per spell level** to you (immediately after casting), increasing by **+1d12 per level** each additional time. This self-damage **ignores resistance and immunity**.

### School of Illusion (PHB p.118)

Deceiving senses and mind.

- **Illusion Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **illusion** spells.
- **Improved Minor Illusion (2nd level).** You learn the **minor illusion** cantrip (if you already know it, learn a different wizard cantrip; it doesn't count against your cantrips known). When you cast **minor illusion**, you can create **both a sound and an image** with a single casting.
- **Malleable Illusions (6th level).** When you cast an illusion spell with a duration of **1 minute or longer**, you can use your **action** to change the nature of that illusion (using the spell's normal parameters), provided you can see it.
- **Illusory Self (10th level).** When a creature makes an **attack roll against you**, use your **reaction** to interpose an illusory duplicate — **the attack automatically misses**, then the illusion dissipates. Recharges on a **short or long rest**.
- **Illusory Reality (14th level).** When you cast a **1st-level-or-higher illusion spell**, you can choose one **inanimate, nonmagical** object that's part of the illusion and **make it real** as a **bonus action** while the spell is ongoing. It stays real for **1 minute**. The object **can't deal damage or otherwise directly harm anyone** (e.g., make an illusory bridge real to cross a chasm).

### School of Necromancy (PHB p.118–119)

Life, death, and undeath.

- **Necromancy Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **necromancy** spells.
- **Grim Harvest (2nd level).** **Once per turn**, when you **kill** one or more creatures with a **spell of 1st level or higher**, you **regain HP equal to twice the spell's level** — or **three times** its level if it's a **necromancy** spell. No benefit for killing **constructs or undead**.
- **Undead Thralls (6th level).** Add **animate dead** to your spellbook (if not already there). When you cast it, you can target **one additional** corpse/pile of bones (one extra zombie or skeleton). Any undead you create with a **necromancy** spell gains: **+wizard level to its HP maximum**, and adds your **proficiency bonus to its weapon damage rolls**.
- **Inured to Undeath (10th level).** You have **resistance to necrotic damage**, and your **HP maximum can't be reduced**.
- **Command Undead (14th level).** As an **action**, choose one undead you can see within **60 feet**; it makes a **Charisma save** (vs. your spell save DC). On a success, you can't use this on it again. On a failure, it becomes **friendly and obeys your commands** until you use this feature again. Intelligent undead resist: Int **8+** grants **advantage** on the save; if it fails and has Int **12+**, it can **repeat the save every hour** until it succeeds and breaks free.

### School of Transmutation (PHB p.119)

Altering energy, matter, and form.

- **Transmutation Savant (2nd level).** Halve gp cost & time to copy **transmutation** spells.
- **Minor Alchemy (2nd level).** Temporarily transform one nonmagical object made entirely of **wood, stone (not gemstone), iron, copper, or silver** into another of those materials. Takes **10 minutes per 1 cubic foot** transformed; reverts after **1 hour** or when you lose concentration (as if concentrating on a spell).
- **Transmuter's Stone (6th level).** Spend **8 hours** to create a stone storing transmutation magic; you or a creature holding it gains one chosen benefit: **Darkvision 60 ft.**, **+10 ft. speed** while unencumbered, **proficiency in Constitution saves**, or **resistance to acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder** (your choice). Each time you cast a 1st-level-or-higher transmutation spell, you can change the stone's effect if it's on your person. Creating a new stone deactivates the old one.
- **Shapechanger (10th level).** Add **polymorph** to your spellbook (if not already there). You can cast **polymorph without expending a slot**, but only to target **yourself** and turn into a **beast of CR 1 or lower**. Recharges on a **short or long rest** (you can still cast it normally with a slot).
- **Master Transmuter (14th level).** As an **action**, consume your Transmuter's Stone (destroyed; can't be remade until a long rest) for one effect: **Major Transformation** (transmute one nonmagical object ≤5-ft. cube into another of similar size/mass and equal-or-lesser value; 10 minutes of handling), **Panacea** (remove all curses, diseases, and poisons on a touched creature and restore all its HP), **Restore Life** (cast *raise dead* on a touched creature without a slot or having the spell), or **Restore Youth** (reduce a willing creature's apparent age by **3d10 years**, minimum 13).

## Common questions

**Q: What's the difference between spells "in my spellbook" and spells "prepared"?**
A: Your **spellbook** is your whole library — every spell you've researched, copied, or learned on level-up. **Prepared** spells are the subset (Int modifier + wizard level) you can actually cast on a given day, chosen from the book after a long rest. You can also cast any **ritual** in your book without preparing it. (PHB p.114)

**Q: How many spells does a wizard prepare, and does my spellbook size limit it?**
A: Prepared count = **Intelligence modifier + wizard level** (minimum 1), of levels you have slots for. Your spellbook normally holds *more* than you can prepare; the book is the menu, prepared spells are today's order. Cantrips are separate (always available, never "prepared"). (PHB p.114)

**Q: When can I use Arcane Recovery, and how much do I get back?**
A: **Once per day**, on a **short rest** of your choosing. You recover slots totaling **half your wizard level rounded up** in combined levels, and **none can be 6th level or higher** (so it's only ever 1st–5th-level slots). Example: a level-10 wizard recovers up to 5 combined levels (e.g., a 3rd + a 2nd, or five 1st). (PHB p.115)

**Q: Do free level-up spells and copied spells both go in the book? Is there a cost?**
A: Yes — both go in the spellbook. The **two spells per level** from leveling up are **free** (your own research). **Found** spells (scrolls, enemy books) cost **2 hours and 50 gp per spell level** to copy. School Savant **halves the cost and time** for spells of your chosen school. (PHB p.114–115)

**Q: How does Portent work, and does Greater Portent change it?**
A: After each long rest you roll **two d20s** (set aside). Before any d20 roll by you or a creature you can see, you may **replace** that roll with a Portent die (once per turn). At 14th level **Greater Portent** makes it **three d20s**. Unused dice are lost at your next long rest. Note it replaces the roll *before* it's made, so it can force a hit, a miss, or a failed/succeeded save. (PHB p.116–117)

**Q: Can Evocation's Sculpt Spells protect me and my allies from my own fireball?**
A: It protects **allies** (creatures you choose, up to **1 + the spell's level**) — they auto-succeed and take no damage on what would be a half-damage save. The feature lets you shape the AoE around your party. It doesn't list yourself separately, so position to keep yourself out of the area as usual. (PHB p.117–118)
