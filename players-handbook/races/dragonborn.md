# Dragonborn

Proud, dragon-blooded humanoids who wear their draconic heritage on the outside — scaled hide, a clawed and crested frame, and a **breath weapon** keyed to the dragon in their ancestry. A natural **Strength + Charisma** martial face with a recharging area attack and a matching damage resistance.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 32–34).

---

## Flavor & lore

Dragonborn are born of dragons and carry that inheritance into everything they do. They stand head and shoulders over a human, dry-scaled and reptilian, with a tail-less but unmistakably draconic build and eyes in shades of red or gold. They have no need to *prove* their heritage — it is written across their bodies — so what matters to a dragonborn is what they *do* with it.

Clan and honor sit at the center of dragonborn life. To a dragonborn, the self is defined by the clan, and the clan name is spoken first, before any personal name, as a mark of where one's loyalty and history lie. A dragonborn is expected to master a craft or calling and pursue it to excellence, because failure shames not only the individual but the whole bloodline. They tend to hold themselves to demanding personal codes; even an exile or a "black sheep" usually clings to some private standard of conduct.

Because their colors echo both chromatic (evil) and metallic (good) dragons, dragonborn are sometimes met with suspicion — a stranger may assume scales mean a monster. Most dragonborn answer that prejudice with self-possession rather than rage: they choose their alignment deliberately, lean strongly toward good, and let their conduct correct the first impression. Among their own, childhood nicknames and clutch-names — earned from a deed or a habit — show the warmer, more affectionate side of a famously dignified people.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Str +2, Cha +1.
- **Size / Speed:** Medium (often well over 6 ft, ~250 lb); 30 ft.
- **No darkvision.**
- **Draconic Ancestry:** pick a dragon — it sets your breath weapon shape, save, and resistance.
- **Breath Weapon:** action; 2d6 (→5d6 by L16); save DC = 8 + Con + prof; recharges on a short or long rest.
- **Damage Resistance:** to the damage type of your ancestry.
- **Languages:** Common + Draconic.

## Racial traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** **Strength +2, Charisma +1**.
- **Age:** Grow quickly — reach adulthood by **15**; live to around **80**.
- **Alignment:** Tend to extremes, **consciously choosing** good or evil. Most are good.
- **Size:** Well over 6 feet, ~250 lb. **Medium**.
- **Speed:** 30 feet.
- **Draconic Ancestry:** Choose one dragon type from the table below. Your choice determines the **damage type** of your breath weapon and resistance, and the **shape and save** of your breath weapon.
- **Breath Weapon:** Use your **action** to exhale destructive energy in the size and shape set by your draconic ancestry. Each creature in the area must make a saving throw (the **type** of save is set by your ancestry). The **DC = 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus**. A creature takes **2d6 damage** on a failed save, and **half as much** on a success. The damage **increases with your character level**: **3d6 at 6th level, 4d6 at 11th level, and 5d6 at 16th level**. After you use your breath weapon, you **can't use it again until you complete a short or long rest**.
- **Damage Resistance:** You have **resistance** to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry.
- **Languages:** You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **Draconic**.

The **damage type** column also sets your **Damage Resistance**.

## Roleplay hooks

- **Clan first.** Your clan name comes before your own. What does your clan stand for, and are you upholding its honor or laboring under its shame?
- **The code you keep.** Dragonborn hold to personal standards even when exiled or outcast. What is the one rule you will not break, and what did it cost you?
- **First impressions.** Strangers may flinch at your scales and assume "monster." Do you patiently disprove them, or have you grown tired of having to?
- **Chromatic or metallic shadow.** Your color hints at a draconic line. Do you embrace it, fear what it implies, or hunt the truth of which dragon you descend from?
- **Excellence as duty.** A dragonborn is expected to master a calling. What craft, art, or martial discipline defines you — and what happens to your honor if you fall short of it?

## Common questions

**Q: How does a dragonborn's breath weapon save DC work, and how often can it be used?**
A: **DC = 8 + Constitution modifier + proficiency bonus.** It's an **action**, deals **2d6** (scaling to **5d6 by level 16**), and recharges on a **short or long rest** — not once per turn (PHB p.32, 34).

**Q: Does the breath weapon improve with my Dragonborn levels or my character level?**
A: With your **total character level**: 2d6, then 3d6 at 6th, 4d6 at 11th, and 5d6 at 16th (PHB p.34).

**Q: Is the breath weapon an attack roll?**
A: No. Targets make a **saving throw** against your DC (type set by your ancestry). It deals **full damage on a failure, half on a success** — there's no to-hit roll (PHB p.34).

**Q: Do dragonborn have darkvision?**
A: **No.** Despite their draconic blood, dragonborn rely on ordinary sight — they have no darkvision (PHB p.34).
