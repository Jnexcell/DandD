# Dwarf

Bold, hardy, clan-proud folk of mountain halls and deep stone — short, broad, and famously tough, with darkvision, poison resistance, and an instinct for crafted stone.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 18–20).

---

## Flavor & lore

Dwarves are a people of stone and memory. They build to last — vaulted underground cities, mines that bore for miles, forge-halls where the same techniques pass unbroken from one generation to the next. Tradition, craft, and the honor of one's ancestors matter more to most dwarves than novelty or speed, and a grudge or a debt is remembered for centuries.

Clan and kin sit at the center of dwarven identity. A dwarf's name belongs to the clan, not just the individual, and to disgrace that name is among the worst things a dwarf can do. Dwarves tend to be wary of strangers at first and slow to extend trust, but once given, their loyalty and friendship are as durable as the granite they live among. They prize fair dealing and ordered society, and they hold long, smoldering enmities — especially toward goblins and orcs, who have warred over dwarven holds since time out of mind.

To other races, dwarves can seem gruff, stubborn, and humorless. Those who know them better find a people of deep warmth, dry wit, fierce courage, and an artisan's pride in everything from a worked gemstone to a well-brewed ale. Players are drawn to dwarves for the durability (Constitution, poison resistance, that unstoppable 25-foot stride) and for a ready-made hook: a character with roots, a clan, an old grudge, and a reason to wield an axe.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Con +2 (plus subrace).
- **Size:** Medium (4–5 ft, ~150 lb).
- **Speed:** 25 ft — **not reduced by heavy armor**.
- **Darkvision:** 60 ft.
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish.
- **Signature traits:** Dwarven Resilience (advantage vs. poison + poison resistance), Dwarven Combat Training, Stonecunning, tool proficiency.
- **Subraces:** Hill Dwarf (Wis +1, +1 HP/level), Mountain Dwarf (Str +2, light/medium armor).

## Racial traits

All dwarves share these traits (PHB p.18–20):

- **Ability Score Increase:** Constitution +2.
- **Age:** Considered young until 50; live about 350 years. Mature at the human rate.
- **Alignment:** Most are lawful and lean toward good — a strong sense of fair play and ordered society.
- **Size:** 4–5 feet tall, ~150 lb. **Medium**.
- **Speed:** 25 feet, and your speed is **not reduced by wearing heavy armor**.
- **Darkvision:** 60 feet.
- **Dwarven Resilience:** Advantage on saving throws against poison, and **resistance to poison damage**.
- **Dwarven Combat Training:** Proficiency with the battleaxe, handaxe, throwing hammer, and warhammer.
- **Tool Proficiency:** Proficiency with one set of artisan's tools of your choice: smith's tools, brewer's supplies, or mason's tools.
- **Stonecunning:** On an Intelligence (History) check about the origin of stonework, you're treated as proficient in History and **add double your proficiency bonus** to the check.
- **Languages:** Common and Dwarvish.

## Subraces

### Hill Dwarf (PHB p.20)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Wisdom +1.
- **Dwarven Toughness:** Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and increases by 1 again **every time you gain a level**.

### Mountain Dwarf (PHB p.20)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Strength +2.
- **Dwarven Armor Training:** Proficiency with light and medium armor.

## Random Height & Weight

Roll the **Height Modifier**, add those inches to the **Base Height** to get your height; multiply that same modifier roll by the **Weight Modifier** and add the result to the **Base Weight** to get your weight (PHB p.121).

| Subrace | Base Height | Height Modifier | Base Weight | Weight Modifier |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Hill Dwarf | 3'8" | +2d4 in. | 115 lb. | × (2d6) lb. |
| Mountain Dwarf | 4'0" | +2d4 in. | 130 lb. | × (2d6) lb. |

*Example (hill dwarf):* roll 2d4 = 5 → height 3'8" + 5 in. = **4'1"**; weight 115 + (5 × 2d6) lb.

## Roleplay hooks

- **The grudge.** You carry an old wrong — a clan slight, a lost hold, a goblin warlord still owed. It colors how you treat anyone tied to it.
- **The craft.** Lean into the artisan's tool you chose. Your character notices shoddy stonework, appraises gems on sight, or never travels without their brewing kit.
- **The exile or wanderer.** Many adventuring dwarves left the hold — disgraced, second sons, curiosity-seekers, or the last of a fallen clan. Why are you out here instead of underground?
- **Stubborn loyalty.** Play the slow-to-trust, never-to-betray dynamic. Once the party earns it, you are immovable in their defense.
- **Long memory.** You measure time in centuries. Treat the other races' urgency with patient, faintly amused detachment.

## Common questions

**Q: Does a dwarf in heavy armor still move at 25 feet?**
A: Yes. A dwarf's 25-foot speed is **not reduced by heavy armor** even if the dwarf lacks the Strength score that armor normally requires. (The Strength-shortfall penalty in the armor rules still applies separately, but the racial heavy-armor speed reduction is waived) (PHB p.20).

**Q: How does Stonecunning's double proficiency work if I'm not proficient in History?**
A: Stonecunning treats you as proficient *and* adds double your proficiency bonus — so even an untrained dwarf gets 2× proficiency on Intelligence (History) checks specifically about the origin of stonework. It doesn't help with any other History check (PHB p.20).

**Q: Mountain dwarf armor training plus a class that already gives armor — wasted?**
A: It only grants light and medium armor proficiency. If your class already provides those (e.g., fighter), the redundancy is in the proficiency, but the **Str +2** is the bigger draw, making mountain dwarf a strong frontline pick regardless (PHB p.20).
