# Elf

Graceful, long-lived people with a deep tie to magic and the natural world — keen-sensed, fey-blooded, and ageless, sleeping not at all but entering a four-hour trance instead.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 21–24).

---

## Flavor & lore

Elves are a people out of step with time. Where a human measures a life in decades, an elf measures it in centuries — and that vast span shapes everything about them. They are unhurried, exacting, and devoted to the pursuit of refinement: in art, in magic, in martial skill, in the slow perfection of a single craft. To an elf, a project that takes fifty years is not a burden but a pleasure, and the frantic ambition of shorter-lived races can seem by turns charming, wasteful, and a little sad.

Most elves dwell in secluded woodland realms or remote, otherworldly cities, holding themselves somewhat apart from the rest of the world. They prize freedom, beauty, and self-expression, and they lean toward chaotic good — friends of liberty, tolerant of others' choices, slow to anger but capable of long and patient vengeance. Their fey heritage runs in the blood: they cannot be charmed or magically put to sleep, and instead of sleeping they sink into trance, a meditative half-waking state full of memory and reverie.

Others find elves alternately enchanting and aloof — gracious even to those who fall short of their expectations, yet quietly certain of their own superiority. The drow are the great exception: descendants of elves who were driven underground long ago, most are cruel, suspicious, and feared, though individual drow do break from that legacy. Players choose elves for the Dexterity, free Perception, immunity to sleep magic, and the four-hour rest — and for the chance to play a character whose perspective spans empires risen and fallen.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Dex +2 (plus subrace).
- **Size:** Medium (under 5 to over 6 ft, slender).
- **Speed:** 30 ft (wood elf 35 ft).
- **Darkvision:** 60 ft (drow 120 ft).
- **Languages:** Common, Elvish (high elf +1 extra).
- **Signature traits:** Keen Senses (Perception), Fey Ancestry (advantage vs. charm, no magic sleep), Trance (4-hour rest).
- **Subraces:** High Elf (Int +1, wizard cantrip, weapon training), Wood Elf (Wis +1, 35 ft, Mask of the Wild), Drow (Cha +1, superior darkvision, drow magic, sunlight sensitivity).

## Racial traits

All elves share these traits (PHB p.21–23):

- **Ability Score Increase:** Dexterity +2.
- **Age:** Claim adulthood and an adult name around 100; can live to 750.
- **Alignment:** Lean toward chaos and tend toward good (valuing freedom and self-expression). Drow are the exception — more often evil.
- **Size:** Under 5 to over 6 feet, slender. **Medium**.
- **Speed:** 30 feet.
- **Darkvision:** 60 feet.
- **Keen Senses:** Proficiency in the Perception skill.
- **Fey Ancestry:** Advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and **magic can't put you to sleep**.
- **Trance:** Elves don't sleep. They meditate (trance) for **4 hours** to gain the same benefit a human gets from 8 hours of sleep.
- **Languages:** Common and Elvish.

## Subraces

### High Elf (PHB p.23)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Intelligence +1.
- **Elf Weapon Training:** Proficiency with the longsword, shortsword, shortbow, and longbow.
- **Cantrip:** You know one cantrip of your choice from the **wizard** spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.
- **Extra Language:** Speak, read, and write one extra language of your choice.

### Wood Elf (PHB p.24)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Wisdom +1.
- **Elf Weapon Training:** Proficiency with the longsword, shortsword, shortbow, and longbow.
- **Fleet of Foot:** Your base walking speed increases to **35 feet**.
- **Mask of the Wild:** You can attempt to hide even when only lightly obscured by foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, mist, and similar natural phenomena.

### Dark Elf (Drow) (PHB p.24)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Charisma +1.
- **Superior Darkvision:** Darkvision range is **120 feet**.
- **Sunlight Sensitivity:** Disadvantage on attack rolls and on Wisdom (Perception) checks relying on sight when you, your target, or what you're trying to perceive is in **direct sunlight**.
- **Drow Magic:** You know the *dancing lights* cantrip. At 3rd level you can cast *faerie fire* once per day; at 5th level you can also cast *darkness* once per day. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.
- **Drow Weapon Training:** Proficiency with rapiers, shortswords, and hand crossbows.

## Random Height & Weight

Roll the **Height Modifier**, add those inches to the **Base Height** to get your height; multiply that same modifier roll by the **Weight Modifier** and add the result to the **Base Weight** to get your weight (PHB p.121).

| Subrace | Base Height | Height Modifier | Base Weight | Weight Modifier |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| High Elf | 4'6" | +2d10 in. | 90 lb. | × (1d4) lb. |
| Wood Elf | 4'6" | +2d10 in. | 100 lb. | × (1d4) lb. |
| Drow | 4'5" | +2d6 in. | 75 lb. | × (1d6) lb. |

*Example (high elf):* roll 2d10 = 11 → height 4'6" + 11 in. = **5'5"**; weight 90 + (11 × 1d4) lb.

## Roleplay hooks

- **The long view.** You have watched friends and dynasties age and die. Decide whether that makes you detached, mournful, fiercely protective of the short-lived, or simply patient beyond their understanding.
- **The unfinished pursuit.** Elves perfect one thing across decades. What is your great work — a song, a blade form, a spell, a feud? Let it pull at your character.
- **Trance and reverie.** While the party sleeps, you sit in waking memory. Use the trance for atmosphere: visions of the past, half-remembered Elvish lore, an early warning when something stirs.
- **Apart, then together.** Many elf PCs left a cloistered home for the first time. Play the wonder, the culture shock, and the slow softening of elven aloofness as the party becomes kin.
- **Drow against type (or not).** If you play a drow, lean into the world's fear of you — and decide whether you defy the stereotype or quietly embody it.

## Common questions

**Q: Can an elf in trance be ambushed or gain a full long rest in 4 hours?**
A: An elf trances for 4 hours and gains the same benefit a human gets from 8 hours of sleep. A long rest is still a period of at least 8 hours, of which at least some can be light activity — trance lets the elf stay semiconscious and aware, but it doesn't shorten the long rest itself (PHB p.23).

**Q: Drow get sunlight sensitivity — when exactly does it apply?**
A: Only in **direct sunlight**, and only to sight-based attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks. It doesn't apply in dim light, indoors, or under most artificial or magical light (PHB p.24).

**Q: Which cantrip can a high elf take, and what's the spellcasting ability?**
A: Any one cantrip from the **wizard** spell list, and **Intelligence** is the spellcasting ability for it — regardless of your class. Popular picks are *fire bolt*, *minor illusion*, or *prestidigitation* (PHB p.23).

**Q: Does Fey Ancestry make an elf immune to all sleep effects?**
A: No — only **magic** can't put you to sleep (e.g., the *sleep* spell). You can still be knocked unconscious by damage or fall asleep naturally; you also get advantage on saves against being charmed (PHB p.23).
