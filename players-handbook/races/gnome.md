# Gnome

Small, inventive, irrepressibly curious folk who pack an enormous appetite for life into a slight frame. Natural **Intelligence** specialists with **darkvision** and **Gnome Cunning** — a strong defensive trait against magic — they split into illusion-minded **Forest** gnomes and gadget-building **Rock** gnomes.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 35–37).

---

## Flavor & lore

Gnomes treat existence as a delight to be wrung dry. They are small — well under four feet — bright-eyed, and constantly in motion, throwing themselves into invention, exploration, music, mischief, and study with the same wide grin. A gnome's energy never seems to flag; they sleep little, talk fast, and chase a dozen enthusiasms at once. To longer-faced races this can read as frivolity, but it sits atop genuine seriousness: gnomes are dedicated engineers, alchemists, tinkers, and scholars who simply refuse to be grim about it. They make mistakes cheerfully, laugh at themselves, and take bold (sometimes foolhardy) risks while perfecting their craft.

They love jokes of every kind — puns and pranks above all — and they love names even more: a gnome may go through a whole drift of nicknames in a lifetime, collecting and discarding them as moods and exploits suggest. Their homes are tucked into hilly, wooded country, often underground but airier and more open to the living surface world than a dwarf's halls, hidden behind clever construction and simple illusions. Welcome guests are swept into bright, warm burrows; unwelcome ones tend to wander off, baffled, never realizing a door was ever there.

Others rarely find gnomes threatening — they don't look the part, and good humor disarms suspicion quickly. The common reaction is curiosity rather than fear, which suits gnomes fine: it gives them one more audience to charm, puzzle, or prank.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Int +2 (Forest +Dex; Rock +Con).
- **Size / Speed:** Small (3–4 ft, ~40 lb); 25 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Gnome Cunning:** advantage on **Int, Wis, and Cha saves against magic**.
- **Forest Gnome:** *minor illusion* cantrip + speak with Small beasts.
- **Rock Gnome:** Artificer's Lore + Tinker (build tiny clockwork gadgets).
- **Languages:** Common + Gnomish.

## Racial traits

**Gnome traits (all gnomes):**

- **Ability Score Increase:** **Intelligence +2**.
- **Age:** Mature at the human rate and settle into adult life around **40**; live **350 to almost 500 years**.
- **Alignment:** Most often **good**.
- **Size:** 3–4 feet tall, ~40 lb. **Small**.
- **Speed:** 25 feet.
- **Darkvision:** 60 feet.
- **Gnome Cunning:** You have **advantage on all Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against magic**.
- **Languages:** You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **Gnomish**.

## Subraces

### Forest Gnome subrace (PHB p.37)

- **Ability Score Increase:** **Dexterity +1**.
- **Natural Illusionist:** You know the *minor illusion* cantrip. **Intelligence** is your spellcasting ability for it.
- **Speak with Small Beasts:** Through sounds and gestures, you can communicate simple ideas with **Small or smaller beasts**.

### Rock Gnome subrace (PHB p.37)

- **Ability Score Increase:** **Constitution +1**.
- **Artificer's Lore:** Whenever you make an **Intelligence (History) check** related to **magic items, alchemical objects, or technological devices**, you can **add twice your proficiency bonus** instead of any proficiency bonus you normally apply.
- **Tinker:** You have proficiency with **tinker's tools**. Using those tools, you can spend **1 hour and 10 gp** of materials to construct a **Tiny clockwork device (AC 5, 1 hp)**. It ceases to function after **24 hours** (unless you spend 1 hour repairing it to keep it going), or when you use your action to dismantle it; you can then reclaim the materials. You can have up to **three** such devices active at a time. When you create one, choose:
  - **Clockwork Toy** — an animal, monster, or person (e.g. a frog, mouse, bird, dragon, or soldier) that moves about the floor 5 ft. on your turn in a random direction and makes appropriate sounds.
  - **Fire Starter** — produces a miniature flame to light a candle, torch, or campfire; using it requires an action.
  - **Music Box** — when opened, plays a single song at a moderate volume; it stops when it reaches the song's end or when it's closed.

## Random Height & Weight

Roll for a randomly generated height and weight (PHB p.121):

| Race | Base Height | Height Modifier | Base Weight | Weight Modifier |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Gnome | 2'11" | +2d4 | 35 lb. | × 1 lb. |

- **How the roll works:** Roll the **Height Modifier (2d4)** and add those inches to the base height. Then multiply that same 2d4 result by the **Weight Modifier (× 1 lb.)** and add the product (in lb.) to the base weight — so a gnome's weight gain over base is just equal to its height roll in pounds.

## Roleplay hooks

- **The enthusiasm engine.** Gnomes throw themselves at everything. What three half-finished projects, obsessions, or schemes are you juggling right now?
- **A name for every mood.** You've worn a parade of nicknames. Which one are you going by today, who gave it to you, and what did you do to earn it?
- **The hidden burrow.** Your home is concealed behind clever craft and minor illusions. Who's welcome, who never finds the door, and what are you protecting?
- **Serious about silliness.** Behind the puns and pranks is a dedicated engineer, alchemist, or scholar. What is the one piece of work you'd never joke about?
- **Bold to a fault.** Gnomes take foolhardy risks while perfecting their craft. What's the experiment, expedition, or wager that's about to get you in real trouble?

## Common questions

**Q: How good is Gnome Cunning, really?**
A: Very. It grants **advantage on every Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throw against magic** — covering a large share of the most dangerous spell saves in the game (charms, fear, banishment, illusions, mind effects, and more). It doesn't help against non-magical effects or against Strength/Dexterity/Constitution saves (PHB p.36).

**Q: Which gnome cantrip does a Forest Gnome get, and what casts it?**
A: *Minor illusion*, with **Intelligence** as the spellcasting ability — even if you have no class spellcasting otherwise (PHB p.37).

**Q: How many clockwork devices can a Rock Gnome have, and how long do they last?**
A: Up to **three** active at once. Each runs for **24 hours** (or longer if you spend 1 hour repairing it), and stops when dismantled — after which you reclaim the materials (PHB p.37).

**Q: Do gnomes have darkvision?**
A: Yes — **60 feet**, like dwarves and elves (PHB p.36).
