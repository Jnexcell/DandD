# Half-Elf

Walking the line between two heritages, half-elves combine human drive and adaptability with elven grace, long life, and fey instincts — versatile wanderers who belong fully to neither parent culture.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 38–39).

---

## Flavor & lore

Half-elves are the children of a human and an elf, and they spend their lives straddling the gap between those worlds without ever sitting fully inside either. To most humans they seem a little too elven — aloof, unhurried, and unsettlingly long-lived — while to most elves they read as too human: restless, ambitious, and quick to chase change. The result is a people who tend to feel like outsiders almost everywhere, and who learn early to rely on their own charm and adaptability to find a place.

That in-between nature is also a strength. Half-elves inherit the curiosity and ambition of humans alongside the refined senses, keen memory, and easy poise of elves. They can pass comfortably through both societies, picking up customs, languages, and connections as they go, which makes them natural diplomats, go-betweens, spies, and leaders. Many drift toward a wandering or independent life, valuing personal freedom and self-expression — the same chaotic streak that runs through their elven blood. Where elves often act like reserved aristocrats and humans like driven strivers, a half-elf is frequently the one who can talk to (and read) both.

How others treat them varies widely by setting. In cosmopolitan cities a half-elf may be unremarkable; in isolated human or elven communities they may be regarded with suspicion, pity, or fascination. Lacking a homeland or culture purely their own, half-elves tend to define themselves by relationships and deeds rather than by lineage — which is exactly why so many of them end up adventuring.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Charisma +2, plus +1 to two other ability scores of your choice (great flexibility).
- **Size/Speed:** Medium, 30 ft.
- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Fey Ancestry:** Advantage vs. charm; immune to magical sleep.
- **Skill Versatility:** Two skill proficiencies of your choice.
- **Long-lived:** Often past 180 years.
- **Languages:** Common, Elvish, plus one of your choice.

---

## Racial traits

All half-elves share these traits (PHB p.38). Half-elves have **no subraces**.

- **Ability Score Increase:** Charisma +2, and **two other ability scores** of your choice each increase by +1.
- **Age:** Mature at the human rate, reach adulthood around 20; often live beyond 180 years.
- **Alignment:** Share the chaotic bent of their elven heritage; value personal freedom and creative expression.
- **Size:** 5–6 feet, like humans. Medium.
- **Speed:** 30 feet.
- **Darkvision:** 60 feet.
- **Fey Ancestry:** Advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.
- **Skill Versatility:** Proficiency in **two skills** of your choice.
- **Languages:** Common, Elvish, and one extra language of your choice.

## Names

Half-elves use either human or elven names, depending on which culture raised them — and many keep one of each, switching depending on the company they're in (PHB p.39). The Player's Handbook does not give a separate half-elf name list; instead it points you to those of the parent races.

- For **human-style** names (organized by ethnicity/region — Calishite, Chondathan, Damaran, Mulan, Rashemi, Shou, Tethyrian, and more), see the Human entry in `../02-races.md` and PHB p.29–31.
- For **elven** names (child names, adult names, and family names rooted in Elvish), see the Elf entry in `../02-races.md` and PHB p.20–21.

A common touch: a half-elf raised among humans might carry a melodic Elvish name as a quiet nod to the parent they never knew, or vice versa.

## Roleplay hooks

- **Two homes, no home.** Choose which parent's world raised you — and what made you leave it. Are you searching for the other half of your heritage, or running from it?
- **The bridge.** Half-elves often serve as translators, envoys, or mediators between communities that distrust each other. Who relies on you to keep the peace, and what happens when you take a side?
- **Outsider's eye.** Belonging nowhere makes you observant. Lean into Skill Versatility — pick two skills that show how you survived as an outsider (Persuasion and Insight? Stealth and Deception?).
- **The long view.** You'll outlive most of the humans you grew up with but feel hurried next to elven elders. How does that strange timescale shape what you chase — legacy, love, or a name people remember?
- **Self-made.** With no inherited culture to lean on, your identity is what you've chosen. What virtue, craft, or cause defines you when lineage can't?

## Common questions

**Q: How do I assign a half-elf's ability score increases?**
A: Charisma always goes **+2**. Then choose **two different** ability scores (other than Charisma) and raise each by **+1**. That flexibility is the half-elf's hallmark — you can build toward almost any class (PHB p.39).

**Q: Does Fey Ancestry work the same as an elf's?**
A: Yes — advantage on saving throws against being **charmed**, and magic **can't put you to sleep**. It's identical to the elf trait (PHB p.39).

**Q: Do half-elves trance like elves?**
A: No. Half-elves do **not** have the elven Trance trait; they sleep like humans (8 hours for a long rest). Only full elves trance (PHB p.23, 39).

**Q: How many skills does Skill Versatility grant, and can they overlap with my class/background?**
A: It grants proficiency in **two skills of your choice** from the full skill list — there's no class restriction. If your character would gain the same proficiency twice from another source, pick a different skill so you don't waste the grant (PHB p.39).
