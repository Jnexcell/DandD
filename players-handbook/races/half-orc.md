# Half-Orc

Born of human and orc blood, half-orcs are powerful, fierce, and relentlessly tough — survivors who carry the raw strength and fury of their orc ancestry while striving to find a place in a world that often fears them.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 40–41).

---

## Flavor & lore

Half-orcs stand at the meeting point of two very different peoples. From their orc heritage they inherit imposing size, slabs of muscle, jutting jaws, sloping foreheads, and sometimes prominent teeth or greenish skin — features that mark them as outsiders at a glance. From their human side they take ambition, adaptability, and a capacity for loyalty and self-discipline that pure orc culture rarely nurtures. The combination produces individuals who are physically formidable and emotionally driven, often wrestling to channel an inner intensity that can flare into rage.

Many half-orcs grow up among orcs or in the rough frontier settlements where the two bloodlines mingle, and the wider world tends to treat them with wariness or open hostility, assuming savagery before they've said a word. Some respond by leaning into that reputation — letting others' fear keep them at arm's length. Others work twice as hard to prove their worth, demonstrating piety, kindness, or steadfastness as publicly as they can. Either way, a half-orc learns early that respect is something to be seized, not given.

What defines half-orcs more than temper is **endurance**. They are nearly impossible to put down: a half-orc shrugs off wounds that would fell others, claws back from the brink of death, and keeps swinging when the fight should already be over. Orc legend credits this to the war-god's blessing, but whatever the source, it makes half-orcs prized as warriors, guards, and frontline survivors. Beneath the intimidating exterior, many find their deepest satisfaction in earning a place at someone's side — a tribe, a war band, or an adventuring party that takes them as they are.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Strength +2, Constitution +1 (built for melee).
- **Size/Speed:** Medium, 30 ft.
- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Menacing:** Proficiency in Intimidation.
- **Relentless Endurance:** Drop to 1 HP instead of 0, once per long rest.
- **Savage Attacks:** Extra weapon die on melee critical hits.
- **Languages:** Common and Orc.

---

## Racial traits

All half-orcs share these traits (PHB p.40). Half-orcs have **no subraces**.

- **Ability Score Increase:** Strength +2, Constitution +1.
- **Age:** Mature faster than humans (adulthood ~14); rarely live longer than 75 years.
- **Alignment:** Inherit a tendency toward chaos; not strongly inclined toward good.
- **Size:** Somewhat larger and bulkier than humans, 5 to well over 6 feet. Medium.
- **Speed:** 30 feet.
- **Darkvision:** 60 feet.
- **Menacing:** Proficiency in the Intimidation skill.
- **Relentless Endurance:** When reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can **drop to 1 hit point instead**. Usable once per **long rest**.
- **Savage Attacks:** When you score a critical hit with a **melee weapon attack**, roll one of the weapon's damage dice one additional time and add it to the extra critical damage.
- **Languages:** Common and Orc.

## Names

Half-orcs usually take names that fit the culture that raised them. A half-orc who wants to blend in among humans might trade an orc name for a human one — and, just as often, a half-orc with a human name may adopt a guttural orc name precisely because it sounds more intimidating (PHB p.41).

For human-style names by ethnicity/region, see the Human entry in `../02-races.md` and PHB p.29–31. The orc names below come from the half-orc sidebar (PHB p.41):

- **Male Orc Names:** Dench, Feng, Gell, Henk, Holg, Imsh, Keth, Krusk, Mhurren, Ront, Shump, Thokk
- **Female Orc Names:** Baggi, Emen, Engong, Kansif, Myev, Neega, Ovak, Ownka, Shautha, Sutha, Vola, Volen, Yevelda

## Random Height & Weight

| Race | Base Height | Height Modifier | Base Weight | Weight Modifier |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Half-Orc | 4'10" | +2d10 | 140 lb. | × (2d6) lb. |

How the roll works: roll the **height modifier** (here 2d10) and add the inches to the base height to get your character's height. Then multiply that same modifier roll by the **weight modifier** roll (2d6 lb.) and add the result to the base weight to get your weight — which is why half-orcs trend tall and heavy (PHB p.121).

## Roleplay hooks

- **Proving ground.** People assume the worst the moment they see you. Decide how you answer that: do you disprove it with kindness, ignore it with quiet strength, or weaponize their fear?
- **The inner fire.** Half-orcs feel anger keenly. What lights your fuse — injustice, betrayal, threats to your people — and what (or who) keeps you from burning everything down?
- **Where you come from.** Were you raised in an orc war band, a human town, or the rough edges between? Pick a single formative memory that explains why you left.
- **Unkillable.** You've survived things that should have ended you. Relentless Endurance is a story as much as a mechanic — what wound do you still carry from the last time you came back from the brink?
- **Found family.** Half-orcs who give loyalty give it completely. Who earned that loyalty, and what would you do to keep them safe?

## Common questions

**Q: How often can I use Relentless Endurance, and when does it trigger?**
A: When damage would reduce you to 0 HP **but not kill you outright** (i.e., the excess damage isn't enough for instant death), you can choose to drop to **1 HP instead**. It works **once per long rest** — so you can't lean on it every fight (PHB p.41).

**Q: Does Savage Attacks apply to spells, ranged attacks, or every critical die?**
A: No. It applies only to a critical hit with a **melee weapon attack**, and it adds **one extra roll of one weapon damage die** to the critical's extra damage — not your ability modifier, and not bonus dice from sneak attack or smite (PHB p.41).

**Q: Can Relentless Endurance save me from massive damage that drops me well below 0?**
A: Only if you aren't killed outright. If a single hit deals damage equal to or greater than your hit point maximum *past* 0 (the instant-death rule), you die and Relentless Endurance doesn't help (PHB p.41, 197).

**Q: Why are half-orc lifespans so short?**
A: They mature faster than humans (adult around 14) and rarely live past **75**. It's a flavor trait with no mechanical effect, but it can sharpen a half-orc's drive to make their mark quickly (PHB p.41).
