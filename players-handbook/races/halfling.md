# Halfling

Small, practical, good-natured folk who value home, hearth, and community — nimble and quick, blessed with uncanny luck and an unshakable courage out of all proportion to their size.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 26–28).

---

## Flavor & lore

Halflings are a comfortable, unassuming people who would, given the choice, rather be at home than anywhere else — a warm hearth, good food, the company of family and friends, and a tidy garden are most halflings' idea of a life well lived. They are practical and sensible, focused on the everyday needs in front of them rather than grand ambitions or distant glories. This down-to-earth contentment is not laziness; it's a deep, durable cheerfulness that helps them weather hardship and adapt to the world the bigger folk have built around them.

For all their love of comfort, halflings are far braver than their size suggests. They blend easily into human and dwarven settlements, getting by on their affability and a knack for going unnoticed, and they have a remarkable talent for landing on their feet — a kind of inborn luck that turns near-disasters into lucky escapes. When danger finds them, halflings face it with a steady, unflappable courage; they are loyal to their friends and will take surprising risks to protect their community.

Other races tend to find halflings charming, trustworthy, and easy to underestimate — which the cleverer halflings are happy to let them do. Players pick halflings for the Dexterity, the famous **Lucky** trait (reroll a natural 1), advantage against fear, and the ability to dart through bigger creatures' spaces. They make excellent rogues and any character who thrives on being slippery, lucky, and braver than they look.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Dex +2 (plus subrace).
- **Size:** **Small** (~3 ft, ~40 lb).
- **Speed:** 25 ft.
- **Darkvision:** None.
- **Languages:** Common, Halfling.
- **Signature traits:** Lucky (reroll a natural 1), Brave (advantage vs. frightened), Halfling Nimbleness (move through larger creatures' spaces).
- **Subraces:** Lightfoot (Cha +1, Naturally Stealthy), Stout (Con +1, poison resistance).

## Racial traits

All halflings share these traits (PHB p.26–28):

- **Ability Score Increase:** Dexterity +2.
- **Age:** Adulthood at 20; generally live into the middle of their second century.
- **Alignment:** Most are lawful good — kind, orderly, and traditional.
- **Size:** ~3 feet tall, ~40 lb. **Small**.
- **Speed:** 25 feet.
- **Lucky:** When you roll a **1** on the d20 for an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll.
- **Brave:** Advantage on saving throws against being **frightened**.
- **Halfling Nimbleness:** You can move through the space of any creature that is of a size **larger** than yours.
- **Languages:** Common and Halfling.

## Subraces

### Lightfoot (PHB p.28)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Charisma +1.
- **Naturally Stealthy:** You can attempt to hide even when obscured only by a creature that is at least **one size larger** than you.

### Stout (PHB p.28)

- **Ability Score Increase:** Constitution +1.
- **Stout Resilience:** Advantage on saving throws against poison, and **resistance to poison damage**.

## Random Height & Weight

Roll the **Height Modifier**, add those inches to the **Base Height** to get your height; multiply that same modifier roll by the **Weight Modifier** and add the result to the **Base Weight** to get your weight (PHB p.121).

| Race | Base Height | Height Modifier | Base Weight | Weight Modifier |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Halfling | 2'7" | +2d4 in. | 35 lb. | × 1 lb. |

*Example:* roll 2d4 = 5 → height 2'7" + 5 in. = **3'0"**; weight 35 + (5 × 1) = **40 lb.**

## Roleplay hooks

- **Home is the point.** Your character has a place they love and want to get back to — a village, a family, a tavern. Adventuring is the means, not the dream.
- **Lucky and they know it.** Lean into the inborn luck. Your halfling shrugs off near-misses with a grin, fully expecting things to work out.
- **Braver than they look.** Play the contrast: small, cheerful, easily underestimated — then unflinching when it counts. Bullies are in for a surprise.
- **The good neighbor.** Halflings get by on warmth and likeability. Be the party's heart — the one who feeds people, smooths over arguments, and remembers everyone's name.
- **Nimble and unseen.** Use Halfling Nimbleness and (if lightfoot) Naturally Stealthy in the fiction: slipping between an ogre's legs, vanishing behind a bigger ally, always somewhere the enemy didn't expect.

## Common questions

**Q: What's the difference between Lucky (halfling) and the Lucky feat?**
A: The halfling **Lucky** trait only triggers on a natural **1** on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, forcing you to keep the reroll. The Lucky feat (PHB Ch.6) is an unrelated, broader luck-point mechanic (PHB p.28).

**Q: Can Halfling Nimbleness let me end my move in a larger creature's space?**
A: No. You can **move through** the space of any creature larger than you (treating it as difficult terrain), but you can't willingly stop there — if you're forced to, you're effectively squeezing and the space is still occupied (PHB p.28).

**Q: Does Naturally Stealthy let a lightfoot hide behind any creature?**
A: Only one that is **at least one size larger** than the halfling — and you still need to be obscured by it. It lets you take the Hide action using an ally or enemy as cover when you'd otherwise have nowhere to hide (PHB p.28).

**Q: Why is a halfling's speed only 25 feet if they're so nimble?**
A: Short legs. Halflings (like dwarves and gnomes) move 25 feet because of their Small size; their nimbleness shows up in *how* they move (through bigger creatures, around obstacles), not in raw speed (PHB p.28).
