# Human

The most adaptable, ambitious, and widespread of the common races — found in nearly every land, in every walk of life, and pushing into every frontier. A "+1 to everything" generalist as standard, or, with the optional **Variant Human** rules, the only race that can start play with a **feat**.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 29–31).

---

## Flavor & lore

Where the longer-lived races measure their lives in centuries and settle into ancient traditions, humans burn fast and reach far. They have only a handful of decades, and they spend them building — empires, guilds, faiths, family lines, and reputations meant to outlast the brief span of any one life. That short horizon makes humans restless and driven: they pioneer the borderlands, found the trade cities, and adopt new ideas (and new neighbors) faster than any other people.

Because they are everywhere, humans are not really one culture but a thousand. Their look, dress, language, and custom shift from region to region, and a human raised among dwarves, elves, or halflings will carry as much of that culture as of any "human" one. In the Forgotten Realms, scholars name nine widespread ethnic groups (Calishite, Chondathan, Damaran, Illuskan, Mulan, Rashemi, Shou, Tethyrian, and Turami), each with its own coloring, naming traditions, and homeland — and these can stand in for human variety in any world.

Other folk tend to view humans with a mix of respect and wariness: respect for their energy and accomplishment, wariness at how quickly their ambitions can outrun their wisdom. To a human, that ambition is the whole point. A human's name and works are the only mark they will leave, so they make them count.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** +1 to **all six** abilities (Standard); or +1 to **two** of your choice (Variant).
- **Size / Speed:** Medium (barely 5 ft to well over 6 ft); 30 ft.
- **No darkvision.**
- **Standard human:** one extra language — that's the whole package.
- **Variant human (optional):** trade the all-+1 for two +1s, **one skill**, and **one feat** at level 1.
- **Languages:** Common + 1 of your choice.

## Racial traits

**Human traits (standard):**

- **Ability Score Increase:** Every one of your ability scores increases by **1**.
- **Age:** Reach adulthood in their late teens; live less than a century.
- **Alignment:** No particular tendency — the best and worst are found among them.
- **Size:** Barely 5 feet to well over 6 feet. Medium.
- **Speed:** 30 feet.
- **Languages:** You can speak, read, and write **Common** and one extra language of your choice. (PHB p.31)

### Variant Human traits (PHB p.31)

If the campaign uses the optional **feat** rules (PHB Ch.6), the DM may allow these traits, which **replace** the standard "+1 to all abilities" Ability Score Increase. The other human traits (age, alignment, size, speed, languages) are **unchanged**.

- **Ability Score Increase:** Two different ability scores of your choice each increase by **1**.
- **Skills:** Proficiency in **one skill** of your choice.
- **Feat:** You gain **one feat** of your choice.

> The Variant is an either/or swap: you take it *instead of* the standard +1-to-all, not on top of it.

## Names

Humans have no single naming tradition. The names below are organized by the nine widely recognized Forgotten Realms ethnic groups (PHB p.29–31); use any of them as inspiration in any setting.

**Calishite** — short, dusky-skinned folk of southwest Faerûn.
- **Male:** Aseir, Bardeid, Haseid, Khemed, Mehmen, Sudeiman, Zasheir
- **Female:** Atala, Ceidil, Hama, Jasmal, Meilil, Seipora, Yasheira, Zasheida
- **Surnames:** Basha, Dumein, Jassan, Khalid, Mostana, Pashar, Rein

**Chondathan** — slender, tawny folk who dominate the central lands around the Inner Sea. (Tethyrians also use Chondathan names.)
- **Male:** Darvin, Dorn, Evendur, Gorstag, Grim, Helm, Malark, Morn, Randal, Stedd
- **Female:** Arveene, Esvele, Jhessail, Kerri, Lureene, Miri, Rowan, Shandri, Tessele
- **Surnames:** Amblecrown, Buckman, Dundragon, Evenwood, Greycastle, Tallstag

**Damaran** — moderate-built folk of northwest Faerûn.
- **Male:** Bor, Fodel, Glar, Grigor, Igan, Ivor, Kosef, Mival, Orel, Pavel, Sergor
- **Female:** Alethra, Kara, Katernin, Mara, Natali, Olma, Tana, Zora
- **Surnames:** Bersk, Chernin, Dotsk, Kulenov, Marsk, Nemetsk, Shemov, Starag

**Illuskan** — tall, fair, mostly raven-haired folk of the northwest.
- **Male:** Ander, Blath, Bran, Frath, Geth, Lander, Luth, Malcer, Stor, Taman, Urth
- **Female:** Amafrey, Betha, Cefrey, Kethra, Mara, Olga, Silifrey, Westra
- **Surnames:** Brightwood, Helder, Hornraven, Lackman, Stormwind, Windrivver

**Mulan** — tall, amber-skinned folk of the eastern Inner Sea (nobles often shave all their hair).
- **Male:** Aoth, Bareris, Ehput-Ki, Kethoth, Mumed, Ramas, So-Kehur, Thazar-De, Urhur
- **Female:** Arizima, Chathi, Nephis, Nulara, Murithi, Sefris, Thola, Umara, Zolis
- **Surnames:** Ankhalab, Anskuld, Fezim, Hahpet, Nathandem, Sepret, Uuthrakt

**Rashemi** — short, stout, dusky folk east of the Inner Sea, often intermingled with the Mulan.
- **Male:** Borivik, Faurgar, Jandar, Kanithar, Madislak, Ralmevik, Shaumar, Vladislak
- **Female:** Fyevarra, Hulmarra, Immith, Imzel, Navarra, Shevarra, Tammith, Yuldra
- **Surnames:** Chergoba, Dyernina, Iltazyara, Murnyethara, Stayanoga, Ulmokina

**Shou** — yellowish-bronze, black-haired folk of Kara-Tur, far east of Faerûn. Surnames come **before** the given name.
- **Male:** An, Chen, Chi, Fai, Jiang, Jun, Lian, Long, Meng, On, Shan, Shui, Wen
- **Female:** Bai, Chao, Jia, Lei, Mei, Qiao, Shui, Tai
- **Surnames:** Chien, Huang, Kao, Kung, Lao, Ling, Mei, Pin, Shin, Sum, Tan, Wan

**Tethyrian** — medium-built folk of the Sword Coast; they **primarily use Chondathan names** (see above).

**Turami** — tall, muscular, dark-mahogany-skinned folk of the southern Inner Sea.
- **Male:** Anton, Diero, Marcon, Pieron, Rimardo, Romero, Salazar, Umbero
- **Female:** Balama, Dona, Faila, Jalana, Luisa, Marta, Quara, Selise, Vonda
- **Surnames:** Agosto, Astorio, Calabra, Domine, Falone, Marivaldi, Pisacar, Ramondo

## Random Height & Weight

Roll for a randomly generated height and weight (PHB p.121):

| Race | Base Height | Height Modifier | Base Weight | Weight Modifier |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Human | 4'8" | +2d10 | 110 lb. | × (2d4) lb. |

- **How the roll works:** Roll the **Height Modifier (2d10)** and add those inches to the base height. Then multiply that same 2d10 result by the **Weight Modifier (2d4)** and add the product (in lb.) to the base weight. (So a tall roll tends to also be a heavy one.)

## Roleplay hooks

- **A short life, a long ambition.** What legacy is your human racing the clock to leave — a dynasty, a discovery, a name in song, a wrong made right?
- **Child of two worlds.** Raised among dwarves, elves, or halflings? Let that culture color your manners, your tongue (humans love borrowing words and oaths), and what you consider "normal."
- **Ethnic homeland.** Pick one of the nine groups and let it shape your accent, faith, and the prejudices you carry — or the ones leveled at you abroad.
- **The pioneer.** Humans push into frontiers others avoid. Are you a settler, a guild factor, a missionary, or a fortune-hunter on the edge of the map?
- **Bridge between peoples.** Humans are the universal go-between. Maybe you're the diplomat, trader, or translator nobody else trusts but everyone needs.

## Common questions

**Q: Can a standard human take a feat at 1st level?**
A: Only by using the **Variant Human** traits, and only if the DM is using the optional feat rules. Standard humans get **+1 to every ability** instead and gain no starting feat (PHB p.31).

**Q: Can I take Variant Human *and* the +1 to all abilities?**
A: No. The Variant traits **replace** the standard +1-to-all Ability Score Increase. You pick one approach or the other (PHB p.31).

**Q: Does any kind of human get darkvision?**
A: No. Humans have **no darkvision** — they rely on light or magic to see in the dark, unlike most other races in this chapter (PHB p.31).

**Q: Do I have to pick one of the nine ethnicities?**
A: No — they're inspiration, not mechanics. They give humans flavor and naming options, but none of them changes a single rule on your sheet (PHB p.29).
