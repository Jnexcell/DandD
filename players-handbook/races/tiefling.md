# Tiefling

Touched by an ancient infernal bargain, tieflings wear their fiendish heritage openly — horns, tails, and a faint scent of brimstone — and walk a world that distrusts them on sight, forging their own way through charm, cunning, and a spark of inborn hellfire magic.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 2, "Races" (printed pages 42–43).

---

## Flavor & lore

Tieflings are the descendants of humans whose bloodline was bound, generations ago, to a fiendish power of the Nine Hells. That ancient pact left a permanent mark: most tieflings have horns, a long tail, sharp canines, and unusual skin tones (often red), with eyes that may be solid orbs of black, red, white, gold, or silver. The heritage runs deeper than appearance — it's woven into their very souls — yet it does **not** dictate their character. A tiefling is no more inherently evil than anyone else; the fiendish stain is in the blood, not in the will.

The world rarely sees it that way. People tend to assume a tiefling's infernal lineage has twisted their morals along with their looks. Shopkeepers watch them closely, town guards trail them through the streets, and superstitious folk blame them for misfortune they had nothing to do with. Lacking a homeland of their own, many tieflings cluster in the rougher corners of cities — among thieves, outcasts, or other distrusted minorities — and learn that survival means strength and self-reliance.

That hard upbringing shapes a distinctive outlook. Tieflings tend to be independent, self-sufficient, and slow to trust — but the flip side is fierce: once someone proves their loyalty, a tiefling returns it without reservation and stays a firm friend for life. Many carry a chip on their shoulder and a sharp wit to match, using charm, intimidation, or sheer audacity to claim the respect the world won't simply give them. Whether they embrace their heritage as a source of dark glamour or strive to define themselves entirely apart from it, tieflings are nearly always working to write their own story rather than the one others assume for them.

## Quick traits

- **Ability Score Increase:** Intelligence +1, Charisma +2.
- **Size/Speed:** Medium (human build), 30 ft.
- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Hellish Resistance:** Resistance to fire damage.
- **Infernal Legacy:** *Thaumaturgy* cantrip; *hellish rebuke* at L3; *darkness* at L5 (Charisma-based).
- **Languages:** Common and Infernal.

---

## Racial traits

All tieflings share these traits (PHB p.42). Tieflings have **no subraces** in the core 2014 PHB.

- **Ability Score Increase:** Intelligence +1, Charisma +2.
- **Age:** Mature at the human rate but live a few years longer.
- **Alignment:** No innate tendency toward evil, though many end up there; an independent nature inclines many toward chaos.
- **Size:** About the same size and build as humans. Medium.
- **Speed:** 30 feet.
- **Darkvision:** 60 feet.
- **Hellish Resistance:** Resistance to **fire damage**.
- **Infernal Legacy:** You know the *thaumaturgy* cantrip. At 3rd level you can cast *hellish rebuke* once per day as a **2nd-level** spell; at 5th level you can also cast *darkness* once per day. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.
- **Languages:** Common and Infernal.

### Infernal Legacy spell progression

| Character level | Spell gained | How it's cast |
|-----------------|--------------|---------------|
| 1st | *Thaumaturgy* (cantrip) | At will |
| 3rd | *Hellish rebuke* | Once per day, cast as a **2nd-level** spell |
| 5th | *Darkness* | Once per day |

Charisma is your spellcasting ability for all three. The once-per-day spells recover when you finish a **long rest** (PHB p.42).

## Names

Tiefling names fall into three broad styles (PHB p.42):

1. **Names from another culture** — tieflings born into a human (or other) culture often simply carry names from that culture. For human-style names by ethnicity/region, see the Human entry in `../02-races.md` and PHB p.29–31.
2. **Infernal names** — passed down through generations and rooted in the Infernal language, reflecting fiendish heritage.
3. **"Virtue" names** — chosen, usually by younger tieflings, to signify a virtue or concept they strive to embody. The chosen name can be a noble quest or a grim destiny.

**Male Infernal Names:** Akmenos, Amnon, Barakas, Damakos, Ekemon, Iados, Kairon, Leucis, Melech, Mordai, Morthos, Pelaios, Skamos, Therai

**Female Infernal Names:** Akta, Anakis, Bryseis, Criella, Damaia, Ea, Kallista, Lerissa, Makaria, Nemeia, Orianna, Phelaia, Rieta

**"Virtue" Names:** Art, Carrion, Chant, Creed, Despair, Excellence, Fear, Glory, Hope, Ideal, Music, Nowhere, Open, Poetry, Quest, Random, Reverence, Sorrow, Temerity, Torment, Weary

## Roleplay hooks

- **The mark you carry.** Pick the most striking feature of your infernal heritage — horns, tail, glowing eyes, an unnatural skin tone — and decide whether you flaunt it or try to hide it.
- **Your name, your destiny.** If you took a "Virtue" name like Hope, Sorrow, or Quest, what does it mean to you? Living up to it can be a calling; failing it can be a curse you can't escape.
- **The pact behind you.** What fiend or bargain put the infernal blood in your line? You may not know — and finding out could be a campaign-long thread.
- **Earning trust the hard way.** Tieflings are slow to trust but loyal to the bone. Who first proved they saw *you* and not your bloodline, and what did that cost them?
- **Proving the prejudice wrong (or right).** Everyone expects you to be wicked. Lean into how you respond — with defiant decency, sardonic charm, or a quiet decision to give them exactly what they fear.

## Common questions

**Q: When do I actually gain my Infernal Legacy spells?**
A: *Thaumaturgy* at **1st** level (at will). *Hellish rebuke* at **3rd** level, usable once per day and always cast as a **2nd-level** spell. *Darkness* at **5th** level, once per day. The once-per-day spells reset on a **long rest** (PHB p.42).

**Q: Can I cast hellish rebuke at higher than 2nd level using the racial trait?**
A: No. The Infernal Legacy version is locked to a **2nd-level** casting. If you also have *hellish rebuke* from a class and a spell slot to spare, you can use a slot to upcast it that way instead — the two sources are separate (PHB p.42).

**Q: What ability do I use for these spells, and what's the save DC?**
A: **Charisma** for all of them. Your save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier; your spell attack bonus = proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier (PHB p.42, 205).

**Q: Does Hellish Resistance cover all fire?**
A: Yes — you have **resistance to fire damage** from any source (taking half), magical or mundane. It stacks toward (but doesn't grant) immunity if combined with another resistance effect: multiple sources of resistance to the same damage type don't add up beyond halving once (PHB p.42, 197).

**Q: Do tieflings have subraces?**
A: Not in the core 2014 PHB — there's a single tiefling race here. (Later supplements add bloodline variants, but those are outside this knowledge base.) (PHB p.42)
