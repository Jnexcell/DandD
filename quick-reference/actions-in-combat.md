# Actions in Combat (Quick Reference)

The action economy of a single turn, plus a one-line summary of every standard action. For the full combat rules, see `players-handbook/09-combat.md`.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 9, "Combat" (printed pages 189–193).

---

## What you get on your turn

On your turn you can do all of the following, in any order you like (PHB p.189–190):

- **Movement** — move a distance up to your **speed**. You can split it up (move, act, then move the rest) and move between attacks if your action grants multiple attacks (PHB p.190).
- **One action** — choose a standard action below, or an action from a class feature, spell, or one you improvise (PHB p.192).
- **One bonus action** — *only* if a feature, spell, or ability specifically grants one. You otherwise have no bonus action to take, and you can take **at most one** per turn (PHB p.189).
- **One free object interaction** — interact with one object or feature of the environment for free during your move or action (e.g., draw a weapon, open a door). A **second** interaction in the same turn requires the **Use an Object** action (PHB p.190).
- **Communication** — brief speech and gestures, for free.

You can do as little as you like, or nothing at all. If you can't decide, **Dodge** or **Ready** are safe defaults (PHB p.189).

### Reactions
A **reaction** is an instant response to a trigger, and can happen on your turn or someone else's. You get **one reaction per round**; once used, you can't take another until the start of your next turn (PHB p.190). The **opportunity attack** is the most common reaction: you can make one when a hostile creature you can see leaves your reach (using its movement), unless it Disengaged or teleported (PHB p.195).

## Standard actions

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Attack** | Make **one** melee or ranged attack (more if a feature like Extra Attack applies) (PHB p.192). |
| **Cast a Spell** | Cast a spell whose casting time is 1 action (some spells use a bonus action, reaction, or longer) (PHB p.192). |
| **Dash** | Gain extra movement this turn equal to your speed (after modifiers) (PHB p.192). |
| **Disengage** | Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn (PHB p.192). |
| **Dodge** | Until your next turn, attackers you can see have disadvantage and you make Dexterity saves with advantage. Lost if incapacitated or your speed drops to 0 (PHB p.192). |
| **Help** | Give an ally **advantage** on their next ability check on a task you help with, **or** advantage on the first attack roll against a creature within 5 feet of you (before your next turn) (PHB p.192). |
| **Hide** | Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to hide, per the hiding rules (PHB p.192). |
| **Ready** | Choose a trigger and a prepared action (or a move); when the trigger fires, you may take it **using your reaction** (PHB p.193). |
| **Search** | Devote your attention to finding something — usually a Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) check (PHB p.193). |
| **Use an Object** | Interact with a second object this turn, or use an object whose description requires an action (PHB p.193). |

> **Ready uses your reaction.** When you Ready an action, it doesn't happen immediately — it fires later when your trigger occurs, and it consumes your one reaction for the round. A **readied spell** must have a casting time of 1 action; you cast it when you Ready it but hold the energy with **concentration**, releasing it with your reaction (PHB p.193).

## Common questions

**Q: Can I move, attack, then move again?**
A: Yes. You can break up your movement before and after your action, and even move between attacks if your action gives you more than one (PHB p.190).

**Q: Does casting a spell always cost my action?**
A: No — it depends on the spell's casting time. Most are 1 action, but some are a bonus action, a reaction, or take minutes/hours. Note the bonus-action-spell restriction in the spellcasting chapter (PHB p.192).

**Q: How many free object interactions do I get?**
A: One per turn (e.g., drawing a weapon or opening a door). A second interaction needs the **Use an Object** action (PHB p.190, 193).

**Q: What's the most common reaction?**
A: The **opportunity attack**, triggered when a creature leaves your reach. You only get one reaction per round, and Readying an action also spends it (PHB p.190, 193, 195).

**Q: What does a bonus action cost me?**
A: Nothing extra — but you only get one, and only if a feature/spell grants it. Anything that prevents you from taking actions also prevents your bonus action (PHB p.189).
