# Conditions (Quick Reference)

A scannable cheat sheet of every D&D 5e condition and its exact effects, plus the six-level Exhaustion track.

**Source:** PHB Appendix A, "Conditions" (printed pages 290–292).

---

A condition lasts until it is **countered** (e.g., prone is countered by standing up) or for a duration set by the effect that imposed it. If multiple effects impose the **same** condition, each instance has its own duration, but the effects **don't stack or get worse** — a creature either has a condition or it doesn't (PHB p.290).

---

## Blinded
- Can't see, and **automatically fails** any ability check that requires sight.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**; the creature's attack rolls have **disadvantage**.

## Charmed
- Can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects.
- The charmer has **advantage** on any ability check to interact socially with the creature.

## Deafened
- Can't hear, and **automatically fails** any ability check that requires hearing.

## Frightened
- Has **disadvantage** on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of its fear is **within line of sight**.
- Can't willingly move **closer** to the source of its fear.

## Grappled
- Speed becomes **0**, and it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed.
- The condition **ends** if the grappler is incapacitated.
- The condition also **ends** if an effect removes the grappled creature from the reach of the grappler or grappling effect (e.g., being hurled by *thunderwave*).

## Incapacitated
- Can't take **actions** or **reactions**.

## Invisible
- Impossible to see without the aid of magic or a special sense. For hiding, the creature is considered **heavily obscured**; its location can still be detected by noise it makes or tracks it leaves.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **disadvantage**; the creature's attack rolls have **advantage**.

## Paralyzed
- **Incapacitated** (can't take actions or reactions), and can't move or speak.
- **Automatically fails** Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**.
- Any attack that hits is a **critical hit** if the attacker is within **5 feet** of the creature.

## Petrified
- Transformed, along with any nonmagical object it is wearing or carrying, into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Its **weight increases by a factor of ten**, and it ceases aging.
- **Incapacitated**, can't move or speak, and is unaware of its surroundings.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**.
- **Automatically fails** Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
- Has **resistance to all damage**.
- **Immune to poison and disease** (a poison or disease already in its system is **suspended**, not neutralized).

## Poisoned
- Has **disadvantage** on attack rolls and ability checks.

## Prone
- Its only movement option is to **crawl**, unless it stands up (which ends the condition).
- Has **disadvantage** on attack rolls.
- An attack roll against it has **advantage** if the attacker is within **5 feet**; otherwise the attack roll has **disadvantage**.

## Restrained
- Speed becomes **0**, and it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**; the creature's attack rolls have **disadvantage**.
- Has **disadvantage** on Dexterity saving throws.

## Stunned
- **Incapacitated**, can't move, and can speak only falteringly.
- **Automatically fails** Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**.

## Unconscious
- **Incapacitated**, can't move or speak, and is unaware of its surroundings.
- **Drops whatever it's holding** and **falls prone**.
- **Automatically fails** Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
- Attack rolls **against** the creature have **advantage**.
- Any attack that hits is a **critical hit** if the attacker is within **5 feet** of the creature.

## Exhaustion (PHB p.291)

Exhaustion is a special condition measured in **six cumulative levels**. An effect can give one or more levels. A creature suffers the effect of its **current level and all lower levels** (e.g., at level 2 it has both speed halved *and* disadvantage on ability checks).

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Disadvantage on ability checks |
| 2 | Speed halved |
| 3 | Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws |
| 4 | Hit point maximum halved |
| 5 | Speed reduced to 0 |
| 6 | Death |

- If an already exhausted creature suffers another exhaustion effect, its level **increases** by the amount specified in that effect.
- An effect that **removes** exhaustion reduces its level; all exhaustion effects end if the level drops **below 1**.
- **Finishing a long rest** reduces exhaustion by **1 level**, provided the creature has also **ingested some food and drink**.

## Common questions

**Q: What's the difference between Restrained and Grappled?**
A: Both set speed to 0. Grappled is just speed 0 (ended by an incapacitated grappler or being removed from reach). Restrained adds: advantage to attackers, disadvantage on the creature's attacks, and disadvantage on its Dexterity saves (PHB p.290–291).

**Q: When does a hit become an automatic critical?**
A: When the target is **Paralyzed** or **Unconscious** and the attacker is within **5 feet** (PHB p.290–292).

**Q: How does Incapacitated interact with other conditions?**
A: Several conditions (Paralyzed, Petrified, Stunned, Unconscious) include the Incapacitated condition, meaning the creature can take no actions or reactions in addition to those conditions' other effects (PHB p.290–292).

**Q: How do I recover from Exhaustion?**
A: Each long rest removes 1 level, as long as you've also eaten and drunk. There's no faster default method short of a specific magical effect (PHB p.291).

**Q: Can the same condition stack from two sources?**
A: No. The effects don't get worse — a creature either has the condition or it doesn't — though each instance tracks its own duration (PHB p.290). Exhaustion is the exception: its levels are cumulative.

**Q: Can a Frightened creature still attack the source of its fear?**
A: Yes, but with disadvantage while the source is in line of sight. It just can't willingly move closer to it (PHB p.290).
