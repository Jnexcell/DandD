# Death and Dying (Quick Reference)

Everything that happens when a creature hits 0 hit points: instant death, falling unconscious, death saving throws, stabilizing, and knocking out.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 9, "Combat" — "Dropping to 0 Hit Points" (printed page 197).

---

## Dropping to 0 HP

When you drop to **0 hit points**, you either die outright or fall unconscious (PHB p.197).

### Instant death
Massive damage can kill you instantly. When damage reduces you to 0 HP **and there is damage left over**, you die if that **remaining damage equals or exceeds your hit point maximum** (PHB p.197).

> *Example:* a cleric with a max of 12 HP is currently at 6 HP. She takes 18 damage. That drops her to 0 with **12 damage remaining** — which equals her HP max — so she dies outright.

### Falling unconscious
If damage drops you to 0 HP but **doesn't** kill you outright, you fall **unconscious** (see the Unconscious condition). This unconsciousness ends as soon as you regain **any** hit points (PHB p.197).

---

## Death saving throws

Whenever you **start your turn** at 0 HP, you make a **death saving throw** (PHB p.197):

- Roll a **d20** — no ability modifier or proficiency applies.
- **10 or higher = success.** Below 10 = failure.
- A single success or failure has no effect by itself.
- **3 successes** → you become **stable**.
- **3 failures** → you **die**.
- Successes and failures **need not be consecutive** — track both until you collect three of a kind.
- Your count of successes and failures **resets to zero** when you regain any HP or become stable.

### Natural 1 and 20
- Roll a **natural 1** on the d20 → counts as **two failures**.
- Roll a **natural 20** → you **regain 1 hit point** (and so become conscious) (PHB p.197).

### Taking damage at 0 HP
- Any damage while at 0 HP → **one** death save failure.
- If that damage is from a **critical hit** → **two** failures.
- If the damage **equals or exceeds your HP maximum** → **instant death** (PHB p.197).

---

## Stabilizing a creature

The best way to save a dying creature is to **heal** it. If healing isn't available, you can **stabilize** it instead (PHB p.197):

- Use your **action** to administer first aid: a **DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine)** check.
- A **stable** creature is still at 0 HP and **unconscious**, but **stops making death saving throws**.
- A stable creature **starts making death saves again if it takes any damage**.
- A stable creature that isn't healed **regains 1 hit point after 1d4 hours** (PHB p.197).

---

## Knocking a creature out

When you reduce a creature to 0 HP with a **melee** attack, you can choose — the instant the damage is dealt — to **knock it out** instead of killing it. The creature falls **unconscious and is stable** (PHB p.198).

---

## Temporary hit points at 0 HP

Temporary HP are a buffer against damage, not real hit points. Gaining **temporary hit points** while at 0 HP **does not** restore consciousness or save a dying creature — only actual hit points or stabilizing do that. (Temp HP can't keep you up because they were never the issue; you need to climb back above 0 with real healing.) (PHB p.198)

---

## Monsters and death (DM note)

Most DMs have a monster simply **die** the instant it drops to 0 HP, rather than falling unconscious and rolling death saves. Mighty villains and important NPCs are common exceptions — the DM may run them with the same death-save rules as player characters (PHB p.198).

---

## Common questions

**Q: Do my death save successes and failures have to be in a row?**
A: No. Track both independently until you reach three of either. They reset to zero the moment you regain any HP or become stable (PHB p.197).

**Q: I'm at 0 HP and get hit by a melee attack while unconscious — what happens?**
A: You take a death save failure automatically. If the attacker is within 5 feet, the hit is a **critical** (Unconscious condition), so it's **two** failures. And if the damage total meets or beats your HP max, you die instantly (PHB p.197).

**Q: A natural 20 on a death save — what exactly do I get?**
A: You regain **1 hit point**, which ends your unconsciousness, so you're conscious with 1 HP (PHB p.197).

**Q: Will *false life* or *heroism* temp HP stabilize my dying ally?**
A: No. Temporary hit points don't restore consciousness or stop death saves at 0 HP. Use real healing or the Medicine check to stabilize (PHB p.197–198).

**Q: How long until a stabilized PC wakes up on their own?**
A: A stable creature that isn't healed regains **1 HP after 1d4 hours**, at which point it's conscious again with 1 HP (PHB p.197).
