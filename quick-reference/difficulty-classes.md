# Difficulty Classes & Ability Checks (Quick Reference)

How ability checks, DCs, advantage/disadvantage, and passive checks work, plus the 18 skills mapped to their governing abilities.

**Source:** PHB Chapter 7, "Using Ability Scores" (printed pages 174–179).

---

## How an ability check works

When the outcome of an action is uncertain, the DM calls for an ability check (PHB p.174):

1. Roll a **d20**.
2. Add the relevant **ability modifier**.
3. Add your **proficiency bonus** *if* you're proficient in the skill/tool being used.
4. Apply any other bonuses or penalties.

If the total **equals or exceeds the DC**, the check succeeds (PHB p.175). The DM picks the ability and sets the DC.

### Typical Difficulty Classes (PHB p.174)

| Task Difficulty | DC |
|---|---|
| Very easy | 5 |
| Easy | 10 |
| Medium | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Very hard | 25 |
| Nearly impossible | 30 |

## Advantage and disadvantage

When you have **advantage** or **disadvantage**, roll **2d20** and take the **higher** (advantage) or **lower** (disadvantage) (PHB p.173).

- They **don't stack.** No matter how many sources apply, you roll only **one** extra d20.
- If you have **both** advantage and disadvantage from any sources, they **cancel out** — you roll a single d20 with neither, even if one side outnumbers the other (PHB p.173).
- If something lets you **reroll** the d20 (e.g., a halfling's Lucky), you reroll only **one** of the two dice — your choice (PHB p.173).

## Passive checks

A **passive check** has no die roll — useful for repeated tasks or secret determinations (e.g., passive Perception vs. a hidden creature) (PHB p.175):

> **Passive score = 10 + all modifiers that normally apply** (ability mod, proficiency, etc.).
> **+5 if you have advantage**, **−5 if you have disadvantage** on the check.

*Example:* a 1st-level character with Wisdom 15 (+2) and Perception proficiency (+2) has a passive Wisdom (Perception) score of **14**.

## Skills by governing ability (PHB p.175)

No skills are tied to Constitution.

| Ability | Skills |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Athletics |
| **Dexterity** | Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth |
| **Intelligence** | Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion |
| **Wisdom** | Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival |
| **Charisma** | Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion |

> **Variant — skill with a different ability:** the DM may pair a skill with an unusual ability, e.g. a Constitution (Athletics) check for a long swim, or a Strength (Intimidation) check for a show of force. You still add your skill proficiency if you have it (PHB p.175).

## Working together & group checks

- **Working together:** when several characters team up on one task, the one leading (or with the best modifier) makes a single check **with advantage**, reflecting the others' help. A character can only help if the task is one they could attempt **alone** (e.g., you can't help pick a lock without thieves' tool proficiency), and only when extra hands would actually help (PHB p.175). In combat this is the **Help** action.
- **Group check:** when the whole party attempts something together, **everyone rolls**. If **at least half** the group succeeds, the **whole group succeeds**; otherwise it fails. The skilled members effectively cover for the unskilled ones (PHB p.175).

---

## Contests

When two creatures directly oppose each other (e.g., shoving, grappling, a tug-of-war over an object), both make an appropriate ability check and **compare totals** — higher wins. On a **tie**, the situation is unchanged (no one wins) (PHB p.174).

## Common questions

**Q: Do I add my proficiency bonus to every ability check?**
A: Only when you're proficient in the relevant skill or tool. Otherwise it's just d20 + ability modifier. Proficiency is never added to the same roll more than once (PHB p.173–175).

**Q: I have advantage from one thing and disadvantage from another — now what?**
A: They cancel completely. You roll a single, normal d20, regardless of how many sources are on each side (PHB p.173).

**Q: How do I find a creature's passive Perception?**
A: 10 + Wisdom modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient in Perception) + any other modifiers; +5/−5 for advantage/disadvantage on Perception (PHB p.175).

**Q: In a group check, how many need to succeed?**
A: At least **half** the group. If half or more pass, everyone passes (PHB p.175).

**Q: What DC should I set?**
A: Use the Typical DCs table as anchors — Medium (15) for a real-but-fair challenge, Easy (10) for something most trained characters manage, Hard (20) for genuinely tough tasks (PHB p.174).
