# DM Toolkit — Environments & Dressing

**Purpose:** describe a place vividly *on demand* — "they walk into the throne room / the cave / the tavern: what do they see, hear, smell?" Gives a fast description method, sensory prompt menus, dungeon-dressing tables, weather, and wilderness/settlement features so a room is never just "a 20-by-20 stone box."

**Grounded in / pulls from:**
- `dungeon-masters-guide/appendices.md` — DMG **Appendix A** random-dungeon **Dungeon Dressing** tables (DMG p.298–301) for atmosphere/contents.
- `dungeon-masters-guide/05-adventure-environments.md` — dungeon features, **light/vision**, and **wilderness weather/terrain** (DMG p.99–119).
- `players-handbook/08-adventuring.md` + `quick-reference/conditions.md` — vision & light rules (lightly/heavily obscured, darkvision → the *blinded* condition).

## Describe a place in 3 layers (the method)

Don't read a paragraph. Deliver it in beats and let players pull on threads:

1. **First impression (one sentence).** The single thing that hits them on entry — the *vibe* + the most striking feature. *"The air goes cold and wet; somewhere ahead, water drips into a pool you can't see."*
2. **The senses (2–3 quick hits).** Add a sound, a smell, and a light level. This is what makes a room feel *real* (see menus below).
3. **Detail on demand.** Hold the rest in reserve and reveal as they look closer / ask / roll Investigation. ("You said you check the desk?") — keeps description interactive, not a monologue.

> **Rule of thumb:** lead with **sound, smell, and light** — players always get *sight*, so the other senses are what sell a place. Name **one** interactable thing in every room (a lever, a body, a painting, a draft) so there's always a thread to pull.

## Sensory prompt menus (pick one or two)

**Light & sight** — bright daylight · dim torchlight throwing long shadows · the blue-grey of dusk · pitch dark (needs darkvision/light → *blinded* without) · flickering green witch-light · shafts of dusty sun · glittering crystal glow · the dull red of distant forge-fire.

**Sound** — dead silence that rings in the ears · steady drip of water · skittering in the walls · a low hum you feel in your teeth · wind moaning through gaps · distant chanting · the creak of settling timber · chains shifting · your own footsteps too loud.

**Smell** — wet stone & mildew · rot and old death · woodsmoke · incense and tallow · ozone/scorched air · brine and fish · animal musk & dung · blood, fresh and copper-sharp · perfume over something worse · fungal, earthy spores.

**Air & temperature** — clammy and cold · stifling, breath-thick heat · a sudden draft (a passage somewhere) · still and stale · damp fog clinging low · dry and dusty, catching the throat.

**Underfoot & texture** — slick flagstones · ankle-deep cold water · crunching bone/gravel · soft treacherous moss · sucking mud · grit and broken pottery · a too-clean, suspiciously swept floor (→ trap, see `traps-puzzles-hazards.md`).

## Dungeon dressing — what's in the room? (roll/pick)

Atmospheric clutter that implies a history. (The DMG's full dressing tables are Appendix A, p.298–301; these are quick improv menus.)

| d10 | A point of interest | d10 | A sign of past life |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Toppled statue / defaced idol | 1 | Old bloodstain or scorch mark |
| 2 | Dry fountain or stagnant pool | 2 | A skeleton, posed how it died |
| 3 | Rubble pile / cave-in | 3 | Scratched tally marks or a warning scrawled on the wall |
| 4 | Rows of crumbling shelves/urns | 4 | A dropped, rusted weapon |
| 5 | A pit, well, or chasm | 5 | Burned-out campfire, cold |
| 6 | Cracked altar with dried residue | 6 | A child's toy, out of place |
| 7 | Hanging chains / a cage | 7 | Claw marks gouged in stone |
| 8 | Murals or carvings (tell the lore!) | 8 | Footprints in the dust (how many? which way?) |
| 9 | Mushroom growth / luminous fungus | 9 | A half-eaten meal, recent |
| 10 | A draft from a hidden passage | 10 | Religious graffiti / cult sigils |

**Use dressing to do work:** foreshadow the threat (claw marks before the owlbear), seed a clue (the mural shows the puzzle order), or reward curiosity (the loose flagstone hides coin). Pure flavor is fine too — a few "nothing" details make the real ones land.

## Weather (outdoors) — quick table (DMG p.109 → `05-adventure-environments.md`)

Roll **d20** when it matters: **1–14** clear/normal · **15–17** light wind or light rain/snow · **18–19** heavy precipitation or strong wind · **20** storm/extreme. Effects to reach for:

- **Heavy rain / fog / falling snow:** area is **lightly obscured** → disadvantage on sight-based Perception; can become **heavily obscured** (effectively *blinded* into it).
- **Strong wind:** disadvantage on ranged weapon attacks and Perception (hearing); can disperse gas/fog and snuff small flames.
- **Extreme heat/cold:** Con saves vs. exhaustion on long exposure (DMG p.110) — a survival pressure, not just set dressing.

> Weather is a cheap, reusable **complication** for travel scenes and skill challenges (`scenes-goals-and-avenues.md`).

## Wilderness & settlement features (improv seeds)

**Wilderness point-of-interest (d8):** a standing stone / old shrine · a ruined tower · a crossing (ford, rope bridge, ferry) · a strange grove or blighted patch · a hermit's hut / charcoal-burner's camp · animal den or nest · a battlefield gone to grass · a cave mouth.

**Settlement at a glance (pick a few):**
- **Sound & smell:** market cries, smithy clang, livestock, woodsmoke, tannery stink, sea air, temple bells.
- **A telling detail:** boarded windows (fear?), a gallows in the square, fresh-built palisade, a shrine to an odd god, beggars or none (why?), guards too many or too few.
- **One NPC to meet** → hand off to `npc-generator.md`. **One rumor/hook** → `scenes-goals-and-avenues.md`.
- **Three places a party always looks for:** somewhere to **sleep** (inn), **drink/hear talk** (tavern), and **buy/sell** (market/smith/temple). Name those three and the town feels real.

## Light & vision — the rule under the flavor (`08-adventuring.md`, `conditions.md`)

- **Lightly obscured** (dim light, patchy fog, light foliage): **disadvantage** on Wis (Perception) checks relying on sight.
- **Heavily obscured** (darkness, thick fog, dense foliage): you're effectively **blinded** when trying to see into it — attacks out have disadvantage, attacks in have advantage.
- **Darkvision** turns darkness into *dim* light (so: lightly obscured, disadvantage on Perception) out to its range — it is **not** color vision and doesn't beat *magical* darkness.
- A **bright** light source (torch ~20 ft bright + 20 dim) also makes the bearer easy to spot in the dark — a tactical choice, not just description.

## 20-second checklist (use this live)

1. **First impression** — one sentence, the vibe + the standout feature.
2. **Two senses** — pick a **sound** and a **smell**; state the **light level**.
3. **One interactable** — name a thread they can pull.
4. **(If relevant)** weather/terrain complication; vision rule if it's dark.
5. **Hold the rest** — reveal on a look, a question, or an Investigation check.
