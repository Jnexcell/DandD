# DM Toolkit — Social Interaction & Dialog

**Purpose:** run a conversation with mechanical teeth *and* give every NPC a distinct voice on demand — "the party tries to talk the captain into letting them through; give me his attitude, what he says, and what it takes to move him." Pairs the DMG social rules with **dialog/voice generation hooks** so in-character NPC lines come out fast and consistent.

**Grounded in / pulls from:**
- `dungeon-masters-guide/08-running-the-game.md` — the full **social interaction** procedure: starting attitude, conversation, the **Conversation Reaction** table, aiding/repeating (DMG p.244–246). *Rules source.*
- `npc-generator.md` — make the person first (face, **voice/mannerism**, want, secret), then talk.
- `players-handbook/04-personality-and-background.md` + `players-handbook/backgrounds/*` — ideal/bond/flaw to push on.

## Run a social scene in 4 steps (DMG p.244–246)

**1. Starting attitude** — pick one (default strangers to **Indifferent**):
- **Friendly** — wants you to succeed; helps freely on anything low-cost. A check is needed only to get it to take a *personal risk*.
- **Indifferent** — helps or hinders by self-interest; a **successful Charisma check is needed** to get cooperation.
- **Hostile** — opposes you (but won't necessarily attack — think a scheming noble); needs **challenging** checks, and a truly ill-disposed NPC may be unpersuadable (auto-fail).

**2. Conversation** — play it out; **attitude can shift at most one step** per interaction. Hitting an NPC's **ideal, bond, or flaw** warms it (hostile→indifferent→friendly); an insult or harmful act cools it. To target those deliberately, the party must *learn* them — a **Wis (Insight)** check reveals one (fail by 10+ and you can feed them a *false* read).

**3. The check** — one **Charisma (Persuasion / Deception / Intimidation)** roll from an active participant; current attitude sets what a result buys:

| Check total | Friendly | Indifferent | Hostile |
|---:|---|---|---|
| **0** | does as asked, no risk | no help, no harm | opposes you |
| **10** | accepts a *minor* risk/sacrifice | does it if no risk | no help, no harm |
| **20** | accepts a *significant* risk/sacrifice | accepts a *minor* risk | does it if no risk |

*A helpful second character grants **advantage**; a clumsy one imposes **disadvantage**.*

**4. Repeat?** Pushing again usually fails and risks souring the NPC toward hostility. Let a *different* PC defuse a botched exchange with fresh roleplay + a new check.

> **Which skill?** **Persuasion** = honest appeals, logic, goodwill. **Deception** = lies, bluffs, false identity. **Intimidation** = threats, force of presence (success can *lower* attitude even as it gets compliance — a coerced NPC remembers). Reward players for matching the skill to the *fiction they describe*.

## Give an NPC a voice in 5 seconds

Stack 2–3 dials and commit. Consistency matters more than range — the *same* tic each time is what makes an NPC recognizable.

| Dial | Options |
|---|---|
| **Pitch / volume** | booming · soft · reedy · gravelly · breathy |
| **Pace** | clipped & fast · slow & deliberate · rambling · halting/stammering |
| **Vocabulary** | flowery & formal · blunt & plain · slangy/streetwise · archaic/biblical · jargon-heavy (trade, military, clergy) |
| **Verbal tic** | repeats your words back · third-person self-reference · pet phrase ("mark my words") · trails off… · ends on a question, hm? · odd oaths ("by the Nine") |
| **Attitude leak** | eager to please · barely tolerating you · fishing for advantage · frightened, covering it · amused at your expense |

> Cross-reference the **mannerism** you rolled in `npc-generator.md` — it's the same dial. One physical gesture + one vocal tic = a memorable NPC.

## Dialog generation hooks (produce in-character lines on demand)

When Josh asks for an NPC's actual lines, generate from this spine so the voice stays true:

1. **What do they want in this exact moment?** (the `npc-generator.md` "want" — every line should push toward it.)
2. **What's their attitude to the party right now?** (friendly/indifferent/hostile colors *everything* — warmth, hedging, or veiled threat.)
3. **How do they talk?** (apply the voice dials above — pace, vocabulary, tic.)
4. **What are they hiding?** (their secret leaks as a tell — a too-quick denial, a subject change, a glance at the door — never a flat info-dump.)
5. **Give them a relationship to the topic** (bored bureaucrat vs. true believer vs. terrified witness all answer the same question differently.)

**Line-generation patterns to pull from:**
- **Deflect:** answer a question with a question, or a complaint. ("Papers? In *this* weather you ask me for papers?")
- **Bargain:** name a price, favor, or condition. ("Maybe I saw something. Maybe my memory wants oiling.")
- **Reveal-with-a-catch:** give real info wrapped in self-interest or a lie. ("The cult meets below the tannery — but you didn't hear it from me, and you'll forget my face.")
- **Color, not content:** a line that's pure character and moves nothing — used sparingly to breathe. ("Gods, my feet. Thirty years on this gate and not one decent pair of boots.")

**Keep NPC lines short** — 1–2 sentences, then kick it back to the players. Dialog is a volley, not a speech (save the monologue for the villain's big beat).

## Quick voice archetypes (grab-and-go)

- **The gruff veteran:** clipped, plain, impatient; respects competence, hates wasted words.
- **The oily fixer:** smooth, flattering, always angling for the deal; never a straight answer.
- **The nervous witness:** stammering, over-explaining, eyes darting; spills if reassured, clams up if pushed.
- **The haughty noble:** formal, condescending, third-person flourishes; insulted easily (→ attitude drops a step).
- **The true believer:** intense, scripture-quoting, certain; unmovable by logic, reachable through their *ideal*.
- **The weary official:** bored, procedural, rule-bound; a small bribe or the *right form* works better than charm.
- **The street kid:** fast slang, cocky, transactional; loyal once you treat them as a person (lift from `urchin.md`).

## 20-second checklist (run a talk live)

1. **Attitude** — Friendly / Indifferent / Hostile (default Indifferent).
2. **Voice** — two dials + one tic; stay consistent.
3. **Let them push on ideal/bond/flaw** (Insight to learn it) → attitude can move one step.
4. **One Charisma check** when it matters → read the Reaction table for what it buys.
5. **Generate lines** from want + attitude + secret; keep them short; volley back to the players.
