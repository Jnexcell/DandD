# Encounter Building (Quick Reference)

The XP-budget method for gauging and building combat encounters: per-character thresholds, the encounter multiplier, and the adventuring-day budget.

**Source:** DMG Chapter 3, "Creating Adventures" — "Combat Encounter Difficulty" and "The Adventuring Day" (printed pages 82–85).

---

## The procedure (DMG p.82)

1. **Determine each character's XP thresholds.** Look up every PC's level on the table below; each level has four thresholds (Easy / Medium / Hard / Deadly).
2. **Total the party's thresholds.** Add up all the PCs' values in each column. You get four party budgets — Easy, Medium, Hard, Deadly. (Record these; they're reusable for the whole adventure.)
3. **Total the monsters' XP.** Add the XP value from each monster's stat block.
4. **Apply the encounter multiplier** based on the **number of monsters** (see table). This **adjusted XP** is only a difficulty gauge — it is *not* the XP awarded to players.
5. **Compare.** The **highest party threshold that the adjusted XP still meets or exceeds** is the encounter's difficulty.

## Encounter Multipliers (DMG p.82)

The more monsters, the more dangerous a given XP total is. Multiply the monsters' **total** base XP by:

| Number of Monsters | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| 1 | ×1 |
| 2 | ×1.5 |
| 3–6 | ×2 |
| 7–10 | ×2.5 |
| 11–14 | ×3 |
| 15+ | ×4 |

> **Party-size adjustment (DMG p.83).** The table assumes **3–5 PCs**.
> - **Fewer than 3 PCs:** use the **next higher** multiplier row (e.g., 1 monster → ×1.5; 15+ → ×5).
> - **6 or more PCs:** use the **next lower** multiplier row (e.g., 1 monster → ×0.5).

When making this calculation, ignore any monster whose CR is *significantly* below the average CR of the group, unless you judge it meaningfully adds to the difficulty (DMG p.82).

## Worked example: four 3rd-level PCs vs. goblins

**Party budget** — each level-3 PC: Easy 75 / Medium 150 / Hard 225 / Deadly 400. Four PCs:

| | Easy | Medium | Hard | Deadly |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Party of 4 (L3) | 300 | 600 | 900 | 1,600 |

**The monsters** — a goblin is worth **50 XP** each. Suppose **6 goblins**: base total = 6 × 50 = **300 XP**. Six monsters falls in the **3–6** band, so multiply by **×2** → **adjusted 600 XP**.

Compare 600 to the party budget: it meets the **Medium** threshold (600) but not Hard (900). So six goblins is a **Medium** encounter for this party. Note how the multiplier matters — the raw 300 XP alone would have read as merely Easy.

(Push it to **8 goblins**: base 400 XP, in the 7–10 band → ×2.5 → **1,000 adjusted**, which beats Hard (900) → a **Hard** encounter.)

## The adventuring day (DMG p.84)

A party can typically handle about **6–8 medium-or-hard encounters per day** before needing a long rest. To budget a whole day, add each character's **Adventuring Day XP** value:

| Level | Adjusted XP / Character / Day | Level | Adjusted XP / Character / Day |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 300 | 11 | 10,500 |
| 2 | 600 | 12 | 11,500 |
| 3 | 1,200 | 13 | 13,500 |
| 4 | 1,700 | 14 | 15,000 |
| 5 | 3,500 | 15 | 18,000 |
| 6 | 4,000 | 16 | 20,000 |
| 7 | 5,000 | 17 | 25,000 |
| 8 | 6,000 | 18 | 27,000 |
| 9 | 7,500 | 19 | 30,000 |

This is a rough estimate of the total **adjusted** monster XP the party can chew through in a day. Plan for about **two short rests**, roughly one-third and two-thirds of the way through (DMG p.84).

## Common questions

**Q: Is the adjusted XP what I give the players?**
A: No. The multiplier exists only to gauge difficulty. Players are awarded the monsters' **base** XP total (DMG p.82).

**Q: Why does the same XP get harder with more monsters?**
A: More monsters means more attack rolls per round against the party, so the action economy tilts against the PCs. The multiplier accounts for that (DMG p.82).

**Q: My party has 6 PCs — does anything change?**
A: Yes. Sum all six PCs' thresholds for a bigger budget, **and** drop to the next-lower multiplier row (DMG p.82–83).

**Q: Where do I find a monster's XP?**
A: In its stat block; XP is tied to Challenge Rating. The Monster Manual lists monsters by CR in its appendix (DMG p.82, 84).

**Q: How do I build to a target difficulty instead of grading one?**
A: Use the party threshold as a budget: pick monsters whose **adjusted** XP lands between the target difficulty's threshold and the next-higher one (e.g., Medium = at least the Medium total, below the Hard total) (DMG p.83–84).
